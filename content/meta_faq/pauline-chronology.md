Reconstructing Paul's chronology requires piecing together **Acts**, **Paul's own letters** (especially Galatians 1–2 and 2 Corinthians 11), and **external ancient evidence** — especially the Gallio inscription (Acts 18:12) which is datable, and general Roman imperial chronology. Scholars disagree on details, but a broad consensus has emerged.

**The fixed point** is Paul's encounter with Gallio, the proconsul of Achaia, during his eighteen-month stay in Corinth (Acts 18). The **Delphi Inscription** establishes Gallio's proconsulship as mid-51 to mid-52 CE. This anchors Paul's second missionary journey and, working backward and forward, lets us date everything else.

**A representative timeline (dates approximate):**

- **c. AD 5** — Paul born in Tarsus, a Roman citizen of the tribe of Benjamin.
- **c. 30–33** — Jesus crucified and raised (the date depending on your chronology of Jesus).
- **c. 33–35** — Paul's Damascus-road encounter (Acts 9; Gal 1:15–16). Short time in Damascus, then Arabia, then back to Damascus.
- **c. 36–37** — First visit to Jerusalem, brief (Gal 1:18–19). Extended stay in Syria-Cilicia, based in Tarsus.
- **c. 46–47** — Second Jerusalem visit, the famine-relief trip (Acts 11:27–30; Gal 2:1?).
- **c. 47–48** — First missionary journey with Barnabas to Cyprus and South Galatia (Acts 13–14).
- **c. 48–49** — **Galatians** composed, arguably the earliest Pauline letter on the South-Galatia theory.
- **c. 49** — Jerusalem Council (Acts 15; Gal 2:1–10 on one reading).
- **c. 50–52** — Second missionary journey: Asia Minor, Macedonia, Corinth. **1 Thessalonians** written from Corinth c. 50–51; perhaps **2 Thessalonians** soon after. Gallio encounter in Corinth (51–52).
- **c. 53–57** — Third missionary journey. Extended stay in Ephesus (c. 53–56). **1 Corinthians** from Ephesus; **2 Corinthians** from Macedonia; **Romans** from Corinth in winter 57–58.
- **c. 57** — Paul arrested in Jerusalem (Acts 21).
- **c. 57–59** — Imprisonment in Caesarea under Felix and then Festus.
- **c. 59–60** — Voyage to Rome; shipwreck on Malta.
- **c. 60–62** — House arrest in Rome (Acts 28). The **"prison epistles"** — Philippians, Colossians, Philemon, Ephesians — written during this period on most reconstructions.
- **c. 62–63** — Possibly released; some traditions place a western mission (to Spain? per 1 Clement 5; Romans 15:24–28).
- **c. 63–66** — **Pastoral Epistles** (1–2 Timothy, Titus) on the tradition that Paul wrote them after release.
- **c. 64–67** — Neronian persecution. Paul martyred in Rome, traditionally beheaded.

**Disputed points.** Was Galatians the earliest letter (South-Galatia theory) or mid-career (North-Galatia)? Did Paul make one or two visits to Jerusalem before the council? Was he released from Rome for a fourth journey or executed at the end of his first imprisonment? These remain scholarly debates with serious evangelical voices on multiple sides.

A reliable timeline is essential context for reading Paul's letters. Without it, the urgency and occasion of each epistle blur into a generalized "Pauline theology."
