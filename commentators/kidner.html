Derek Kidner

Old Testament Scholar & Master of Concision

Biography

Derek Kidner (1913–2008) was a British Old Testament scholar and Warden of Tyndale House, Cambridge, one of the world's leading centres for biblical research. Ordained in the Church of England, he combined pastoral sensitivity with rigorous scholarship, producing commentaries that are models of economy and insight.

Key Works

His Tyndale Old Testament Commentaries on Genesis (1967), Proverbs (1964), Psalms (1973–75), and Ezra & Nehemiah (1979) remain widely used decades after publication. Each is characterised by remarkable compression: Kidner could illuminate an entire passage in a single sentence where others required paragraphs. His Ezra-Nehemiah commentary is particularly valued for its theological clarity on the tension between exclusivism and universalism in the post-exilic community.

Approach

Kidner wrote with a literary ear and a pastor's heart. He treats biblical texts as coherent theological literature, not source-critical puzzles. His gift was the pithy observation — the one sentence that reframes a passage entirely. On Ezra's prayer of confession he writes: "Not a word of excuse; not a word of self-pity. This is repentance that has shed every trace of self-regard." His commentaries reward re-reading; new layers emerge with each encounter.
