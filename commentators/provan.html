Biography

Iain William Provan is Marshall Sheppard Professor of Biblical Studies at Regent College, Vancouver. A Scottish-born Old Testament scholar, he previously held chairs at the University of Edinburgh. Provan is known for combining rigorous historical-critical scholarship with a deep commitment to reading Scripture as canonical literature that addresses the contemporary church.

Interpretive Approach

Provan reads 1–2 Kings as a theological narrative rather than merely a historical chronicle. His NIBCOT commentary focuses on the text’s literary artistry — narrative pacing, characterization, irony, and structural design — while always asking what the Deuteronomistic Historian intended the reader to learn theologically. He is particularly strong on the theological evaluation of kings and the prophetic word as the engine that drives the narrative.

Theological Tradition

Provan writes from a Reformed evangelical perspective with particular interest in the philosophy of history. He has argued vigorously (in A Biblical History of Israel, co-authored with Long and Longman) that the biblical narratives should be treated as credible historical testimony rather than dismissed as late theological construction.

Key Works

1 and 2 Kings (New International Biblical Commentary OT, 1995) — Literary-theological commentary with attention to narrative structure and Deuteronomistic themes. Also: A Biblical History of Israel (2003, with Long and Longman).
