A.A. Anderson

1927–2009 · OT Scholar · University of Manchester · WBC 2 Samuel

“The Succession Narrative is one of the finest examples of ancient historiography — psychologically realistic, theologically profound, and literarily sophisticated.”

Biography

A.A. (Arnold Albert) Anderson was a British OT scholar at the University of Manchester known for careful, philologically grounded commentary on the Hebrew text.

Interpretive Approach

Anderson reads 2 Samuel with close attention to the Hebrew text, text-critical questions, and the literary structure of the Succession Narrative. His WBC commentary provides detailed verse-by-verse analysis with extensive engagement with ancient versions and modern scholarship.

Theological Tradition

Anderson writes from a centrist scholarly perspective, engaging critically with both conservative and liberal scholarship. His approach is measured and judicious.

Key Works

2 Samuel (Word Biblical Commentary, 1989) — Detailed philological commentary with extensive text-critical apparatus.
