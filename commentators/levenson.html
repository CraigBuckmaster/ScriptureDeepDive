Jon D. Levenson

Harvard Jewish Studies & Esther Authority

Biography

Jon Levenson is Albert A. List Professor of Jewish Studies at Harvard Divinity School, one of the most distinguished positions in Jewish biblical scholarship. A fellow of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences, he has written extensively on the theology of the Hebrew Bible, creation, death and resurrection in ancient Israel, and Jewish-Christian relations.

Key Works

His Old Testament Library commentary on Esther (1997) is the standard academic reference. He is also author of The Death and Resurrection of the Beloved Son (1993), Creation and the Persistence of Evil (1988), and Sinai and Zion (1985) — each a landmark in biblical theology. His work consistently illuminates the theological depth of texts that others treat as merely historical.

Approach

Levenson reads Esther through the lens of the Exodus: Haman is the new Pharaoh who seeks genocide; Esther is the new Moses who intercedes; the Jews' victory echoes the Sea crossing. He is attentive to Purim traditions and to the book's role in Jewish liturgical life. His commentary combines critical rigor with genuine appreciation for the text's theological claims — a rare combination in academic scholarship.
