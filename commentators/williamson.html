H.G.M. Williamson

Regius Professor of Hebrew, Oxford & Ezra-Nehemiah Authority

Biography

Hugh G.M. Williamson (1947–) is Regius Professor of Hebrew at the University of Oxford, one of the most prestigious Old Testament chairs in the world. A fellow of the British Academy, he has spent his career on the historical books of the Hebrew Bible, with particular expertise in Chronicles, Ezra-Nehemiah, and Isaiah.

Key Works

His Word Biblical Commentary on Ezra, Nehemiah (1985) is the standard academic reference for these books, combining rigorous textual analysis with theological sensitivity. He is also author of 1 and 2 Chronicles in the New Century Bible Commentary (1982) and Israel in the Books of Chronicles (1977). His work on the Aramaic sections of Ezra is particularly authoritative, drawing on Persian administrative documents to illuminate the biblical text.

Approach

Williamson combines critical method with theological seriousness. He is attentive to the Persian-period historical context that most commentators skim past — the administrative structures, the Aramaic formulae, the imperial policies that shaped Jewish restoration. His textual work is meticulous: he distinguishes editorial layers without losing sight of the final form's theological message. He reads Ezra-Nehemiah as purposeful literature, not merely as a historical source to be mined for facts.
