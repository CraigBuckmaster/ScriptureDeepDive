Sara Japhet

Professor of Bible, Hebrew University of Jerusalem

Biography

Sara Japhet (1934–2022) was Professor of Bible at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem and one of the most distinguished biblical scholars of the 20th century. Born in Czechoslovakia and educated in Israel, she became the world's foremost authority on the books of Chronicles, devoting her career to demonstrating the Chronicler's literary artistry and theological independence.

Key Works

Her magisterial commentary on I & II Chronicles in the Old Testament Library (OTL) series (1993) is considered the definitive critical treatment. Her earlier monograph The Ideology of the Book of Chronicles and Its Place in Biblical Thought (1977/1989) established the framework that all subsequent Chronicles scholarship has engaged. She argued convincingly that the Chronicler was not merely copying Samuel-Kings but creating an independent theological work with its own sources and purposes.

Approach

Japhet is meticulous on textual detail, source analysis, and the Chronicler's editorial technique. She demonstrates how the Chronicler reshaped inherited traditions to address the concerns of the post-exilic community: identity, legitimate worship, and the continuity of God's promises. Her commentary is essential for understanding the differences between Chronicles and Samuel-Kings — not as errors but as deliberate theological choices.
