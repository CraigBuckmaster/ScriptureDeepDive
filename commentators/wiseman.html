Biography

Donald John Wiseman (1918–2010) was Professor of Assyriology at the University of London and one of the foremost Assyriologists of the twentieth century. A committed evangelical Christian, he uniquely combined deep expertise in cuneiform texts and ancient Near Eastern archaeology with careful biblical scholarship. He served as chairman of the British School of Archaeology in Iraq and edited the journal Iraq for decades.

Interpretive Approach

Wiseman reads 1–2 Kings with unmatched command of the Assyrian, Babylonian, and Egyptian source material that illuminates the biblical narrative. His TOTC commentary integrates archaeological discoveries — seal impressions, annals, king lists, treaty forms — to show how the biblical text fits within the broader ancient Near Eastern world. He treats the text as historically reliable while acknowledging interpretive difficulties.

Theological Tradition

Wiseman writes from a conservative evangelical perspective, affirming the historical reliability of the biblical narrative while engaging seriously with ancient Near Eastern evidence. His approach is characterized by meticulous philological care and a conviction that archaeology consistently supports rather than undermines the biblical record.

Key Works
