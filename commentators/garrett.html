Duane A. Garrett

NAC Song of Songs & Proverbs

Biography

Duane Garrett is John R. Sampey Professor of Old Testament Interpretation at The Southern Baptist Theological Seminary in Louisville, Kentucky. He has authored commentaries on Proverbs, Ecclesiastes, and Song of Songs, as well as studies in Hosea and Joel.

Key Works

His NAC Song of Songs (2004) reads the poem as a unified dramatic sequence celebrating marital love. He also contributed the NAC Proverbs volume and has written on OT introduction and Hebrew exegesis.

Approach

Garrett reads Song of Songs as a single poem with a dramatic arc, not a loose anthology. He identifies the speakers (Beloved, Lover, Daughters of Jerusalem) and traces their relationship through courtship, wedding, and consummation. His approach is literary-evangelical: the text is both great love poetry and a theological statement about the goodness of marital intimacy as God designed it.
