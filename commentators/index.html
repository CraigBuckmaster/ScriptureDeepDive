Scholars & Commentators

Four voices appear throughout Scripture Walkthrough — each bringing a distinct tradition, method, and set of questions to the same text.

John MacArthur Conservative Evangelical · Contemporary Verse-by-verse exposition with pastoral application, grounded in grammatical-historical interpretation. The most widely used evangelical study Bible commentator of the late 20th century. Nahum Sarna Jewish Biblical Scholar · 20th Century JPS Torah Commentary series editor. Brings rabbinic tradition, ancient Near Eastern parallels, and deep Hebrew philology to bear on Genesis and Exodus. Robert Alter Literary Criticism · Contemporary UC Berkeley professor whose landmark translation and commentary illuminates the narrative art, poetic structure, and rhetorical genius of the Hebrew Bible. Robert L. Hubbard Jr. Evangelical OT Scholar · NICOT Commentary Author of the definitive NICOT commentary on Ruth. Combines rigorous Hebrew philology with deep engagement with ancient Near Eastern legal customs and the book’s hesed theology. Bruce K. Waltke Evangelical OT Scholar · Reformed Co-author of the standard Biblical Hebrew grammar and author of the two-volume NICOT commentary on Proverbs. Unmatched in Hebrew philology and canonical-theological reading of the wisdom literature. John Calvin Reformed Theology · 16th Century Reformer and theologian whose Commentaries remain among the most rigorous exegetical works produced — precise, pastoral, and deeply doctrinal. A.T. Robertson NT Greek Scholarship · Early 20th Century Southern Baptist Seminary professor and the greatest NT Greek grammarian in American scholarship. His Word Pictures illuminate what the Greek text is actually doing, verse by verse. Catena Aurea Patristic Compilation · Aquinas, 13th Century Thomas Aquinas’s “Golden Chain” — a carefully curated anthology of Chrysostom, Augustine, Ambrose, and eighty other Fathers commenting verse by verse on all four Gospels. NET Bible Notes Textual-Critical Scholarship · Contemporary Over 60,000 translator notes documenting every significant textual and translational decision — making visible the interpretive work embedded in every English rendering. Joel Marcus NT Historical-Critical Scholarship · Contemporary Author of the two-volume Anchor Bible Commentary on Mark — the most comprehensive critical commentary on the Gospel in English. Brings Dead Sea Scrolls parallels, Roman imperial context, and rigorous Greek philology to every pericope. Rhoads & Michie Narrative Criticism · Contemporary Authors of Mark as Story — the founding text of Markan narrative criticism. Their analysis of narrator, characters, plot, and rhetoric transformed how scholars read Mark as a unified literary work. Jacob Milgrom Jewish Biblical Scholar · Anchor Bible Author of the definitive three-volume Anchor Bible commentary on Leviticus and the Anchor Bible Numbers. Transformed understanding of the priestly purity system as a coherent moral theology, not arid ritualism. Timothy R. Ashley Evangelical OT Scholar · NICOT Numbers Author of the NICOT commentary on Numbers — the standard evangelical-scholarly resource on the book. Combines close Hebrew philology with theological sensitivity and consistent attention to narrative structure. Craig S. Keener Evangelical NT Scholar · Socio-Historical Author of the four-volume Acts commentary — the most comprehensive evangelical treatment of the book ever produced. Brings encyclopedic knowledge of Greco-Roman culture, Jewish diaspora practice, and ancient historiography. Peter C. Craigie Evangelical OT Scholar · NICOT Deuteronomy Author of the NICOT commentary on Deuteronomy. Brings deep knowledge of Hittite treaty structure, Ugaritic literature, and the ethics of holy war to bear on the most theologically demanding book of the Pentateuch. Jeffrey H. Tigay Jewish Biblical Scholar · JPS Torah Commentary Author of the JPS Torah Commentary on Deuteronomy. Combines philological rigour, rabbinic tradition, and ANE comparative study with characteristic Jewish scholarly clarity. The definitive modern Jewish scholarly commentary on Deuteronomy in English.
