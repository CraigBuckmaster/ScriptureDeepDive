# Narrative continuity:
#   1 Chronicles retells Israel's history from Adam to David's death — but
#   from a post-exilic priestly perspective. Originally one book with
#   2 Chronicles (Hebrew: Dibrê Hayyamîm, "Events of the Days"). Where
#   Samuel-Kings is the Deuteronomistic History (written during exile,
#   asking "why did we lose everything?"), Chronicles is the Priestly
#   History (written after the return, asking "who are we now?").
#
# The theological angle:
#   Chronicles doesn't replace Samuel-Kings; it reinterprets the same events
#   for a different audience. The Chronicler emphasises: legitimate worship,
#   Levitical service, the temple, David as worship-organiser (not warrior-king),
#   and the unity of "all Israel." He omits David's sins (Bathsheba, Absalom)
#   and the northern kingdom almost entirely. This is not whitewashing but
#   theological selectivity: for a post-exilic community rebuilding the temple,
#   the relevant question is not "what went wrong?" but "how do we worship rightly?"

# ── THE GENEALOGIES: ADAM TO ISRAEL (Ch 1-9) ─────────────────────────────
#
# NOTE: The genealogies are NOT filler. They are the Chronicler's way of saying:
# "We are still God's people. Our identity stretches from Adam to the present."
# For a post-exilic community that has lost land, temple, and monarchy, the
# genealogies answer the existential question: "Do we still exist as Israel?"

# Chapter 1: "From Adam to Abraham to Edom"
#   Section 1: vv 1-27  — Adam to Abraham (compressed world history)
#   Section 2: vv 28-54 — Abraham's descendants; Esau/Edom genealogy
#   Key: The Chronicler begins at Adam, not Abraham — universal scope before particular election

# Chapter 2: "The Sons of Israel; Judah's Line to David"
#   Section 1: vv 1-17  — The twelve sons; Judah's line through Perez
#   Section 2: vv 18-55 — Hezron's clans; Caleb's territory; Jerahmeel
#   Key: Judah gets massive treatment (55 verses) — the royal tribe

# Chapter 3: "David's Royal Line Through the Exile"
#   Section 1: vv 1-9   — David's sons born at Hebron and Jerusalem
#   Section 2: vv 10-24 — Solomon to the exile and beyond — the Davidic line survives
#   Key: The genealogy extends PAST the exile — proof the dynasty endures

# Chapter 4: "Judah and Simeon — Jabez's Prayer"
#   Section 1: vv 1-23  — More Judahite clans; Jabez's prayer (vv 9-10)
#   Section 2: vv 24-43 — Simeon's descendants and territorial expansion
#   Key: Jabez — a brief prayer for blessing embedded in a genealogical list

# Chapter 5: "Reuben, Gad, and the Half-Tribe of Manasseh"
#   Section 1: vv 1-10  — Reuben (lost firstborn rights to Joseph)
#   Section 2: vv 11-26 — Gad and Manasseh; their exile by Assyria
#   Key: v.25-26 — these tribes were exiled because they were unfaithful

# Chapter 6: "The Levites — Priests, Musicians, and Cities"
#   Section 1: vv 1-30  — Levitical genealogy; Aaron's priestly line
#   Section 2: vv 31-48 — Temple musicians appointed by David
#   Section 3: vv 49-81 — Aaron's priestly duties; Levitical cities
#   Key: Longest chapter (81 verses) — Levites are the Chronicler's main interest

# Chapter 7: "Issachar, Benjamin, Naphtali, Manasseh, Ephraim, Asher"
#   Section 1: vv 1-19  — Issachar, Benjamin, Naphtali, Manasseh
#   Section 2: vv 20-40 — Ephraim (includes mourning episode vv 21-23); Asher
#   Key: Several northern tribes get brief treatment; the Chronicler's focus is Judah/Levi

# Chapter 8: "Benjamin's Genealogy — Saul's Line"
#   Section 1: vv 1-28  — Benjamin's clans and settlements
#   Section 2: vv 29-40 — Gibeon genealogy leading to Saul's family
#   Key: Sets up the Saul narrative in chapter 10

# Chapter 9: "The Post-Exilic Community — Who Returned?"
#   Section 1: vv 1-16  — Families who resettled Jerusalem
#   Section 2: vv 17-34 — Gatekeepers, Levitical duties, temple service
#   Section 3: vv 35-44 — Saul's genealogy repeated (bridge to ch 10)
#   Key: The genealogies end with the PRESENT — the returned community. Identity confirmed.

# ── SAUL'S DEATH AND DAVID'S RISE (Ch 10-12) ────────────────────────────

# Chapter 10: "The Death of Saul — and Why"
#   Section 1: vv 1-7   — The battle of Gilboa; Saul and his sons die
#   Section 2: vv 8-14  — Philistines desecrate the bodies; the Chronicler's verdict
#   Key: The Chronicler explains Saul's death theologically: "unfaithfulness" + consulting a medium

# Chapter 11: "David King Over All Israel; Mighty Warriors"
#   Section 1: vv 1-9   — All Israel anoints David at Hebron; capture of Jerusalem
#   Section 2: vv 10-47 — The catalogue of David's mighty warriors
#   Key: "All Israel" — the Chronicler omits the 7-year civil war; David rules united Israel immediately

# Chapter 12: "Warriors Who Came to David"
#   Section 1: vv 1-22  — Supporters at Ziklag and in the wilderness
#   Section 2: vv 23-40 — The army that made David king; the celebration feast
#   Key: All twelve tribes represented — the Chronicler's "all Israel" theme

# ── THE ARK COMES TO JERUSALEM (Ch 13-16) ────────────────────────────────

# Chapter 13: "First Attempt to Move the Ark — Uzzah Dies"
#   Section 1: vv 1-8   — David consults all Israel; the procession begins
#   Section 2: vv 9-14  — Uzzah touches the ark and dies; the ark diverted to Obed-Edom
#   Key: Wrong method (cart instead of Levites); holiness cannot be handled casually

# Chapter 14: "David Established — Philistines Defeated"
#   Section 1: vv 1-7   — Hiram's recognition; David's family in Jerusalem
#   Section 2: vv 8-17  — Two Philistine defeats; David's fame spreads
#   Key: God establishes David between the two ark episodes — international recognition

# Chapter 15: "The Ark Brought to Jerusalem — Properly"
#   Section 1: vv 1-15  — David prepares Levites to carry the ark correctly
#   Section 2: vv 16-29 — Music and celebration; the procession succeeds
#   Key: The Chronicler adds extensive Levitical detail absent from 2 Samuel 6

# Chapter 16: "David's Psalm of Thanksgiving"
#   Section 1: vv 1-7   — The ark placed in the tent; offerings and blessings
#   Section 2: vv 8-36  — The psalm (composite: Ps 105:1-15 + Ps 96:1-13 + Ps 106:1,47-48)
#   Section 3: vv 37-43 — Regular worship established at Gibeon and Jerusalem
#   Key: David as liturgist — composing and assigning worship. This is the Chronicler's David.

# ── THE DAVIDIC COVENANT AND WARS (Ch 17-20) ─────────────────────────────

# Chapter 17: "The Davidic Covenant — God's Promise to David"
#   Section 1: vv 1-15  — Nathan's oracle: "I will build YOU a house"
#   Section 2: vv 16-27 — David's prayer of thanksgiving
#   Key: Nearly identical to 2 Samuel 7 — the covenant is too important to paraphrase

# Chapter 18: "David's Military Victories"
#   Section 1: vv 1-8   — Philistines, Moabites, Hadadezer, Arameans defeated
#   Section 2: vv 9-17  — Tribute flows in; David's officials listed
#   Key: Compressed military history — the Chronicler moves quickly through wars

# Chapter 19: "The Ammonite-Aramean War"
#   Section 1: vv 1-9   — Ammonites humiliate David's envoys
#   Section 2: vv 10-19 — Joab and Abishai defeat the coalition
#   Key: Parallel to 2 Sam 10 — but the Chronicler OMITS ch 11 (Bathsheba/Uriah)

# Chapter 20: "More Wars; Philistine Giants"
#   Section 1: vv 1-3   — Capture of Rabbah (the Chronicler skips Bathsheba entirely)
#   Section 2: vv 4-8   — Three Philistine giants killed
#   Key: The most significant OMISSION in Chronicles: David's sin is erased from the record

# ── TEMPLE PREPARATION (Ch 21-27) ────────────────────────────────────────

# Chapter 21: "The Census, the Plague, and the Threshing Floor"
#   Section 1: vv 1-8   — Satan incites David to take a census (cf. 2 Sam 24:1 — "the LORD")
#   Section 2: vv 9-17  — Plague strikes; the angel at the threshing floor of Araunah/Ornan
#   Section 3: vv 18-30 — David buys the site; builds an altar; "this is the house of the LORD"
#   Key: The census disaster PRODUCES the temple site — evil redirected to sacred purpose

# Chapter 22: "David Prepares for the Temple"
#   Section 1: vv 1-5   — David stockpiles materials for the temple
#   Section 2: vv 6-16  — David's charge to Solomon: "build the house"
#   Section 3: vv 17-19 — David commands the leaders to help Solomon
#   Key: David cannot build but prepares everything — the vision exceeds the builder's lifetime

# Chapter 23: "The Levites Organised for Service"
#   Section 1: vv 1-6   — David makes Solomon king; numbers the Levites
#   Section 2: vv 7-32  — Levitical divisions by family: Gershon, Kohath, Merari
#   Key: Institutional organisation — the Chronicler cares about how worship is structured

# Chapter 24: "Priestly Divisions — the 24 Courses"
#   Section 1: vv 1-19  — The 24 priestly divisions (including Abijah — Luke 1:5)
#   Section 2: vv 20-31 — Remaining Levitical families assigned by lot
#   Key: The 24-course system persists into NT times — Zechariah serves in Abijah's division

# Chapter 25: "The Musicians — 24 Divisions"
#   Section 1: vv 1-7   — Asaph, Heman, Jeduthun set apart for prophetic music
#   Section 2: vv 8-31  — 24 divisions of musicians assigned by lot
#   Key: Music as prophecy — the musicians "prophesy" with harps and lyres

# Chapter 26: "Gatekeepers and Treasurers"
#   Section 1: vv 1-19  — Divisions of gatekeepers for the temple
#   Section 2: vv 20-32 — Treasury officials and external administrators
#   Key: Every role in worship is accounted for — nothing is improvised

# Chapter 27: "Military and Civic Organisation"
#   Section 1: vv 1-15  — Twelve monthly military divisions (24,000 each)
#   Section 2: vv 16-34 — Tribal officers; David's advisors; the incomplete census
#   Key: The kingdom is organised for both war and worship — parallel structures

# ── DAVID'S FAREWELL (Ch 28-29) ──────────────────────────────────────────

# Chapter 28: "David's Charge to Solomon and Israel"
#   Section 1: vv 1-10  — David addresses the assembly; Solomon chosen by God
#   Section 2: vv 11-21 — Temple plans given to Solomon — "the Spirit gave him"
#   Key: Temple blueprints given by divine revelation, like the tabernacle to Moses

# Chapter 29: "Offerings for the Temple; David's Prayer; Solomon Enthroned"
#   Section 1: vv 1-9   — David and the leaders give generously for the temple
#   Section 2: vv 10-20 — David's doxology — "Yours, O LORD, is the greatness"
#   Section 3: vv 21-30 — Solomon enthroned; David's death; summary of his reign
#   Key: The book ends with worship, generosity, and orderly succession — the Chronicler's ideal
