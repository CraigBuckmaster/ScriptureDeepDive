# Narrative continuity:
#   2 Kings picks up seamlessly from 1 Kings. Originally one book in the
#   Hebrew canon (Melachim / "Kings"), divided by the LXX. 1 Kings ends
#   with Ahab's death (ch 22) and Ahaziah's accession. 2 Kings opens with
#   Ahaziah's injury and Elijah's final confrontation with royal idolatry.
#   The book carries the story from the Elisha cycle through the fall of
#   both kingdoms: Israel (722 BC) and Judah (586 BC).
#
# The theological arc:
#   If 1 Kings asked "will Israel be faithful?" — 2 Kings answers "no."
#   The northern kingdom never reforms and is destroyed by Assyria.
#   Judah gets two great reformers (Hezekiah, Josiah) but both are too
#   late. The Deuteronomistic verdict: the covenant curses of Deut 28
#   are fulfilled. Yet the book ends with a flicker of hope — Jehoiachin
#   is released from prison in Babylon (25:27-30).

# ── ELISHA CYCLE (Ch 1-8) ─────────────────────────────────────────────────

# Chapter 1: "Fire from Heaven — Elijah vs Ahaziah"
#   Section 1: vv 1-8  — Ahaziah's injury, Baal-Zebub, the messengers intercepted
#   Section 2: vv 9-16 — Fire from heaven on the soldiers; the third captain's plea
#   Section 3: vv 17-18 — Ahaziah dies; Joram succeeds
#   Key: Elijah's final royal confrontation; "Is it because there is no God in Israel?"
#   Hebrew: ba'al z'vûv (lord of flies — deliberate mockery of ba'al z'vûl, "lord prince")
#   Cross-ref: Luke 9:54 (James and John want to call fire from heaven — Jesus rebukes them)

# Chapter 2: "The Chariot of Fire — Elijah Taken, Elisha Succeeds"
#   Section 1: vv 1-12 — Gilgal to Bethel to Jericho to Jordan; the chariot of fire
#   Section 2: vv 13-18 — Elisha parts the Jordan; the sons of the prophets
#   Section 3: vv 19-25 — Healing Jericho's water; the bears at Bethel
#   Key: Double portion of Elijah's spirit; mantle transfer; prophetic succession
#   Timeline: Elijah taken up (c. 852 BC)
#   Cross-ref: Mal 4:5 (Elijah's return); Matt 17:1-13 (Transfiguration)

# Chapter 3: "The Campaign Against Moab"
#   Section 1: vv 1-12  — Joram, Jehoshaphat, Edom march; water crisis
#   Section 2: vv 13-20 — Elisha called; water in the valley
#   Section 3: vv 21-27 — Moab deceived; Mesha's child sacrifice; Israel withdraws
#   Key: Mesha Stele (archaeological confirmation); horror of child sacrifice
#   ANE: The Moabite Stone — one of the most important inscriptions for Kings

# Chapter 4: "Elisha's Miracles — The Prophet Among the People"
#   Section 1: vv 1-7   — The widow's oil
#   Section 2: vv 8-37  — The Shunammite woman; her son raised from death
#   Section 3: vv 38-44 — Death in the pot; feeding a hundred
#   Key: Parallels to Elijah (1 Kgs 17); foreshadowing of Jesus' miracles
#   Cross-ref: Luke 7:11-17 (widow of Nain); John 6 (feeding the multitude)

# Chapter 5: "Naaman the Leper — Faith from an Unexpected Source"
#   Section 1: vv 1-14  — Naaman comes to Elisha; "wash in the Jordan seven times"
#   Section 2: vv 15-19 — Naaman's confession: "there is no God in all the earth except in Israel"
#   Section 3: vv 20-27 — Gehazi's greed; leprosy transferred
#   Key: A Gentile healed; the servant girl's faith; Luke 4:27 (Jesus cites this)
#   People: Naaman, Gehazi

# Chapter 6: "Invisible Armies — Elisha and the Arameans"
#   Section 1: vv 1-7   — The floating axe head
#   Section 2: vv 8-23  — "Those who are with us are more than those who are with them"
#   Section 3: vv 24-33 — Ben-Hadad besieges Samaria; famine horror
#   Key: Spiritual sight vs blindness; Elisha's prayer; mercy to enemies

# Chapter 7: "The Siege Lifted — Lepers at the Gate"
#   Section 1: vv 1-2   — Elisha's prophecy: abundance tomorrow
#   Section 2: vv 3-11  — Four lepers discover the empty Aramean camp
#   Section 3: vv 12-20 — The officer trampled; the word of the LORD fulfilled exactly
#   Key: Faith vs doubt; the marginalized as agents of salvation

# Chapter 8: "Hazael, Joram, Ahaziah — The Net Tightens"
#   Section 1: vv 1-6   — The Shunammite woman's land restored
#   Section 2: vv 7-15  — Elisha weeps; Hazael murders Ben-Hadad
#   Section 3: vv 16-29 — Joram and Ahaziah of Judah; the house of Ahab corrupts Judah
#   Key: Elisha's tears over the evil Hazael will do; Judah drawn into Israel's sin

# ── REVOLUTION AND REFORM (Ch 9-12) ──────────────────────────────────────

# Chapter 9: "Jehu's Revolution — Blood at Jezreel"
#   Section 1: vv 1-13  — Jehu anointed king by a prophetic messenger
#   Section 2: vv 14-26 — Joram killed; Ahaziah wounded; Naboth's vineyard avenged
#   Section 3: vv 27-37 — Jezebel's death — "who is on my side?"
#   Key: 1 Kgs 21:21-24 fulfilled; the cost of prophetic revolution
#   Timeline: Jehu's revolution (c. 841 BC)

# Chapter 10: "The Purge — Jehu Destroys Baal Worship"
#   Section 1: vv 1-17  — Seventy sons of Ahab killed; the house of Ahab destroyed
#   Section 2: vv 18-28 — Jehu traps and kills all Baal worshippers
#   Section 3: vv 29-36 — Verdict: Jehu did not turn from Jeroboam's sins; Israel diminished
#   Key: Jehu's zeal is genuine but not pure; violence as instrument of reform

# Chapter 11: "Athaliah and Joash — The Boy King Saved"
#   Section 1: vv 1-3   — Athaliah seizes the throne; Joash hidden for six years
#   Section 2: vv 4-16  — Jehoiada's coup; Athaliah overthrown; the covenant renewed
#   Section 3: vv 17-21 — Baal worship destroyed; the boy king enthroned
#   Key: The Davidic line nearly extinguished; one child preserves the promise

# Chapter 12: "Joash Repairs the Temple"
#   Section 1: vv 1-3   — Joash's reign; partial faithfulness
#   Section 2: vv 4-16  — The temple repair project; the chest at the gate
#   Section 3: vv 17-21 — Hazael threatens; Joash assassinated
#   Key: Temple as barometer of covenant health; institutional reform

# ── THE LONG DECLINE (Ch 13-16) ──────────────────────────────────────────

# Chapter 13: "Elisha's Death — The Arrow of Victory"
#   Section 1: vv 1-9   — Jehoahaz; Israel oppressed by Aram
#   Section 2: vv 10-19 — Jehoash; Elisha's deathbed prophecy; the arrows
#   Section 3: vv 20-25 — Elisha's bones raise a dead man; three victories
#   Key: Elisha's final act; even in death, prophetic power persists

# Chapter 14: "Amaziah and Jeroboam II"
#   Section 1: vv 1-14  — Amaziah's reign; war with Israel; Jerusalem breached
#   Section 2: vv 15-22 — Jeroboam II expands Israel's borders
#   Section 3: vv 23-29 — Prosperity without righteousness; Jonah mentioned (v 25)
#   Key: Jeroboam II = peak territorial recovery but spiritual bankruptcy

# Chapter 15: "The Parade of Kings — Rapid Decline"
#   Section 1: vv 1-7   — Azariah/Uzziah (struck with leprosy)
#   Section 2: vv 8-22  — Five kings of Israel in rapid succession; assassinations
#   Section 3: vv 23-38 — Pekah, Jotham; Assyria's shadow falls (Tiglath-Pileser)
#   Key: Political chaos; the Assyrian threat materialises

# Chapter 16: "Ahaz — Judah Surrenders to Assyria"
#   Section 1: vv 1-4   — Ahaz walks in the ways of Israel's kings; child sacrifice
#   Section 2: vv 5-9   — The Syro-Ephraimite war; Ahaz appeals to Assyria
#   Section 3: vv 10-20 — Ahaz copies a Damascus altar; rearranges the temple
#   Key: The worst king of Judah so far; voluntarily paganises the temple
#   Cross-ref: Isaiah 7:1-14 (Isaiah's Immanuel prophecy during this crisis)

# ── THE FALL OF ISRAEL (Ch 17) ───────────────────────────────────────────

# Chapter 17: "The End of the Northern Kingdom"
#   Section 1: vv 1-6   — Hoshea's reign; Assyrian siege; Samaria falls (722 BC)
#   Section 2: vv 7-23  — The Deuteronomistic historian's verdict: why Israel fell
#   Section 3: vv 24-41 — Foreign peoples settle in Samaria; syncretistic worship
#   Key: THE theological centre of the book. The verdict is comprehensive.
#   Timeline: Fall of Samaria (722 BC)
#   ANE: Sargon II's annals; Assyrian deportation records

# ── HEZEKIAH'S REFORM (Ch 18-20) ─────────────────────────────────────────

# Chapter 18: "Hezekiah — Judah's Great Reformer"
#   Section 1: vv 1-8   — Hezekiah destroys the high places and the bronze serpent
#   Section 2: vv 9-16  — Fall of Samaria recapped; Hezekiah pays tribute to Sennacherib
#   Section 3: vv 17-37 — The Rabshakeh's speech; psychological warfare at Jerusalem's walls
#   Key: The contrast with Ahaz; the Rabshakeh's terrifying rhetoric
#   Timeline: Sennacherib's invasion (701 BC)
#   ANE: Taylor Prism; Sennacherib's own account

# Chapter 19: "Isaiah and Hezekiah — The LORD Delivers Jerusalem"
#   Section 1: vv 1-7   — Hezekiah in sackcloth; Isaiah's first response
#   Section 2: vv 8-19  — Sennacherib's letter; Hezekiah spreads it before the LORD
#   Section 3: vv 20-37 — Isaiah's oracle; 185,000 Assyrians struck; Sennacherib assassinated
#   Key: Prayer + prophetic word = deliverance; the inviolability of Zion?
#   Cross-ref: Isaiah 36-37 (parallel account)

# Chapter 20: "Hezekiah's Illness and Babylon's Embassy"
#   Section 1: vv 1-11  — "Set your house in order"; the shadow retreats; 15 years added
#   Section 2: vv 12-19 — Merodach-Baladan's envoys; Hezekiah shows everything; Isaiah's warning
#   Section 3: vv 20-21 — Summary; the tunnel (Siloam inscription)
#   Key: Hezekiah's pride seeds Judah's future destruction by Babylon
#   ANE: Siloam Tunnel inscription — confirms Hezekiah's water tunnel

# ── MANASSEH AND JOSIAH (Ch 21-23) ───────────────────────────────────────

# Chapter 21: "Manasseh — The Point of No Return"
#   Section 1: vv 1-9   — 55 years of apostasy; rebuilds high places; child sacrifice; sorcery
#   Section 2: vv 10-18 — Prophets: Judah will be destroyed because of Manasseh
#   Section 3: vv 19-26 — Amon; assassinated after two years
#   Key: Manasseh undoes everything Hezekiah built; this is irreversible (23:26-27)

# Chapter 22: "Josiah Finds the Book of the Law"
#   Section 1: vv 1-7   — Josiah repairs the temple (echo of Joash)
#   Section 2: vv 8-13  — Hilkiah finds the Book of the Law; Josiah tears his robes
#   Section 3: vv 14-20 — Huldah the prophetess: judgment is set but delayed for Josiah
#   Key: Discovery of Torah (likely Deuteronomy); Huldah — a woman validates Scripture

# Chapter 23: "The Great Reform — and Its Limits"
#   Section 1: vv 1-14  — Covenant renewal; destruction of pagan shrines
#   Section 2: vv 15-20 — Josiah destroys Bethel altar (fulfils 1 Kgs 13:2)
#   Section 3: vv 21-30 — Passover restored; verdict: "still the LORD did not turn"; Josiah killed
#   Section 4: vv 31-37 — Jehoahaz deported; Jehoiakim enthroned as Egyptian vassal
#   Key: The most thorough reform in Israel's history — and it's still not enough

# ── THE FALL OF JERUSALEM (Ch 24-25) ─────────────────────────────────────

# Chapter 24: "Babylon Rises — Judah's Final Kings"
#   Section 1: vv 1-7   — Jehoiakim rebels; Nebuchadnezzar invades
#   Section 2: vv 8-17  — Jehoiachin deposed; first deportation (597 BC); temple treasures taken
#   Section 3: vv 18-20 — Zedekiah installed; "he did evil"
#   Key: First deportation; Ezekiel is among the exiles (Ezek 1:1-3)
#   Timeline: First deportation (597 BC)

# Chapter 25: "The Destruction of Jerusalem"
#   Section 1: vv 1-12  — Siege, famine, breach, Zedekiah captured and blinded
#   Section 2: vv 13-21 — Temple destroyed; vessels taken; executions at Riblah
#   Section 3: vv 22-26 — Gedaliah appointed governor; assassinated; remnant flees to Egypt
#   Section 4: vv 27-30 — Epilogue: Jehoiachin released from prison — a flicker of hope
#   Key: The temple destroyed (586 BC); the Davidic promise seems extinguished —
#         but the final four verses keep it alive
#   Timeline: Fall of Jerusalem (586 BC); temple destroyed
#   Cross-ref: Jeremiah 39-41 (parallel), Lam 1-2, Ezek 33:21
