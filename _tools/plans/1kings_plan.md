## Step 3: Chapter-by-Chapter Outline

### Chapter 1: Adonijah's Power Grab; Solomon Anointed
- **Section 1: Verses 1–27 — David Old and Cold; Adonijah Exalts Himself**
  - David's decline, Abishag, Adonijah's feast, Bathsheba & Nathan's intervention
  - Hebrew: *yitnaśśēʾ* (exalts himself), *ḥagîgāh* (sacrificial feast)
  - People: David, Adonijah, Bathsheba, Nathan, Abishag, Joab, Abiathar
  - Places: En Rogel, Gihon
- **Section 2: Verses 28–53 — Solomon Anointed at Gihon; Adonijah Seizes the Altar Horns**
  - Nathan-Bathsheba plan, anointing at Gihon, Adonijah's party scatters
  - Hebrew: *māšaḥ* (anointed), *qeren hammizbeaḥ* (horns of the altar)

### Chapter 2: David's Charge; Solomon Secures the Throne
- **Section 1: Verses 1–12 — David's Last Words to Solomon; David Dies**
  - Deathbed charge: be strong, keep the Torah, deal with Joab and Shimei
  - Hebrew: *ḥăzaq* (be strong), *derek* (way/path of all the earth)
- **Section 2: Verses 13–46 — Adonijah, Joab, Abiathar, Shimei: Four Threats Removed**
  - Adonijah's request for Abishag, Abiathar exiled, Joab executed, Shimei executed
  - Hebrew: *wĕhannāḵôn* (the kingdom was established)

### Chapter 3: Solomon's Wisdom; the Two Mothers
- **Section 1: Verses 1–15 — The Dream at Gibeon: Ask What I Shall Give You**
  - Solomon's marriage to Pharaoh's daughter, high-place worship, the dream
  - Hebrew: *lēb šōmēaʿ* (listening/understanding heart)
  - Places: Gibeon
- **Section 2: Verses 16–28 — The Judgment of Solomon: Divide the Living Child**
  - The two prostitutes, Solomon's verdict, Israel's awe
  - Hebrew: *ḥokmāh* (wisdom), *mišpāṭ* (justice/judgment)

### Chapter 4: Solomon's Officials and Prosperity
- **Section 1: Verses 1–19 — The Administration: Twelve District Governors**
  - Solomon's cabinet, twelve supply districts
  - People: Azariah, Zabud, Ahishar, Adoniram
- **Section 2: Verses 20–34 — Wisdom Greater Than All: Cedar to Hyssop**
  - Population, daily provisions, 3,000 proverbs, 1,005 songs
  - Hebrew: *ḥokmāh* exceeding the sand of the seashore

### Chapter 5: Alliance with Hiram; Preparing for the Temple
- **Section 1: Verses 1–12 — Hiram of Tyre: Timber and Treaty**
  - Solomon's message, Hiram's response, wheat and oil for cedar and cypress
  - People: Hiram of Tyre
  - Places: Lebanon, Tyre
- **Section 2: Verses 13–18 — The Labour Force: 30,000 Conscripts**
  - Corvée labour, stonecutters, the Gebalites
  - Hebrew: *mas* (forced labour)

### Chapter 6: The Temple Built
- **Section 1: Verses 1–22 — Dimensions, Materials, the Inner Sanctuary**
  - 480 years after Egypt, 60×20×30 cubits, cedar panelling, gold overlay
  - Hebrew: *dĕbîr* (inner sanctuary / Most Holy Place)
  - Timeline: ~966 BC, 480 years after the Exodus
- **Section 2: Verses 23–38 — The Cherubim, Doors, and the Conditional Promise**
  - Two 15-foot cherubim, olive-wood doors, the 7-year construction
  - Cross-ref: God's conditional word (vv.11–13) echoing the Davidic covenant

### Chapter 7: Solomon's Palace; the Temple Furnishings
- **Section 1: Verses 1–12 — The Palace Complex: 13 Years of Building**
  - Forest of Lebanon hall, throne hall, Pharaoh's daughter's house
  - Hebrew: *bêt yaʿar halLĕbānôn* (House of the Forest of Lebanon)
- **Section 2: Verses 13–51 — Huram's Bronze Work; the Sea; the Furnishings Completed**
  - Huram-abi, two pillars (Jakin/Boaz), the bronze sea, ten basins
  - People: Huram (craftsman from Tyre)
  - Hebrew: *Yākîn* (He establishes), *Bōʿaz* (In him is strength)

### Chapter 8: The Temple Dedication; Solomon's Prayer
- **Section 1: Verses 1–21 — The Ark Brought In; the Cloud Fills the Temple**
  - Ark transferred, priests withdraw, glory fills the house
  - Hebrew: *kābôd YHWH* (glory of the LORD)
  - Timeline: Feast of Tabernacles, 7th month
- **Section 2: Verses 22–53 — Solomon's Dedicatory Prayer: Seven Petitions**
  - Blessing, prayer with outstretched hands, 7 "if/when" petitions
  - Hebrew: *tĕḥinnāh* (supplication), *šāmayim* (heaven)
- **Section 3: Verses 54–66 — The Blessing; the Sacrifices; the People Dismissed**
  - 22,000 cattle, 120,000 sheep, 14-day festival, the people sent home joyful

### Chapter 9: God's Second Appearance; Cities and Fleet
- **Section 1: Verses 1–9 — The LORD Appears Again: Conditional Covenant**
  - Warning: if you turn aside, this house becomes a heap of ruins
  - Cross-ref: Echoes Deut 28 blessings/curses
- **Section 2: Verses 10–28 — Twenty Cities; the Fleet at Ezion-Geber**
  - Hiram's dissatisfaction, Gezer, forced labour, fleet at Red Sea
  - Places: Ezion-geber, Ophir
  - Hebrew: *ʿebed Šĕlōmōh* (Solomon's servants)

### Chapter 10: The Queen of Sheba; Solomon's Wealth
- **Section 1: Verses 1–13 — The Queen of Sheba: Nothing Was Hidden**
  - Her hard questions, Solomon's answers, breathlessness, gifts
  - People: Queen of Sheba
  - Places: Sheba (likely modern Yemen)
- **Section 2: Verses 14–29 — 666 Talents of Gold; the Throne; the Horses**
  - Annual income, ivory throne, gold shields, trade network
  - Hebrew: *šēš mēʾôt šiššîm wāšēš* (666 talents)

### Chapter 11: Solomon's Apostasy; the Kingdom Torn
- **Section 1: Verses 1–25 — 700 Wives Turn His Heart; Adversaries Raised**
  - Foreign wives, Ashtoreth, Chemosh, Molek, Hadad, Rezon
  - Hebrew: *nāšîm nokriyyôt* (foreign women)
  - Cross-ref: Deut 17:17 (the king shall not multiply wives)
- **Section 2: Verses 26–43 — Ahijah Tears the Cloak: Ten Tribes for Jeroboam**
  - Jeroboam's rise, Ahijah's prophecy, Solomon's death
  - People: Jeroboam, Ahijah the Shilonite
  - Hebrew: *qāraʿ* (to tear) — enacted parable

### Chapter 12: The Kingdom Divides
- **Section 1: Verses 1–24 — Rehoboam's Folly: My Little Finger Is Thicker**
  - Shechem assembly, elders' advice rejected, the young men's counsel
  - People: Rehoboam, Jeroboam
  - Places: Shechem
  - Hebrew: *qāṭonnî* (my little finger)
- **Section 2: Verses 25–33 — Jeroboam's Golden Calves: Here Are Your Gods**
  - Dan and Bethel calves, non-Levitical priests, alternative feast
  - Places: Dan, Bethel
  - Cross-ref: Exod 32 (Aaron's golden calf — identical formula)

### Chapter 13: The Man of God from Judah; the Old Prophet
- **Section 1: Verses 1–10 — The Man of God Confronts the Altar at Bethel**
  - Prophecy against Bethel altar, Josiah named 300 years early, hand withered
  - People: man of God from Judah, Jeroboam
  - Cross-ref: 2 Kgs 23:15–18 (Josiah fulfils this prophecy)
- **Section 2: Verses 11–34 — The Old Prophet's Deception; the Lion on the Road**
  - Lies about an angel, shared meal, judgment, the lion and the donkey
  - Hebrew: *nābîʾ zāqēn* (old prophet)

### Chapter 14: Jeroboam's Son Dies; Rehoboam's Judah
- **Section 1: Verses 1–20 — Ahijah's Word to Jeroboam's Wife: Disaster**
  - Disguised visit, blind Ahijah sees clearly, Abijah dies at the threshold
  - People: Ahijah, Jeroboam's wife
- **Section 2: Verses 21–31 — Rehoboam: Judah Does Evil; Shishak Invades**
  - High places, Asherah poles, male shrine prostitutes, Egyptian raid
  - People: Rehoboam, Shishak
  - Timeline: ~925 BC Shishak invasion (confirmed by Egyptian records)

### Chapter 15: Abijah and Asa (Judah); Nadab and Baasha (Israel)
- **Section 1: Verses 1–24 — Asa's Reforms: He Did Right in the Eyes of the LORD**
  - Abijah's brief reign, Asa's 41-year reign, Maakah's idol removed
  - People: Abijah, Asa, Maakah
- **Section 2: Verses 25–34 — Baasha Assassinates Nadab; Jeroboam's Line Destroyed**
  - Nadab's 2 years, Baasha's coup, Ahijah's prophecy fulfilled

### Chapter 16: Elah, Zimri, Omri, Ahab — Rapid Succession
- **Section 1: Verses 1–20 — Baasha to Zimri: Seven Days on the Throne**
  - Jehu's prophecy against Baasha, Elah assassinated, Zimri's 7-day reign
  - People: Baasha, Elah, Zimri
- **Section 2: Verses 21–34 — Omri Builds Samaria; Ahab: Worst of All**
  - Omri vs Tibni, Samaria founded, Ahab marries Jezebel, Baal temple built
  - People: Omri, Ahab, Jezebel
  - Places: Samaria
  - Timeline: ~880 BC Omri founds Samaria (confirmed by Mesha Stele + Assyrian records)

### Chapter 17: Elijah Appears: Drought, Ravens, and Resurrection
- **Section 1: Verses 1–16 — No Rain; Fed by Ravens; the Widow's Jar**
  - Elijah's sudden appearance, Kerith brook, Zarephath widow
  - People: Elijah, widow of Zarephath
  - Places: Kerith, Zarephath (Sidon)
  - Hebrew: *ʾĒliyyāhû* (My God is YHWH)
- **Section 2: Verses 17–24 — The Widow's Son Raised: Now I Know**
  - Death, Elijah stretches over the child, life returns
  - Cross-ref: Luke 4:25–26 (Jesus cites this as grace to a Gentile)

### Chapter 18: Carmel: The LORD, He Is God
- **Section 1: Verses 1–19 — Three Years of Drought; Obadiah and the 100 Prophets**
  - Obadiah hides prophets, meets Elijah, Ahab confronts Elijah
  - People: Elijah, Ahab, Obadiah
- **Section 2: Verses 20–40 — The Contest on Mount Carmel**
  - 450 prophets of Baal, the taunting, the soaking altar, fire from heaven
  - Places: Mount Carmel, Kishon brook
  - Hebrew: *ʿad-mātay ʾattem pōsĕḥîm ʿal-šĕtê hassĕʿippîm* (how long will you limp between two opinions?)
- **Section 3: Verses 41–46 — Rain Returns; Elijah Outruns Ahab's Chariot**
  - Cloud like a man's hand, torrential rain, supernatural run to Jezreel

### Chapter 19: Horeb: The Still Small Voice
- **Section 1: Verses 1–14 — Jezebel's Threat; Elijah Flees to Horeb**
  - "I've had enough, LORD, take my life," angel feeds him, 40-day journey
  - People: Elijah, Jezebel
  - Places: Beersheba, Horeb/Sinai
  - Hebrew: *qôl dĕmāmāh daqqāh* (a sound of thin silence / still small voice)
- **Section 2: Verses 15–21 — Three Commissions; Elisha Called**
  - Anoint Hazael, Jehu, Elisha; 7,000 who have not bowed to Baal
  - People: Elisha, Hazael, Jehu
  - Cross-ref: Rom 11:4 (Paul cites the 7,000)

### Chapter 20: Ahab's Wars with Ben-Hadad
- **Section 1: Verses 1–21 — Ben-Hadad Besieges Samaria; the Prophet's Word**
  - Outrageous demands, 7,000 young officers, surprise attack
  - People: Ben-Hadad, Ahab
- **Section 2: Verses 22–43 — Second Victory; Ahab Spares Ben-Hadad; Condemned**
  - Aphek battle, wall collapse, treaty instead of herem, prophet's parable
  - Hebrew: *ḥerem* (devoted to destruction — Ahab violated this)

### Chapter 21: Naboth's Vineyard
- **Section 1: Verses 1–16 — Jezebel's Plot: Letters, False Witnesses, Murder**
  - Naboth refuses to sell ancestral land, Jezebel fabricates charges
  - People: Naboth, Ahab, Jezebel
  - Places: Jezreel
  - Hebrew: *naḥălat ʾăbōtāy* (inheritance of my fathers)
- **Section 2: Verses 17–29 — Elijah Confronts Ahab: Dogs Will Lick Your Blood**
  - "Have you murdered and also taken possession?", Ahab repents temporarily
  - Cross-ref: 2 Kgs 9:25–26 (fulfilment at Naboth's plot)

### Chapter 22: Micaiah's Prophecy; Ahab Dies at Ramoth Gilead
- **Section 1: Verses 1–28 — 400 Prophets Say Go; Micaiah Says Disaster**
  - Jehoshaphat visits, lying spirit in the prophets' mouths, Micaiah imprisoned
  - People: Ahab, Jehoshaphat, Micaiah, Zedekiah
  - Hebrew: *rûaḥ šeqer* (lying spirit)
- **Section 2: Verses 29–40 — Ahab Disguised; a Random Arrow; Dogs Lick His Blood**
  - Disguise fails, arrow "at random" finds the gap, blood washed at pool
  - Cross-ref: 1 Kgs 21:19 (Elijah's prophecy fulfilled precisely)
- **Section 3: Verses 41–53 — Jehoshaphat; Ahaziah: A Brief Epilogue**
  - Jehoshaphat's 25 years, ships wrecked, Ahaziah walks in his father's ways

## Step 6: Scholarly Apparatus

**ANE Sources:**
- Assyrian Royal Annals (Shalmaneser III mentions Ahab at Battle of Qarqar, 853 BC)
- Mesha Stele (confirms Omride dynasty and Moabite relations)
- Shishak/Shoshenq I relief at Karnak (confirms 1 Kgs 14:25–26 invasion)
- Phoenician temple architecture parallels (Ain Dara temple)
- Tel Dan Stele ("House of David" inscription, ~841 BC)

**Textual Notes:**
- LXX of 1 Kings has a significantly different chapter order (esp. chs 4–5, 12)
- 4QKgs fragments from Qumran
- Solomon's dream at Gibeon: LXX adds material not in MT
- The "lying spirit" passage (22:19–23): MT vs LXX variants

**Major Scholarly Debates:**
1. The United Monarchy: historical maximalism vs minimalism (Dever vs Finkelstein)
2. Solomon's temple: archaeological evidence and absence
3. The Deuteronomistic History: when was 1 Kings composed? Josianic or exilic edition?
