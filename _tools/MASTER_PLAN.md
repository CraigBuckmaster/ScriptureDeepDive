## Build Waves

Books are grouped into waves by thematic affinity and dependency. Within each wave, build order
is sequenced so that scholars, people entries, and timeline events can be batched efficiently.

### Wave 1 — Complete the Wisdom/Poetry Corpus (20 ch)

| # | Book | Ch | Why here | New scholars |
|---|------|----|----------|-------------|
| 1 | Ecclesiastes | 12 | Pairs with Job (same tradition, opposite angle) | Longman, Fox |
| 2 | Song of Solomon | 8 | Completes wisdom corpus | Longman (reuse), Garrett |

**Rationale:** Small, fast, high-impact. Ecclesiastes directly connects to Job's theology.
Longman covers both books (add once, use twice). Completes Wisdom Literature
(Job + Proverbs + Ecclesiastes + Song of Solomon).

**Existing scholars extended:** Alter → add Ecclesiastes, Song of Solomon.

### Wave 2 — Psalms (150 ch)

| # | Book | Ch | Why here | New scholars |
|---|------|----|----------|-------------|
| 3 | Psalms | 150 | Most cross-referenced book; massive standalone project | Kidner (extend), VanGemeren, Goldingay |

**Rationale:** Deserves its own wave. 150 chapters = ~20 batches of 7–8.

### Wave 3 — Major Prophets (183 ch)

| # | Book | Ch | Why here | New scholars |
|---|------|----|----------|-------------|
| 4 | Daniel | 12 | Shortest major prophet; bridges OT/NT apocalyptic | Collins, Longman (reuse) |
| 5 | Lamentations | 5 | Short; pairs with Jeremiah but can stand alone | Berlin, O'Connor |
| 6 | Isaiah | 66 | The "fifth Gospel"; connects to everything NT | Oswalt, Childs |
| 7 | Jeremiah | 52 | Second-longest OT book; massive prophetic voice | Lundbom, Brueggemann |

Isaiah before Jeremiah because the NT quotes Isaiah more than any other OT book.
Block already exists for Judges — extending him to Ezekiel (he wrote NICOT Ezekiel, 2 vols).

**Existing scholars extended:** Block → add Ezekiel.

## Decision points

These choices can be revisited at each wave boundary:

1. **Psalms timing** — Wave 2 puts it early (unlocks cross-references). Could defer
   to later if smaller books build momentum better.
2. **Minor Prophet ordering** — listed by thematic affinity, not canonical order.
   Could reorder to canonical if the navigation benefits outweigh the thematic grouping.
3. **Pauline ordering** — Romans first is theological; 1 Thessalonians first is
   chronological. Theological ordering chosen because it matches how most readers
   approach Paul.
4. **Scholar count** — 35 new scholars is a lot. Could reduce by using fewer per book
   (2 specialists instead of 3) or by finding more multi-book scholars.
