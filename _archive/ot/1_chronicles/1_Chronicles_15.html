1 Chronicles 15

The Ark Brought to Jerusalem — Properly

Verses 1–15 — David Prepares the Levites to Carry the Ark Correctly
123456789101112131415

Context

David learns from the Uzzah disaster. He prepares a tent for the ark, then declares: “No one but the Levites may carry the ark of God, because the LORD chose them.” He assembles 862 Levites from the three main families (Kohath, Merari, Gershon) and three additional Levitical clans. David explains: “The LORD our God broke out against us because we did not inquire of him about how to do it in the prescribed way.” The lesson is explicit: zeal without knowledge is dangerous; the method matters.

Cross-Reference

  • Num 4:15The Kohathites are designated to carry holy objects — the law David now follows.
  • Exod 25:14“Insert the poles into the rings on the sides of the ark to carry it.” No carts. Poles and Levitical shoulders.

Calvin’s Commentary

John Calvin, Commentaries — Faithful Paraphrase
15:13

David’s admission of error teaches that even good leaders make mistakes, and the mark of godliness is not perfection but willingness to learn. The king who failed in chapter 13 succeeds in chapter 15 because he submits to God’s word.

NET Bible Notes

NET Bible Full Notes Edition — Biblical Studies Press
15:15

bĕmottōt (“with poles”) on their shoulders (biktēpām) — exactly as prescribed in Num 7:9 and Exod 25:14. The Chronicler uses the technical vocabulary of the Priestly legislation.

Verses 16–29 — Music and Celebration; the Procession Succeeds
1617181920212223242526272829

Context

David appoints Levitical musicians for the procession: singers, instrumentalists, and gatekeepers. Heman, Asaph, and Ethan lead with bronze cymbals; others play harps and lyres. Kenaniah, the head Levite, directs the music because “he was skillful at it.” The ark enters Jerusalem with shouting, trumpets, cymbals, harps, and lyres. David dances, wearing a linen ephod. Michal (Saul’s daughter) watches from a window and “despises him in her heart.” The Chronicler preserves this detail from Samuel but omits the subsequent quarrel — the positive event takes priority.

Cross-Reference

  • 2 Sam 6:12–19The Samuel parallel includes the quarrel with Michal; the Chronicler stops at the despising.
  • Ps 150:1–6“Praise the LORD with the sounding of the trumpet, with harp and lyre... Let everything that has breath praise the LORD.” The full-spectrum worship described here.

Calvin’s Commentary

John Calvin, Commentaries — Faithful Paraphrase
15:29

Michal’s contempt for David’s dancing warns against the spirit of religious sophistication that despises genuine worship. Those who are too dignified to worship wholeheartedly are spiritually poorer than those who dance.

NET Bible Notes

NET Bible Full Notes Edition — Biblical Studies Press
15:27

The “linen ephod” (ʾēpōd bad) is priestly clothing (cf. 1 Sam 2:18; 22:18). David’s wearing it blurs the line between king and priest — a deliberate theological statement about David’s liturgical role.
