1 Chronicles 6

The Levites — Priests, Musicians, and Cities

Verses 1–30 — Levitical Genealogy; Aaron’s Priestly Line
123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627282930

Context

The Levitical genealogy is the longest in Chronicles (81 verses) — reflecting the Chronicler’s core conviction that worship is Israel’s primary vocation. The line of Aaron is traced from Levi through Aaron to Zadok and beyond, establishing the legitimate priestly succession. For the post-exilic community, this genealogy answered a critical question: which families have the right to serve as priests in the rebuilt temple?

Cross-Reference

  • Exod 6:16–25The Levitical genealogy in Exodus provides the foundation. The Chronicler extends and expands it with additional generations.
  • Ezra 7:1–5Ezra traces his own priestly ancestry through the same line — from Aaron through Zadok. The genealogies in Chronicles and Ezra serve the same legitimating function.

Calvin’s Commentary

John Calvin, Commentaries — Faithful Paraphrase
6:1

The detailed priestly genealogy teaches that access to God is not casual but ordered. God established a specific line of mediators — a principle fulfilled ultimately in Christ, the great High Priest.

NET Bible Notes

NET Bible Full Notes Edition — Biblical Studies Press
6:10

The parenthetical about Azariah serving in Solomon’s temple is a chronological anchor. Some scholars identify this as Azariah I who resisted Uzziah’s attempt to burn incense (2 Chr 26:17).

Verses 31–81 — Temple Musicians; Levitical Cities

Context

David appointed musicians for the house of the LORD (v.31) — the Chronicler traces the genealogies of the three chief musicians: Heman (Kohathite), Asaph (Gershonite), and Ethan/Jeduthun (Merarite). Each one’s ancestry is traced back through the Levitical line to Levi himself. Then the 48 Levitical cities are listed, distributed across all twelve tribal territories (cf. Joshua 21). The musicians and the cities together represent the Chronicler’s vision: worship is central to Israel, and the Levites who serve it are distributed throughout the entire land.

Cross-Reference

  • Josh 21:1–42The Levitical cities allocated by Joshua. The Chronicler’s list closely follows Joshua 21, confirming the tradition’s stability.
  • 1 Chr 25:1–7The musicians will be formally organised by David in chapter 25. Here their genealogical credentials are established.

Calvin’s Commentary

John Calvin, Commentaries — Faithful Paraphrase
6:31

David’s appointment of musicians teaches that worship is not spontaneous but ordered. God deserves not just our hearts but our best art, organised with care and executed with skill.

NET Bible Notes

NET Bible Full Notes Edition — Biblical Studies Press
6:31

The phrase “after the ark came to rest” refers to the period between the ark’s arrival in Jerusalem (ch 15–16) and the temple’s construction. Music ministry bridged the transitional period.
