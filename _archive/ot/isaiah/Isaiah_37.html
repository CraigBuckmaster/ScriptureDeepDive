Isaiah 37

The Angel of the LORD Strikes 185,000

Verses 1–20 — “Hezekiah Spreads the Letter Before the LORD”
1234567891011121314151617181920

Hebrew Word Study

פָּרַשׂ pāras — to spread out: "Hezekiah received the letter and read it. Then he went up to the temple and spread it out (pāras) before the LORD" (v.14). Hezekiah tears his clothes, puts on sackcloth, goes to the temple, and sends for Isaiah (vv.1–2). Isaiah’s response: "Do not be afraid. This is what the LORD says: I will put a spirit in him so that he will hear a report and return to his own country" (vv.6–7). Sennacherib sends a taunting letter (vv.10–13). Hezekiah’s response is the model prayer of crisis: he physically spreads the letter before God’s face (v.14). His prayer: "You alone are God over all kingdoms. Give ear and hear; open your eyes and see" (vv.16, 17). "Deliver us so that all kingdoms may know that you alone, LORD, are God" (v.20).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
37:1–2

"Hezekiah tore his clothes and put on sackcloth and went into the temple." The right response to crisis: mourning, humility, and the house of God. Not the war room but the temple.

37:14–15

"Hezekiah spread it out before the LORD. And Hezekiah prayed." The physical act: unrolling the threatening letter in God’s presence. The most vivid prayer-posture in the OT.

37:20

"Deliver us so that all kingdoms may know that you alone are God." The prayer’s climax: not "save us for our sake" but "save us so the world knows YOU."

Verses 21–38 — “The Angel of the LORD Put to Death 185,000”
212223242526272829303132333435363738

Hebrew Word Study

מַלְאַךְ malʾak̅ — angel / messenger: "Then the angel of the LORD (malʾak̅) went out and put to death 185,000 in the Assyrian camp" (v.36). Isaiah sends word to Hezekiah: God has heard. His taunt-song against Sennacherib: "The Virgin Daughter Zion despises you and mocks you" (v.22). "Whom have you ridiculed and blasphemed? Against whom have you raised your voice? Against the Holy One of Israel!" (v.23). God will put his hook in Sennacherib’s nose and turn him back (v.29). The sign: this year eat what grows of itself; next year the same; in the third year, plant and harvest (v.30). A remnant will take root and bear fruit (v.31). Then: the angel strikes. 185,000 dead in one night (v.36). Sennacherib returns to Nineveh and is murdered by his own sons (v.38).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
37:22–23

"The Virgin Daughter Zion despises you and mocks you. Whom have you ridiculed? The Holy One of Israel!" God’s taunt-song: Assyria’s mockery is redirected. The virgin daughter LAUGHS at the empire.

37:29

"I will put my hook in your nose and my bit in your mouth, and I will make you return by the way you came." God as animal-handler: the empire is livestock. The hook-in-nose imagery is Assyria’s own treatment of prisoners turned back on them.

37:36

"The angel of the LORD put to death 185,000 in the Assyrian camp." One angel. One night. 185,000. The deliverance Isaiah promised through 30 chapters of preaching. Sennacherib’s own records confirm his withdrawal without conquering Jerusalem.

37:38

"His sons killed him with the sword." Sennacherib murdered by his own children. The man who threatened God’s children is destroyed by his own.
