Isaiah 24

The Isaiah Apocalypse Begins: The Earth Devastated

Verses 1–13 — “The Earth Is Defiled by Its People”
12345678910111213

Hebrew Word Study

בָּלַק bālaq — to devastate / to empty / to lay waste: "See, the LORD is going to lay waste (bālaq) the earth and devastate it" (v.1). THE Isaiah Apocalypse (chs.24–27) — cosmic judgment beyond any single nation. The earth is emptied, scattered, stripped (v.1). Priest and people, master and servant, buyer and seller — all alike (v.2). The earth dries up; the world languishes (v.4). WHY? "The earth is defiled by its people; they have disobeyed the laws, violated the statutes and broken the everlasting covenant" (v.5). A CURSE consumes the earth (v.6). Joy gone: wine dries up, merry-making silenced, songs stopped, beer bitter (vv.7–11). The city of chaos is desolate (v.10). "Only a few are left, as when an olive tree is beaten" (v.13) — the remnant metaphor again.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
24:1–3

"The LORD is going to lay waste the earth. It will be the same for priest as for people, for master as for servant." No exceptions. No class immunity. Universal devastation.

24:5–6

"The earth is defiled by its people; they have broken the everlasting covenant. Therefore a curse consumes the earth." The "everlasting covenant" — probably the Noahic covenant (Gen 9). Humanity has violated the most basic creation-order. The curse of Genesis 3 reaches its culmination.

24:10–13

"The ruined city lies desolate. Gladness is banished from the earth. Only a few are left." The city of chaos (tohu — the same word as Genesis 1:2). Creation is un-created. But: a remnant remains.

Verses 14–23 — “The LORD Will Reign on Mount Zion”
14151617181920212223

Hebrew Word Study

מָלַךְ mālak̅ — he reigns / he has become king: "The LORD Almighty will reign (mālak̅) on Mount Zion and in Jerusalem, and before its elders — with great glory" (v.23). From the west the remnant raises their voices and shouts for joy (v.14). From the east: glory to the Righteous One (v.16). But Isaiah says: "Woe to me! The treacherous betray!" (v.16). Terror, pit, and snare await the inhabitants (v.17). The floodgates of the heavens open; the foundations of the earth shake (v.18) — the flood-imagery returns. The earth reels like a drunkard, sways like a hut in the wind (v.20). "In that day the LORD will punish the powers in the heavens above and the kings on the earth below" (v.21). Moon ashamed, sun disgraced — because the LORD’s glory outshines them (v.23).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
24:16

"From the ends of the earth we hear singing: ‘Glory to the Righteous One.’ But I said, ‘I waste away! Woe to me!’" The global praise is real AND the prophet’s anguish is real. Both coexist. The treachery hasn’t ended.

24:19–20

"The earth is broken up, the earth is split asunder, the earth is violently shaken. The earth reels like a drunkard." Three verbs of breaking; one simile of drunkenness. The planet itself is destabilised.

24:21–23

"The LORD will punish the powers in the heavens above and the kings on the earth below. The moon will be dismayed, the sun ashamed, when the LORD reigns on Mount Zion." Two-tier judgment: heavenly and earthly. Then God’s reign outshines sun and moon. Rev 21:23 develops this.
