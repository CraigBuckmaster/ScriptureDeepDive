Isaiah 48

Come Out of Babylon!

Verses 1–11 — “For My Own Name’s Sake I Delay My Wrath”
1234567891011

Hebrew Word Study

שֵׁם shēm — name / reputation: "For my own name’s sake (shēm) I delay my wrath; for the sake of my praise I hold it back from you, so as not to destroy you completely" (v.9). God recounts Israel’s stubbornness: "I knew how treacherous you were; you were called a rebel from birth" (v.8). He predicted events in advance specifically because Israel would credit idols otherwise (vv.3–5). "I have refined you, though not as silver; I have tested you in the furnace of affliction" (v.10). "For my own sake, for my own sake, I do this. How can I let myself be defamed?" (v.11). God’s motivation: his own name, his own glory, his own reputation. Not Israel’s merit.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
48:3–5

"I foretold the former things long ago. I told you before they happened so that you could not say, ‘My idol did them.’" Prophecy as anti-idolatry: God predicts to prevent misattribution.

48:10–11

"I have refined you in the furnace of affliction. For my own sake, for my own sake, I do this." Exile as furnace. Salvation for God’s sake — doubled for emphasis. This is the theology of Ezekiel 36:22 too.

Verses 12–22 — “Go Out from Babylon! Flee from the Chaldeans!”
1213141516171819202122

Hebrew Word Study

צְאוּ ṣəʾū — go out! / come out!: "Leave Babylon, flee (ṣəʾū) from the Babylonians! Announce this with shouts of joy and proclaim it. Send it out to the ends of the earth; say, ‘The LORD has redeemed his servant Jacob!’" (v.20). "I am the first and I am the last" (v.12) — repeated from 44:6. "My own hand laid the foundations of the earth, and my right hand spread out the heavens" (v.13). God called Cyrus; God will fulfil his purpose against Babylon (v.15). "Come near me and listen: from the first announcement I have not spoken in secret; at the time it happens, I am there" (v.16). Then the lament: "If only you had paid attention to my commands, your peace would have been like a river, your well-being like the waves of the sea" (v.18). "There is no peace for the wicked" (v.22) — the refrain that divides Second Isaiah into three sections (48:22; 57:21; ending implied at 66:24).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
48:16

"From the first announcement I have not spoken in secret." God’s revelation is public, historical, verifiable. Not whispered in mystery temples but declared openly.

48:18

"If only you had paid attention! Your peace would have been like a river." The conditional lament: God imagines what could have been. River-peace and wave-wellbeing — the loss is immeasurable.

48:20–22

"Leave Babylon! Announce it with shouts of joy! The LORD has redeemed his servant Jacob!" The exodus command: leave now. Shout while you go. But: "There is no peace for the wicked." The section-divider: peace for the redeemed, none for the wicked.
