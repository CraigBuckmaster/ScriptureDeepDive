Isaiah 64

We Are the Clay, You Are the Potter

Verses 1–7 — “Oh, That You Would Rend the Heavens and Come Down!”
1234567

Hebrew Word Study

קָרַע qāraʿ — to tear / to rend: "Oh, that you would rend (qāraʿ) the heavens and come down, that the mountains would tremble before you!" (v.1). The great cry: tear open the sky! Come down! "When you did awesome things that we did not expect, you came down, and the mountains trembled" (v.3). "Since ancient times no one has heard, no ear has perceived, no eye has seen any God besides you, who acts on behalf of those who wait for him" (v.4) — Paul quotes this in 1 Corinthians 2:9 for what God has prepared. "All of us have become like one who is unclean, and all our righteous acts are like filthy rags; we all shrivel up like a leaf" (v.6). Righteous acts as filthy rags: the most devastating self-assessment in the OT.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
64:1

"Oh, that you would rend the heavens and come down!" The cry for theophany. Mark 1:10: at Jesus baptism, the heavens are "torn open" (schizo) — the same verb. God answers Isaiah’s prayer at the Jordan.

64:4

"No eye has seen, no ear has heard any God like you." 1 Cor 2:9: Paul applies this to the Spirit’s revelation. The incomparability of God extended to what he prepares for those who love him.

64:6

"All our righteous acts are like filthy rags." Not sins — righteous acts. Even the best human effort is contaminated. The total-depravity text of the OT.

Verses 8–12 — “We Are the Clay; You Are the Potter”
89101112

Hebrew Word Study

יוֹצֵר yōṣēr — potter / former: "Yet you, LORD, are our Father. We are the clay; you are the potter (yōṣēr); we are all the work of your hand" (v.8). The potter-clay metaphor reaches its fullest expression. Jeremiah 18 develops it; Romans 9:21 quotes it. "Do not be angry beyond measure, LORD; do not remember our sins forever" (v.9). "Your sacred cities have become a wasteland. Our holy and glorious temple has been burned with fire" (vv.10–11). "After all this, LORD, will you hold yourself back? Will you keep silent and punish us beyond measure?" (v.12). The book’s second-to-last chapter ends with an unanswered question — like Lamentations 5:22. The silence demands response.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
64:8

"We are the clay; you are the potter; we are all the work of your hand." The appeal: we belong to you because you made us. The potter-clay is not resignation but intimacy. We are YOUR handiwork.

64:10–11

"Your holy temple, where our ancestors praised you, has been burned with fire." The destruction is personal: YOUR temple, OUR ancestors. Shared loss.

64:12

"After all this, will you hold yourself back?" The chapter ends pleading. No answer comes in ch.64. The answer arrives in ch.65–66: new heavens and new earth.
