Isaiah 30

Woe to the Obstinate Children

Verses 1–17 — “In Repentance and Rest Is Your Salvation”
1234567891011121314151617

Hebrew Word Study

שׁוּבָה shūbâ — return / repentance: "In repentance (shūbâ) and rest is your salvation, in quietness and trust is your strength, but you would have none of it" (v.15). Woe to the obstinate children who carry out plans not from God, who go down to Egypt for help without consulting the LORD (vv.1–2). Egypt’s help is utterly useless (v.7). They tell the prophets: "Stop confronting us with the Holy One of Israel!" (v.11). They reject the word and trust in oppression and deceit (v.12). Their wall will collapse suddenly, in an instant — shattered like pottery (vv.13–14). Then v.15 — one of the most important verses in Isaiah: salvation is in returning and resting, strength is in quietness and trusting. But they refused.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
30:1–2

"Woe to the obstinate children who carry out plans that are not mine. They go down to Egypt for help." The political sin: trusting Egypt’s military instead of God’s promise.

30:10–11

"Tell us pleasant things, prophesy illusions. Stop confronting us with the Holy One of Israel!" The demand for comfortable preaching. The refusal to hear truth.

30:15

"In repentance and rest is your salvation, in quietness and trust is your strength, but you would have none of it." The prescription: return, rest, be quiet, trust. The diagnosis: you refused. The four verbs are passive — they require stopping, not striving.

Verses 18–33 — “The LORD Longs to Be Gracious to You”
18192021222324252627282930313233

Hebrew Word Study

חָנַן ḥānan — to be gracious / to show favour: "Yet the LORD longs to be gracious (ḥānan) to you; therefore he will rise up to show you compassion" (v.18). After the refusal of v.15, God STILL longs to be gracious. The bread of adversity and the water of affliction will come, but "your Teacher will be hidden no more; your eyes will see your Teacher" (v.20). "Whether you turn to the right or to the left, your ears will hear a voice behind you, saying, ‘This is the way; walk in it’" (v.21). The guidance promise: a voice BEHIND you — following, not leading from a distance. Idols will be discarded (v.22). Rain and abundant harvest (vv.23–25). The moon will shine like the sun, and the sun seven times brighter — "when the LORD binds up the bruises of his people" (v.26).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
30:18

"The LORD longs to be gracious to you; he rises to show you compassion." God LONGS. The verb is ḥākah — waiting eagerly, not passively. God is impatient to show mercy.

30:20–21

"Your Teacher will be hidden no more. Your ears will hear a voice behind you: This is the way; walk in it." The guidance verse. A voice BEHIND — like a shepherd following the flock, not a general commanding from the front.

30:26

"The moon will shine like the sun, and the sunlight will be seven times brighter, when the LORD binds up the bruises of his people." Cosmic healing: light multiplied. The binding of bruises produces a seven-fold sun.
