Isaiah 50

The Third Servant Song: I Offered My Back to Those Who Beat Me

Verses 1–3 — “Where Is Your Mother’s Certificate of Divorce?”
123

Hebrew Word Study

כְּרִיתוּת kərîtūṭ — certificate of divorce: "Where is your mother’s certificate of divorce (kərîtūṭ) with which I sent her away? To which of my creditors did I sell you?" (v.1). God denies divorce and debt-sale: Israel was not divorced or sold; they were sent away for their own sins (v.1). "Was my arm too short to deliver you? Do I lack the strength to rescue you?" (v.2). The arm that dried up the sea and turned rivers to desert is not shortened (vv.2–3). The exile is not abandonment; it’s discipline within an unbroken marriage.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
50:1

"Where is the certificate of divorce?" God demands the legal document. There is none. The marriage covenant stands. Exile is separation, not divorce.

50:2–3

"Was my arm too short to deliver? I dry up the sea; I clothe the heavens with darkness." The arm-of-the-LORD theme: the power that parted the Red Sea has not weakened.

Verses 4–11 — “I Offered My Back to Those Who Beat Me”
4567891011

Hebrew Word Study

לִמּוּדִים limmūdîm — disciples / those who are taught: "The Sovereign LORD has given me a well-instructed (limmūdîm) tongue, to know the word that sustains the weary" (v.4). THE THIRD SERVANT SONG (50:4–9). The servant is a learner: "He wakens me morning by morning, wakens my ear to listen like one being instructed" (v.4). Then the cost: "I offered my back to those who beat me, my cheeks to those who pulled out my beard; I did not hide my face from mocking and spitting" (v.6). The Passion narrative before the Passion: Jesus’ flogging, beard-pulling, spitting (Matt 26:67; 27:26, 30). "Because the Sovereign LORD helps me, I will not be disgraced. I have set my face like flint" (v.7). Luke 9:51: "Jesus resolutely set his face to go to Jerusalem" — echoing this verse.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
50:4

"He wakens me morning by morning, wakens my ear to listen." The servant’s daily discipline: morning listening. The tongue that sustains the weary comes from the ear that listens first.

50:6

"I offered my back to those who beat me, my cheeks to those who pulled out my beard; I did not hide my face from mocking and spitting." The Passion foretold: beating, beard-pulling, spitting. Matt 26:67; 27:26, 30 fulfil this verse-by-verse.

50:7–9

"I have set my face like flint. It is the Sovereign LORD who helps me. Who will condemn me?" Luke 9:51: Jesus "set his face" to go to Jerusalem. Romans 8:33–34: "Who will bring any charge? Who is the one who condemns?" — Paul’s questions echo the servant’s confidence.

50:10–11

"Let the one who walks in the dark and has no light trust in the name of the LORD. But you who light fires and provide yourselves with flaming torches — walk in the light of your fires! This is what you shall receive from my hand: you will lie down in torment." Two paths: trust God’s unseen light or light your own fire. Self-illumination leads to torment.
