Isaiah 29

Woe to Ariel: The Overturned Wisdom

Verses 1–14 — “These People Honour Me with Their Lips, but Their Hearts Are Far from Me”
1234567891011121314

Hebrew Word Study

אֲרִיאֵל ʿarîʾēl — Ariel / lion of God / altar-hearth: "Woe to you, Ariel (ʿarîʾēl), Ariel, the city where David settled!" (v.1). Ariel = Jerusalem (the altar-hearth of God). God will besiege his own city (v.3). But then enemies who attack it will vanish like a dream (vv.7–8). The people are in a spiritual stupor: "The LORD has brought over you a deep sleep" (v.10). Sealed scrolls no one can read (vv.11–12). Then the devastating quote Jesus uses most against the Pharisees: "These people come near to me with their mouth and honour me with their lips, but their hearts are far from me. Their worship of me is based on merely human rules taught by men" (v.13). Jesus quotes this in Matthew 15:8–9 and Mark 7:6–7. God will proceed to do "a wonder among these people" (v.14) — the wisdom of the wise will perish.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
29:1–4

"Woe to Ariel!" God besieges Jerusalem — then rescues it. The double movement: discipline and deliverance.

29:10–12

"The LORD has brought a deep sleep. The scroll is sealed." Spiritual blindness: the vision is right in front of them but no one can read it. The literate say "it’s sealed"; the illiterate say "I can’t read."

29:13

"These people honour me with their lips, but their hearts are far from me." Jesus’ primary weapon against the Pharisees (Matt 15:8–9; Mark 7:6–7). Lip-worship without heart-worship is Isaiah’s diagnosis of religious hypocrisy.

29:14

"The wisdom of the wise will perish, and the intelligence of the intelligent will vanish." Paul quotes this in 1 Corinthians 1:19 — God’s foolishness wiser than human wisdom.

Verses 15–24 — “The Deaf Will Hear; the Blind Will See”
15161718192021222324

Hebrew Word Study

חֵרֵשׁ ḥērēsh — deaf: "In that day the deaf (ḥērēsh) will hear the words of the scroll, and out of gloom and darkness the eyes of the blind will see" (v.18). Woe to those who hide their plans from God, who work in the dark and say "Who sees us?" (v.15). The potter/clay reversal: "Can the pot say to the potter, ‘You know nothing’?" (v.16) — Paul echoes this in Romans 9:20–21. But then hope: Lebanon will become a fertile field (v.17). The deaf hear, the blind see (v.18). The humble rejoice in the Holy One of Israel (v.19). The ruthless vanish (v.20). Those who erred will gain understanding (v.24). Reversal upon reversal: deafness → hearing, blindness → seeing, error → understanding.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
29:16

"Shall what is formed say to the one who formed it, ‘You did not make me’? Can the pot say of the potter, ‘You know nothing’?" Paul develops this in Romans 9:20–21. The creature cannot evaluate the Creator.

29:18–19

"The deaf will hear the words of the scroll; the blind will see. The humble will rejoice in the Holy One of Israel." The reversal: the sealed scroll of v.11 becomes readable. The spiritual blindness of v.10 is healed. Jesus’ ministry enacts this literally (Matt 11:5).

29:24

"Those who are wayward in spirit will gain understanding; those who complain will accept instruction." The chapter ends with learning. Error is not permanent; even the complaining come to understand.
