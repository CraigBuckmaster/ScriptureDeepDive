Isaiah 65

New Heavens and a New Earth

Verses 1–16 — “I Revealed Myself to Those Who Did Not Ask for Me”
12345678910111213141516

Hebrew Word Study

דָרַשׁ dārash — to seek / to inquire: "I revealed myself to those who did not ask (dārash) for me; I was found by those who did not seek me. To a nation that did not call on my name, I said, ‘Here am I, here am I’" (v.1). Paul quotes this in Romans 10:20 for Gentile inclusion. God spread out his hands all day to an obstinate people (v.2). They provoke him with pagan practices: gardens, incense on bricks, graves at night, pig flesh (vv.3–4). "Stand by yourself; don’t come near me, for I am too sacred for you!" (v.5) — arrogant holiness. God will repay (v.6). But a remnant is preserved: "As when juice is still found in a cluster of grapes and people say, ‘Don’t destroy it, there is still a blessing in it’" (v.8). "My servants will eat, but you will go hungry. My servants will rejoice, but you will be put to shame" (v.13). The separation of faithful and unfaithful within Israel.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
65:1

"I revealed myself to those who did not ask for me." Romans 10:20: Paul applies this to the Gentiles. God takes the initiative toward those who never sought him.

65:8

"Don’t destroy it, there is still a blessing in it." The grape-cluster remnant: even in corrupt Israel, juice remains. The remnant theology in agricultural form.

65:13–16

"My servants will eat, you will go hungry. My servants will drink, you will go thirsty." The binary: servants feast, rebels starve. "Whoever invokes a blessing will do so by the God of truth." The new name: God of truth (ʾamen).

Verses 17–25 — “I Will Create New Heavens and a New Earth”
171819202122232425

Hebrew Word Study

בָרָא bārāʾ — to create: "See, I will create (bārāʾ) new heavens and a new earth. The former things will not be remembered, nor will they come to mind" (v.17). Revelation 21:1: "Then I saw a new heaven and a new earth" — drawn directly from this verse. "I will create Jerusalem to be a delight and its people a joy" (v.18). "Never again will there be an infant who lives but a few days" (v.20). "They will build houses and dwell in them; they will plant vineyards and eat their fruit" (v.21). No more stolen labour: "they will not build and others inhabit; they will not plant and others eat" (v.22). "Before they call I will answer; while they are still speaking I will hear" (v.24). "The wolf and the lamb will feed together. They will neither harm nor destroy on all my holy mountain" (v.25) — the 11:6–9 vision returns. The answer to 64:12: God will not hold back. He will create everything new.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
65:17

"I will create new heavens and a new earth." bārāʾ — the creation-verb of Genesis 1:1. Not renovation but new creation. Rev 21:1 quotes this. The former things: forgotten.

65:20–22

"No infant who lives but a few days. They will build houses and dwell in them." The new creation is concrete: long life, secure homes, no stolen labour. Not abstract heaven but renewed earth.

65:24

"Before they call I will answer." The ultimate intimacy: God responds before the prayer is spoken. The gap between need and supply is eliminated.

65:25

"The wolf and the lamb will feed together." The 11:6–9 peace-kingdom returns. The predator-prey reconciliation. "They will neither harm nor destroy on all my holy mountain." The last echo of the holy mountain vision.
