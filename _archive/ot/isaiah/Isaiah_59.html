Isaiah 59

Your Sins Have Hidden His Face from You

Verses 1–15 — “Your Iniquities Have Separated You from Your God”
123456789101112131415

Hebrew Word Study

עָוֹן ʿāwōn — iniquity / guilt: "Surely the arm of the LORD is not too short to save, nor his ear too dull to hear. But your iniquities (ʿāwōn) have separated you from your God; your sins have hidden his face from you" (vv.1–2). The problem is not God power but human sin. Hands stained with blood, lips speak lies (v.3). Justice is far away; righteousness does not reach us (v.9). "We grope along the wall like the blind" (v.10). "Truth has stumbled in the streets; honesty cannot enter" (v.14). The catalogue of social collapse: lies, injustice, violence, blindness, stumbling truth. "The LORD looked and was displeased that there was no justice. He saw that there was no one; he was appalled that there was no one to intervene" (vv.15–16).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
59:1–2

"The arm of the LORD is not too short. But your iniquities have separated you." The diagnosis: not divine weakness but human sin. The arm (51:9) is strong; the barrier is moral, not mechanical.

59:9–11

"Justice is far away. We grope like the blind. We look for light, but all is darkness." The confession shifts to first person: WE grope, WE growl. The community owns its blindness.

59:14–16

"Truth has stumbled in the streets. The LORD saw that there was no one to intervene." No human intercessor available. The vacancy sets up the divine warrior of v.16–17.

Verses 16–21 — “He Put on Righteousness as a Breastplate”
161718192021

Hebrew Word Study

גֹאֵל gōʾēl — redeemer / kinsman-redeemer: "The Redeemer (gōʾēl) will come to Zion, to those in Jacob who repent of their sins" (v.20). No human intervenes, so God arms HIMSELF: righteousness as breastplate, salvation as helmet, vengeance as clothing, zeal as cloak (v.17). Paul adapts this for the believer armour in Ephesians 6:14–17. "From the west, people will fear the name of the LORD, and from the rising of the sun, they will revere his glory. For he will come like a pent-up flood that the breath of the LORD drives along" (v.19). Then the Redeemer comes to Zion (v.20). Paul quotes v.20 in Romans 11:26: "The deliverer will come from Zion." God covenant: "My Spirit, who is on you, will not depart from you, and my words that I have put in your mouth will always be on your lips, and on the lips of your children, and on the lips of their descendants, from this time on and forever" (v.21).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
59:16–17

"He saw that there was no one. So his own arm achieved salvation. He put on righteousness as a breastplate, salvation as a helmet." God arms himself because no human could. Ephesians 6:14–17 adapts the armour for believers.

59:20

"The Redeemer will come to Zion." Romans 11:26: "The deliverer will come from Zion; he will turn godlessness away from Jacob." Paul key verse for Israel future salvation comes from Isaiah 59.

59:21

"My Spirit on you will not depart, and my words in your mouth will not depart — from now and forever." The permanent covenant: Spirit and word, generation after generation. The guarantee that the prophetic word will never be silenced.
