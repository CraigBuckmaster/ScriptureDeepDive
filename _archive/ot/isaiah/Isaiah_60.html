Isaiah 60

Arise, Shine, for Your Light Has Come!

Verses 1–14 — “Nations Will Come to Your Light”
1234567891011121314

Hebrew Word Study

אוֹר ʾōr — light: "Arise, shine, for your light (ʾōr) has come, and the glory of the LORD rises upon you. See, darkness covers the earth and thick darkness is over the peoples, but the LORD rises upon you and his glory appears over you. Nations will come to your light, and kings to the brightness of your dawn" (vv.1–3). THE Epiphany text. Darkness covers the earth; light breaks over Zion; nations stream toward it. Camels from Midian and Ephah, gold and incense from Sheba (v.6) — Matthew 2:11 (the Magi bringing gold and frankincense) fulfils this. "Your gates will always stand open, they will never be shut, day or night" (v.11). The sons of those who oppressed you will bow at your feet (v.14). Revelation 21:24–26 develops vv.3, 5, 11 for the new Jerusalem.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
60:1–3

"Arise, shine! Nations will come to your light, kings to the brightness of your dawn." The Epiphany: light in darkness, nations drawn. The verbs are imperatives: get up, glow.

60:6

"Herds of camels will cover your land. They will bring gold and incense." Matt 2:11: the Magi bring gold and frankincense. The fulfilment is specific.

60:11

"Your gates will always stand open." Rev 21:25: "On no day will its gates ever be shut." Isaiah blueprint, Revelation fulfilment.

60:14

"The sons of your oppressors will come bowing. They will call you the City of the LORD." The reversal: persecutors become worshippers.

Verses 15–22 — “The LORD Will Be Your Everlasting Light”
1516171819202122

Hebrew Word Study

עוֹלָם ʿōlām — forever / everlasting: "The sun will no more be your light by day, nor will the brightness of the moon shine on you, for the LORD will be your everlasting (ʿōlām) light, and your God will be your glory" (v.19). Revelation 21:23: "The city does not need the sun or the moon to shine on it, for the glory of God gives it light." Sun and moon replaced by divine glory. "Your days of sorrow will end" (v.20). "Then all your people will be righteous and they will possess the land forever" (v.21). "The least of you will become a thousand, the smallest a mighty nation. I am the LORD; in its time I will do this swiftly" (v.22).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
60:19–20

"The LORD will be your everlasting light. Your sun will never set again; your days of sorrow will end." Rev 21:23; 22:5 draw directly from this. No more sun needed because God IS the light.

60:22

"The least will become a thousand, the smallest a mighty nation. In its time I will do this swiftly." The Abraham-promise (Gen 12:2) fulfilled cosmically. Swift in God timing.
