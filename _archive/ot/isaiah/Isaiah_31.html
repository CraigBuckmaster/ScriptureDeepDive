Isaiah 31

Woe to Those Who Go Down to Egypt

Verses 1–5 — “The Egyptians Are Mortals, Not God; Their Horses Are Flesh, Not Spirit”
12345

Hebrew Word Study

בָשָׂר bāśār — flesh: "Woe to those who go down to Egypt for help, who rely on horses. But the Egyptians are mortals (adām), not God (ʾēl); their horses are flesh (bāśār), not spirit (rūaḥ)" (v.3). The sharpest formulation of the Egypt-trust critique. Two contrasts: mortal vs God, flesh vs spirit. "Like birds hovering overhead, the LORD Almighty will shield Jerusalem; he will shield it and deliver it, he will pass over it and will rescue it" (v.5). The “passing over” echoes Passover (Ex 12). God as mother-bird: hovering, protecting.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
31:1

"Woe to those who go down to Egypt for help, who rely on horses." The fourth time Isaiah condemns the Egypt-alliance. Horses = military technology. Trusting technology instead of theology.

31:3

"The Egyptians are mortals, not God; their horses are flesh, not spirit." The binary that defines Isaiah’s theology: flesh vs spirit, mortal vs divine. All political calculation reduces to this.

31:5

"Like birds hovering overhead, the LORD will shield Jerusalem; he will pass over and rescue it." The Passover verb: pāsaḥ — pass over. God as mother-bird: wings spread, hovering, protecting.

Verses 6–9 — “Return to the One You Have So Greatly Revolted Against”
6789

Hebrew Word Study

שׁוּבוּ shūbū — return! / repent!: "Return (shūbū), you Israelites, to the One your hands have so greatly revolted against" (v.6). The call to return — the imperative form of the verb that gives ch.30:15 its theology. Throw away your idols of silver and gold (v.7). Assyria will fall by a sword not of mortals (v.8) — "not by human" echoes Daniel 2:34 (the stone cut without hands) and 8:25 (destroyed not by human power). God’s furnace is in Zion (v.9). The fire is his, in Jerusalem.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
31:6–7

"Return to the One you have revolted against. In that day each of you will reject the idols of silver and gold your sinful hands have made." Repentance produces idol-disposal. You cannot return to God and keep the alternatives.

31:8–9

"Assyria will fall by a sword that is not of man. His stronghold will fall because of terror." Not-by-man: the divine pattern. God’s defeats of empires require no human army.
