Isaiah 47

The Fall of Babylon

Verses 1–7 — “Sit in the Dust, Virgin Daughter Babylon”
1234567

Hebrew Word Study

יָרַד yārad — to descend / to go down: "Go down (yārad), sit in the dust, Virgin Daughter Babylon; sit on the ground without a throne" (v.1). Babylon personified as a dethroned queen forced into slavery. "Take millstones and grind flour; take off your veil. Lift up your skirts, bare your legs, wade through the streams" (v.2). From throne to millstone — the reversal is total. "I was angry with my people and desecrated my inheritance; I gave them into your hand, and you showed them no mercy. Even on the aged you laid a very heavy yoke" (v.6). God used Babylon but Babylon exceeded the mandate: no mercy shown.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
47:1–3

"Sit in the dust. Take millstones and grind flour." The queen becomes a slave. The most vivid reversal in Second Isaiah.

47:6

"I was angry with my people; I gave them into your hand, and you showed them no mercy." God sent Babylon as judgment but Babylon overplayed. The same logic as Assyria in 10:5–15: the instrument exceeds its commission.

Verses 8–15 — “I Am, and There Is None Besides Me”
89101112131415

Hebrew Word Study

שׁוֹאָה shōʾâ — disaster / catastrophe: "Disaster (shōʾâ) will come upon you, and you will not know how to conjure it away" (v.11). Babylon says "I am, and there is none besides me" (v.8) — stealing God’s own claim (45:5–6). "I will never be a widow or suffer the loss of children" (v.8). Both will come in a single day (v.9). "You have trusted in your wickedness and said, ‘No one sees me.’ Your wisdom and knowledge mislead you" (v.10). Let the astrologers, stargazers, and monthly prognosticators save you (v.13) — they cannot. They are stubble; the fire burns them (v.14). "Each of them goes on in his error; there is not one that can save you" (v.15). Revelation 18 develops this chapter extensively for eschatological Babylon.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
47:8–10

"I am, and there is none besides me." Babylon quotes God’s words. The empire claims divine status. "No one sees me" — the delusion of impunity.

47:11–14

"Disaster will come. Keep on with your sorceries! Let your astrologers save you! They are stubble; the fire burns them." The occult arts that made Babylon famous are useless. The most anti-astrology passage in the Bible.

47:15

"There is not one that can save you." The final verdict: alone, unsaved, unsaveable. Rev 18:2–19 builds the eschatological Babylon’s fall directly on this chapter.
