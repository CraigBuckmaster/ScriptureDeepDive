Isaiah 55

Come, All You Who Are Thirsty

Verses 1–5 — “Come, Buy Without Money”
12345

Hebrew Word Study

לָכוּ ləkū — come!: "Come (ləkū), all you who are thirsty, come to the waters; and you who have no money, come, buy and eat! Come, buy wine and milk without money and without cost" (v.1). THE great invitation. Free grace: no money, no cost. "Why spend money on what is not bread, and your labour on what does not satisfy?" (v.2). "Give ear and come to me; listen, that you may live. I will make an everlasting covenant with you, my faithful love promised to David" (v.3). The Davidic covenant extended to "all who are thirsty" — not just kings but anyone. "See, I have made him a witness to the peoples, a ruler and commander of the peoples" (v.4). Jesus at the feast: "Let anyone who is thirsty come to me" (John 7:37) — echoing Isaiah 55:1.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
55:1

"Come, all you who are thirsty, come to the waters. Come, buy without money." The triple "come": invitation, not command. Free: no price, no prerequisite, no qualification. John 7:37: Jesus echoes this at the Feast of Tabernacles.

55:2–3

"Why spend money on what is not bread? I will make an everlasting covenant with you." The economics of grace: you are spending everything on nothing; God offers everything for nothing. The Davidic covenant democratised: available to all.

55:4–5

"I have made him a witness to the peoples." The Davidic figure is witness, ruler, commander — but to ALL peoples. Nations you do not know will come running.

Verses 6–13 — “My Word Will Not Return to Me Empty”
678910111213

Hebrew Word Study

דָבָר dābār — word: "So is my word (dābār) that goes out from my mouth: it will not return to me empty, but will accomplish what I desire and achieve the purpose for which I sent it" (v.11). "Seek the LORD while he may be found; call on him while he is near" (v.6). The urgency: there is a window. "Let the wicked forsake their ways and the unrighteous their thoughts" (v.7). "For my thoughts are not your thoughts, neither are your ways my ways" (v.8). "As the heavens are higher than the earth, so are my ways higher than your ways" (v.9). The height-gap between human and divine thought. Then the rain-and-snow metaphor: as rain waters the earth and makes it produce, so God word goes out and accomplishes its purpose (vv.10–11). "You will go out in joy and be led forth in peace; the mountains and hills will burst into song, and all the trees of the field will clap their hands" (v.12). Nature celebrates the redeemed. Trees applaud.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
55:6–7

"Seek the LORD while he may be found." The conditional "while" — there is a time to seek. The window is open now. "Let the wicked forsake their ways." Repentance before the window closes.

55:8–9

"My thoughts are not your thoughts. As the heavens are higher than the earth, so are my ways higher." The height-gap: God incomprehensibility is not frustrating but liberating. You cannot predict or contain him.

55:10–11

"As the rain comes down and waters the earth, so is my word: it will not return empty." The word as rain: it falls, it soaks, it produces. It does not evaporate. The confidence of preaching: God word achieves its purpose regardless of human response.

55:12

"The mountains will burst into song, and the trees will clap their hands." The cosmic celebration: nature personified as worshipping. The exodus-hymn applied to creation itself.
