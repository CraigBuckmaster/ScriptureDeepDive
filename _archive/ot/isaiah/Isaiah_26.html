Isaiah 26

A Song of Trust: The Dead Will Live

Verses 1–11 — “You Will Keep in Perfect Peace Those Whose Minds Are Steadfast”
1234567891011

Hebrew Word Study

שָׁלוֹם shālōm — peace / wholeness: "You will keep in perfect peace (shālōm shālōm) those whose minds are steadfast, because they trust in you. Trust in the LORD forever, for the LORD, the LORD himself, is the Rock eternal" (vv.3–4). Shālōm shālōm — doubled for emphasis: peace peace. The condition: a steadfast mind. The cause: trust. The foundation: the eternal Rock. The city of salvation has strong walls; the gates open for the righteous (vv.1–2). The lofty city is brought low to the dust; the feet of the oppressed trample it (v.6). "The path of the righteous is level; you, the Upright One, make the way of the righteous smooth" (v.7). Isaiah’s soul yearns for God in the night (v.9). Even when God’s grace is shown to the wicked, they do not learn righteousness (v.10).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
26:3–4

"Perfect peace to those whose minds are steadfast, because they trust in you. Trust in the LORD forever, for the LORD is the Rock eternal." Doubled shālōm. The most beloved peace-promise in Isaiah. The Rock eternal: ṣūr ʿōlāmîm.

26:7–9

"The path of the righteous is level. My soul yearns for you in the night; in the morning my spirit longs for you." Night-yearning, morning-longing. The same rhythm as Psalm 63. Desire for God across the hours.

26:10

"Though grace is shown to the wicked, they do not learn righteousness." The hardening problem: grace does not automatically produce goodness. Some receive mercy and remain unchanged.

Verses 12–21 — “Your Dead Will Live; Their Bodies Will Rise”
12131415161718192021

Hebrew Word Study

יִחְיוּ yiḥyū — they will live: "But your dead will live (yiḥyū), LORD; their bodies will rise — let those who dwell in the dust wake up and shout for joy — your dew is like the dew of the morning; the earth will give birth to her dead" (v.19). Alongside Daniel 12:2, the OT’s strongest resurrection text. God’s dead LIVE; their bodies RISE; the dust-dwellers WAKE and SHOUT. The dew of morning falls on graves and produces life. The earth in labour, giving birth to the dead. "Go, my people, enter your rooms and shut the doors behind you; hide yourselves for a little while until his wrath has passed by" (v.20) — the Passover-night image: shelter while judgment passes over. Then: "the earth will disclose the blood shed on it; the earth will conceal its slain no longer" (v.21). Every hidden injustice revealed.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
26:12–15

"LORD, you establish peace for us. We acknowledge no God but you. They are now dead, they live no more." The dead tyrants stay dead. The wicked do not rise.

26:19

"Your dead will live; their bodies will rise. Wake up and shout for joy, you who dwell in the dust! Your dew is like the dew of the morning; the earth will give birth to her dead." Resurrection as morning dew: gentle, life-giving, quiet. The earth as a womb delivering the dead into new life.

26:20–21

"Go, my people, enter your rooms and shut the doors behind you. Hide until his wrath has passed. The earth will disclose the blood shed on it." Passover echoes: go inside, shut the door, wait for the destroyer to pass. Then: all hidden blood revealed. No crime stays buried.
