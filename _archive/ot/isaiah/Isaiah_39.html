Isaiah 39

The Babylonian Envoys: The Bridge to Second Isaiah

Verses 1–4 — “Hezekiah Showed Them Everything”
1234

Hebrew Word Study

הֶרְאָה herʾâ — he showed: "Hezekiah was pleased and showed them (herʾâ) all that was in his storehouses — the silver, the gold, the spices, the fine olive oil — his entire armoury and everything found among his treasures" (v.2). Merodach-Baladan of Babylon sends envoys with a gift, ostensibly for Hezekiah’s recovery. But Hezekiah’s response is catastrophic: he shows them EVERYTHING. Isaiah asks: "What did they see?" Hezekiah admits: "They saw everything; there is nothing among my treasures that I did not show them" (v.4). The king who trusted God against Assyria trusts Babylon with his security secrets. The fatal miscalculation that sets up the exile.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
39:1

"Merodach-Baladan sent letters and a gift." Diplomatic courtesy that conceals intelligence-gathering. Babylon is not yet the superpower; it is scouting.

39:2

"Hezekiah showed them everything." The verb is emphatic. The man who spread Sennacherib’s letter before God now spreads his treasures before Babylon. The contrast is devastating.

39:4

"There is nothing among my treasures that I did not show them." Full disclosure to the future enemy. The king who should have learned from ch.37 has already forgotten.

Verses 5–8 — “Everything Will Be Carried Off to Babylon”
5678

Hebrew Word Study

בָּבֶל Bābel — Babylon: "Hear the word of the LORD: the time will surely come when everything in your palace will be carried off to Babylon (Bābel). Nothing will be left. And some of your descendants will be taken away, and they will become eunuchs in the palace of the king of Babylon" (vv.5–7). Isaiah’s verdict: everything you showed them, they will take. The treasure tour becomes the prophecy of exile. Hezekiah’s response is troubling: "‘The word of the LORD you have spoken is good,’ for he thought, ‘There will be peace and security in my lifetime’" (v.8). He accepts the verdict because it falls on his descendants, not on him. First Isaiah closes on this note of failure — setting up the need for the comfort of Second Isaiah (ch.40).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
39:5–7

"Everything will be carried off to Babylon. Some of your descendants will become eunuchs." The exile prophesied. Daniel and his friends (Dan 1) are the fulfilment: royal descendants serving in Babylon’s palace.

39:8

"The word of the LORD is good — there will be peace in MY lifetime." The most disappointing response in the Hezekiah narrative. The king who prayed magnificently in ch.37 now accepts exile because it won’t happen to him. Personal peace traded for generational disaster.
