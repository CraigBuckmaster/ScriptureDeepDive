Isaiah 41

Fear Not, for I Am with You

Verses 1–16 — “I Took You from the Ends of the Earth”
12345678910111213141516

Hebrew Word Study

אַל תִּירָא ʾal tîrāʾ — do not fear: "So do not fear (ʾal tîrāʾ), for I am with you; do not be dismayed, for I am your God. I will strengthen you and help you; I will uphold you with my righteous right hand" (v.10). God summons the nations to court (v.1). Who has stirred up one from the east? (v.2) — Cyrus, though unnamed here. The coastlands saw and feared (v.5). "But you, Israel, my servant, Jacob, whom I have chosen, descendant of Abraham my friend — I took you from the ends of the earth" (vv.8–9). Then the triple assurance: don’t fear (v.10), don’t fear (v.13), don’t fear (v.14). "I will help you. All who rage against you will surely be ashamed. You will thresh the mountains and reduce the hills to chaff" (vv.14–15).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
41:8–9

"But you, Israel, my servant, whom I have chosen, descendant of Abraham my friend." Abraham called God’s friend (cf. James 2:23). Israel’s identity: servant, chosen, friend’s descendant.

41:10

"Do not fear, for I am with you. I will strengthen you and help you; I will uphold you with my righteous right hand." The most-memorised verse in Second Isaiah. Three promises: strengthen, help, uphold. The right hand: power and covenant faithfulness.

41:13–14

"For I am the LORD your God who takes hold of your right hand and says, Do not fear; I will help you. Do not be afraid, you worm Jacob." God holds the right hand. The "worm" is not insult but tender diminishment: even the smallest, weakest creature is held.

Verses 17–29 — “I Am Doing a New Thing”
17181920212223242526272829

Hebrew Word Study

חָדָשׁ ḥādāsh — new: "When the poor and needy seek water and there is none, I the LORD will answer them" (v.17). Rivers on barren heights, springs in valleys, desert becomes pools (v.18). Cedar, acacia, myrtle, olive, cypress, fir, and pine planted together in the wasteland (v.19) — seven trees, a re-created Eden. God challenges the idols: "Present your case. Tell us what the future holds. Do something, whether good or bad, so we can be dismayed" (vv.22–23). The idols are "less than nothing" (v.24). God alone announces the future: "I was the first to tell Zion" (v.27). The idol-trial theme dominates Second Isaiah: YHWH versus the gods, and the test is prophecy.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
41:17–20

"I will make rivers flow on barren heights. I will put in the desert the cedar, acacia, myrtle, olive, cypress, fir and pine." Seven trees planted. The desert becomes garden — the re-creation theme that runs from 35:1 through the whole of Second Isaiah.

41:21–24

"Tell us what the future holds. Do something! But you are less than nothing." The idol-trial: gods are challenged to prophesy. They cannot. They are nothing. YHWH alone foretells.

41:29

"See, they are all false! Their deeds amount to nothing; their images are but wind and confusion." The verdict on the gods: wind and chaos. The contrast with YHWH who creates order from chaos (Gen 1).
