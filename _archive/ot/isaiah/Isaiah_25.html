Isaiah 25

The Feast on the Mountain: Death Swallowed Up

Verses 1–5 — “You Have Done Wonderful Things”
12345

Hebrew Word Study

פֶּלֶא peleʾ — wonder / marvel: "LORD, you are my God; I will exalt you and praise your name, for in perfect faithfulness you have done wonderful things (peleʾ), things planned long ago" (v.1). After ch.24’s devastation, a hymn of praise. God has made the city a heap of rubble; the foreigners’ stronghold is no more (v.2). Ruthless nations will honour God; the poor and needy find shelter in the storm (vv.3–4). The blast of the ruthless is like a storm against a wall, like heat in the desert — but God silences the uproar (v.5).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
25:1

"In perfect faithfulness you have done wonderful things, planned long ago." God’s works are both wonderful and ancient. Nothing is improvised; everything was planned.

25:4–5

"You have been a refuge for the poor, a shelter from the storm, a shade from the heat." The three images: refuge, shelter, shade. Each addresses a different danger: poverty, violence, oppression.

Verses 6–12 — “He Will Swallow Up Death Forever”
6789101112

Hebrew Word Study

בִּלַּע billaʿ — he will swallow up / he will destroy: "He will swallow up death (billaʿ) forever. The Sovereign LORD will wipe away the tears from all faces" (v.8). THE mountain-feast and THE death-of-death. "On this mountain the LORD Almighty will prepare a feast of rich food for all peoples, a banquet of aged wine — the best of meats and the finest of wines" (v.6). The feast is for ALL PEOPLES. On this mountain he will destroy the shroud that enfolds all peoples, the sheet that covers all nations (v.7) — the veil of death. Then v.8: death SWALLOWED. Paul quotes this in 1 Corinthians 15:54: "Death has been swallowed up in victory." Tears wiped: Revelation 21:4 quotes this. "The disgrace of his people he will remove from all the earth" (v.8). "In that day they will say, ‘Surely this is our God; we trusted in him, and he saved us. Let us rejoice and be glad in his salvation’" (v.9).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
25:6

"On this mountain the LORD will prepare a feast of rich food for all peoples." The eschatological banquet: Jesus’ parables of the wedding feast (Matt 22:1–14; Luke 14:15–24) develop this. The feast is on the mountain; the guests are ALL peoples.

25:7

"He will destroy the shroud that enfolds all peoples, the sheet that covers all nations." Death is a burial shroud covering every nation. God tears it off. The universality: ALL peoples, ALL nations are under the shroud; ALL are liberated.

25:8

"He will swallow up death forever. The LORD will wipe away the tears from all faces." Two of the most quoted OT verses in the NT: 1 Cor 15:54 (death swallowed) and Rev 21:4 (tears wiped). The Old Testament’s most explicit promise of death’s destruction.

25:9

"Surely this is our God; we trusted in him, and he saved us." The response: recognition. The God they trusted turns out to be exactly who they believed he was. Trust vindicated. Salvation received.
