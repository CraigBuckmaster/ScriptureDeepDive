Psalms 70

Hasten, O God, to Save Me

Verses 1–3 — “May Those Who Seek My Life Be Put to Shame”
123

Hebrew Word Study

חוּשָׁה ḥūshâ — hasten / hurry: "Hasten (ḥūshâ), O God, to save me; come quickly, LORD, to help me" (v.1). Nearly identical to Psalm 40:13–17 — preserved independently as an urgent prayer. The brevity IS the urgency: five verses, no preamble, no argument. Just: hurry.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
70:1

"Hasten, O God, to save me." No introduction, no backstory. Straight to the emergency. The psalm is a compressed SOS.

70:2–3

"May those who seek my life be put to shame. May those who say ‘Aha!’ be turned back in disgrace." The enemies gloat with "Aha!" — the taunt of the triumphant bully.

Verses 4–5 — “I Am Poor and Needy; Come Quickly”
45

Hebrew Word Study

עָנִי ʿānî — poor / afflicted / humble: "But as for me, I am poor and needy (ʿānî); come quickly to me, O God. You are my help and my deliverer; LORD, do not delay" (v.5). The psalm’s last words: "do not delay." The prayer is bookended by urgency: "hasten" (v.1) and "do not delay" (v.5). Between them: poverty acknowledged, help claimed, deliverance expected.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
70:4

"May all who seek you rejoice and be glad in you. May those who long for your saving help always say, ‘The LORD is great!’" A brief prayer for the community inserted into the personal emergency.

70:5

"I am poor and needy. Come quickly to me, O God. Do not delay." Self-description: poor. Theology: God is help and deliverer. Request: don’t delay. The entire psalm in one verse.
