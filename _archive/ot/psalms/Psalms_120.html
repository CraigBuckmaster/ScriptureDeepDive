Psalms 120

I Call on the LORD in My Distress

Verses 1–4 — “Save Me from Lying Lips”
1234

Hebrew Word Study

הַמַּעֲלוֹת hammaʿaălōṭ — song of ascents / song of the steps: "A song of ascents (hammaʿaălōṭ)" (superscription). The first of fifteen Songs of Ascents (120–134), sung by pilgrims ascending to Jerusalem for the three annual festivals. Psalm 120 is the pilgrim’s starting point — still in exile, longing for Zion. "I call on the LORD in my distress, and he answers me" (v.1). "Save me, LORD, from lying lips and from deceitful tongues" (v.2). The deceitful tongue is compared to a warrior’s sharp arrows and burning coals (vv.3–4).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
120:1–2

"I call on the LORD in my distress. Save me from lying lips." The pilgrimage begins in distress and deception. The journey to Zion is an escape from lies.

120:3–4

"What will he do to you, you deceitful tongue? He will punish you with a warrior’s sharp arrows, with burning coals." Arrows and fire: the penalties for deception match its weaponry.

Verses 5–7 — “Too Long Have I Lived Among Those Who Hate Peace”
567

Hebrew Word Study

שָׁלוֹם shālōm — peace: "I am for peace (shālōm); but when I speak, they are for war" (v.7). The pilgrim’s lament: too long among the hostile (v.5). Meshek and Kedar (v.5) represent distant, foreign places — the exile’s location. The pilgrim is a peace-seeker surrounded by war-makers. The psalm’s final word is "war" — the pilgrimage is AWAY from war and TOWARD the shalom of Jerusalem (whose name contains shālōm).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
120:5–6

"Woe to me that I dwell in Meshek, that I live among the tents of Kedar! Too long have I lived among those who hate peace." Exile defined: living among the hostile. The pilgrimage is the journey from Meshek to Jerusalem, from war to peace.

120:7

"I am for peace; but when I speak, they are for war." The irreconcilable difference: the pilgrim offers peace; the neighbours choose war. There is no resolution except departure — toward Zion.

Prophet, lawgiver, and covenant mediator
Moses stands at the centre of the Pentateuch’s narrative — the one man who spoke with God face to face (Exod 33:11; Num 12:8) and mediated the Sinai covenant to Israel. In Leviticus he receives the priestly legislation; in Numbers he leads, intercedes, and ultimately fails at Meribah (Num 20). The writer of Hebrews calls him "faithful in all God’s house" (Heb 3:5) while distinguishing him from Christ, the Son over the house.
