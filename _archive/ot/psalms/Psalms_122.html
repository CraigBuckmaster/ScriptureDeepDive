Psalms 122

I Rejoiced When They Said, “Let Us Go to the House of the LORD”

Verses 1–5 — “Our Feet Are Standing in Your Gates, Jerusalem”
12345

Hebrew Word Study

יְרוּשָׁלִַם yərūshālayim — Jerusalem: "I rejoiced with those who said to me, ‘Let us go to the house of the LORD.’ Our feet are standing in your gates, Jerusalem (yərūshālayim)" (vv.1–2). The arrival psalm. The pilgrimage reaches its destination. Jerusalem is described as tightly compacted (v.3) — the city built for community. The tribes go up to praise the LORD’s name (v.4). Thrones for judgment stand there (v.5). Jerusalem as both worship centre and justice centre.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
122:1–2

"I rejoiced when they said, ‘Let us go to the house of the LORD.’ Our feet are standing in your gates." Joy at the invitation; arrival at the goal. The pilgrimage is complete.

122:3–5

"Jerusalem — built like a city that is closely compacted together, where the tribes go up. There stand the thrones for judgment." Two functions of Jerusalem: worship (tribes ascending) and justice (thrones of judgment).

Verses 6–9 — “Pray for the Peace of Jerusalem”
6789

Hebrew Word Study

שָׁלוֹם shālōm — peace: "Pray for the peace (shālōm) of Jerusalem: ‘May those who love you be secure’" (v.6). The most quoted verse about Jerusalem. Shālōm within shālayim — peace within the peace-city. The wordplay is deliberate. "For the sake of my family and friends... for the sake of the house of the LORD, I will seek your prosperity" (vv.8–9). The prayer is motivated by love for people AND love for God’s house.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
122:6–7

"Pray for the peace of Jerusalem. May there be peace within your walls and security within your citadels." Shālōm/shālayim wordplay. The prayer is for completeness, wholeness, security.

122:8–9

"For the sake of my family and friends, I will say, ‘Peace be within you.’ For the sake of the house of the LORD our God, I will seek your prosperity." Two motivations: human relationships and divine dwelling. Peace for Jerusalem is peace for both.
