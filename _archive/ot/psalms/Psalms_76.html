Psalms 76

In Judah God Is Known

Verses 1–6 — “His Tent Is in Salem, His Dwelling Place in Zion”
123456

Hebrew Word Study

שָׁלֵם shālēm — Salem / peace: "His tent is in Salem (shālēm), his dwelling place in Zion" (v.2). Salem = Jerusalem (Gen 14:18). God’s dwelling is in the city of peace. There he broke the weapons of war: arrows, shields, swords (v.3). "You are radiant with light, more majestic than mountains rich with game" (v.4). The warriors were plundered, they slept their last sleep (v.5). At God’s rebuke, horse and chariot lie still (v.6).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
76:1–3

"In Judah God is known. His tent is in Salem, his dwelling place in Zion. There he broke the flashing arrows, the shields and the swords." God’s home is where war ends. Weapons are broken in his presence.

76:5–6

"The valiant lie plundered, they sleep their last sleep. At your rebuke, the horse and chariot lie still." One rebuke from God ends all military might. The most powerful armies become still.

Verses 7–12 — “Make Vows to the LORD Your God and Fulfil Them”
789101112

Hebrew Word Study

נוֹרָא nōrāʾ — to be feared / awesome: "You alone are to be feared (nōrāʾ). Who can stand before you when you are angry?" (v.7). From heaven God pronounced judgment; the earth feared and was quiet (v.8). God arose to save all the afflicted of the land (v.9). "Surely your wrath against mankind brings you praise" (v.10). The nations are commanded: make vows and fulfil them. "He breaks the spirit of rulers; he is feared by the kings of the earth" (v.12).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
76:7–8

"You alone are to be feared. From heaven you pronounced judgment; the earth feared and was quiet." Divine judgment produces cosmic silence. When God speaks, everything else is still.

76:10

"Surely your wrath against mankind brings you praise." Even God’s wrath serves his glory. Judgment becomes praise — because justice is revealed.

76:12

"He breaks the spirit of rulers; he is feared by the kings of the earth." Political power bows to divine power. Every ruler is subject to God’s judgment.
