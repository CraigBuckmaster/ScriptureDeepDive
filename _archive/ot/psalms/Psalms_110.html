Psalms 110

The LORD Says to My Lord: Sit at My Right Hand

Verses 1–4 — “Sit at My Right Hand Until I Make Your Enemies a Footstool”
1234

Hebrew Word Study

נְאֻם nəʾûm — oracle / utterance / declaration: "The LORD (YHWH) says to my lord (adoni): ‘Sit at my right hand until I make your enemies a footstool for your feet’" (v.1). THE most-quoted OT verse in the NT. Jesus uses it to silence the Pharisees (Matt 22:41–46): if David calls the Messiah "lord," how can the Messiah be merely David’s son? Peter quotes it at Pentecost (Acts 2:34–35). Paul quotes it for Christ’s exaltation (1 Cor 15:25; Eph 1:20; Col 3:1). Hebrews quotes it seven times. The psalm establishes Christ’s session: seated at God’s right hand, ruling until every enemy is subdued. "You are a priest forever, in the order of Melchizedek" (v.4) — Hebrews 5–7 develops this as Christ’s eternal priesthood.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
110:1

"The LORD says to my lord: Sit at my right hand." The psalm’s opening is the NT’s most-quoted verse. Two lords: YHWH speaks to David’s lord (the Messiah). The session: seated at God’s right hand. The duration: until enemies become footstool. Jesus uses this to prove the Messiah is more than David’s son.

110:2–3

"The LORD will extend your mighty sceptre from Zion. Your troops will be willing on your day of battle." The Messiah rules from Zion. His army volunteers. The dew of youth (v.3): the Messiah’s followers are fresh and numerous as morning dew.

110:4

"The LORD has sworn and will not change his mind: ‘You are a priest forever, in the order of Melchizedek.’" The dual office: king (v.1) AND priest (v.4). No Davidic king was also a priest. Only Christ fulfils both. The Melchizedek priesthood: pre-Aaronic, eternal, superior. Hebrews 5–7 builds its entire argument on this verse.

Verses 5–7 — “He Will Crush Kings on the Day of His Wrath”
567

Hebrew Word Study

יִשְׁפּוֹט yishpōṭ — he will judge / he will execute judgment: "The Lord is at your right hand; he will crush kings on the day of his wrath. He will judge (yishpōṭ) the nations" (vv.5–6). The Messiah in battle: crushing kings, judging nations, filling the land with the dead. "He will drink from a brook along the way, and so he will lift his head" (v.7). The brook-drinking warrior: refreshed mid-battle, he lifts his head in victory. Seven verses. The most quoted, most christological, most theologically dense psalm in the Psalter.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
110:5–6

"The Lord is at your right hand; he will crush kings on the day of his wrath. He will judge the nations." The battle scene: the Messiah-king-priest conquers in God’s wrath. The day of wrath is the day of victory.

110:7

"He will drink from a brook along the way, and so he will lift his head." The warrior drinks — a moment of refreshment amid conquest. Then the head lifts: triumph. The image is of unstoppable advance, briefly pausing to drink, then pressing on to victory.
