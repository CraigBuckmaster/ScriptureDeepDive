Psalms 74

Why Have You Rejected Us Forever?

Verses 1–11 — “They Burned Your Sanctuary to the Ground”
1234567891011

Hebrew Word Study

מִקְדָשֶׁךָ miqdashĕk̅ā — your sanctuary: "They burned your sanctuary (miqdashĕk̅ā) to the ground; they defiled the dwelling place of your Name" (v.7). A communal lament over the temple’s destruction. The enemy smashed carved panels (v.6), set the meeting place on fire (v.8), destroyed all the sanctuaries in the land (v.8). "We are given no signs from God; no prophets are left" (v.9). The silence of God: no miracles, no prophetic word, no indication of how long.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
74:1

"Why have you rejected us forever, O God?" The "forever" is the anguish. Temporary rejection is discipline; permanent rejection is abandonment.

74:7–9

"They burned your sanctuary. They destroyed every place where God was worshipped. We are given no signs; no prophets are left." Triple desolation: temple destroyed, sanctuaries burned, prophetic silence. God’s presence seems erased from the earth.

Verses 12–23 — “But God Is My King from Long Ago”
121314151617181920212223

Hebrew Word Study

מֶלֶךְ melek̅ — king: "But God is my King (melek̅) from long ago; he brings salvation on the earth" (v.12). The pivot: from destruction to creation memory. God split the sea (v.13), crushed Leviathan (v.14), opened springs and dried up rivers (v.15), established sun, moon, seasons (vv.16–17). The argument: the God who created the world can restore the temple. "Rise up, O God, and defend your cause" (v.22).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
74:12–17

"But God is my King from long ago." The creation-recital as argument for restoration. The same God who split the sea and crushed Leviathan can rebuild what enemies destroyed.

74:18–21

"Remember how the enemy has mocked you. Do not hand over the life of your dove to wild beasts; do not forget the lives of your afflicted people forever." The plea: remember, do not forget, do not hand over. Three negatives to counter the silence.

74:22–23

"Rise up, O God, and defend YOUR cause." The argument shifts: this is not just our problem — it is yours. They mocked YOUR name. Defend YOUR honour.
