Psalms 147

How Good It Is to Sing Praises

Verses 1–11 — “He Heals the Brokenhearted and Binds Up Their Wounds”
1234567891011

Hebrew Word Study

לִשְׁבוּרֵי לֵב lishbūrē lēb — brokenhearted: "He heals the brokenhearted (lishbūrē lēb) and binds up their wounds" (v.3). The God who counts the stars also bandages the broken (vv.3–4). "He determines the number of the stars and calls them each by name" (v.4). The scale: from naming galaxies to binding wounds. "Great is our Lord and mighty in power; his understanding has no limit" (v.5). He covers the sky with clouds, supplies rain, makes grass grow (vv.8–9). He does not delight in horse-strength or warrior-legs but in those who fear him and hope in his ḥesed (vv.10–11).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
147:3–4

"He heals the brokenhearted and binds up their wounds. He determines the number of the stars and calls them each by name." The juxtaposition: star-namer AND wound-binder. The same God who counts galaxies counts tears. This is the Psalter’s most stunning scale-shift.

147:10–11

"His pleasure is not in the strength of the horse, nor his delight in the legs of a warrior; the LORD delights in those who fear him, who put their hope in his unfailing love." God’s delight: not power but trust. Not strength but hope-in-ḥesed.

Verses 12–20 — “He Has Revealed His Word to Jacob”
121314151617181920

Hebrew Word Study

דְבָרוֹ dəbārō — his word: "He sends his command (dəbārō) to the earth; his word runs swiftly" (v.15). God’s word controls nature: he spreads snow like wool, scatters frost like ashes, hurls hail like pebbles (vv.16–17). He sends his word and melts them; he stirs up breezes and the waters flow (v.18). "He has revealed his word to Jacob, his laws and decrees to Israel. He has done this for no other nation" (vv.19–20). The same word that governs weather governs Israel. Nature and Torah share one author. Hallelujah.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
147:15–18

"He sends his command to the earth. He spreads snow, scatters frost, hurls hail. He sends his word and melts them." The word-as-weather-control: snow, frost, hail, melting — all obeying the same command that governs Israel.

147:19–20

"He has revealed his word to Jacob. He has done this for no other nation." Israel’s unique privilege: the word that runs through nature was SPOKEN to them as Torah. Hallelujah.
