Psalms 84

How Lovely Is Your Dwelling Place

Verses 1–7 — “Better Is One Day in Your Courts”
1234567

Hebrew Word Study

יְדִידוּת yədîdūṭ — lovely / beloved / how dear: "How lovely (yədîdūṭ) is your dwelling place, LORD Almighty!" (v.1). A Sons of Korah pilgrimage psalm. The soul yearns and even faints for God’s courts (v.2). "Even the sparrow has found a home, and the swallow a nest for herself near your altar" (v.3). The smallest birds have a home in God’s temple. "Blessed are those whose strength is in you, whose hearts are set on pilgrimage" (v.5). "They go from strength to strength, till each appears before God in Zion" (v.7).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
84:1–2

"How lovely is your dwelling place! My soul yearns, even faints, for the courts of the LORD." Total-person longing: soul and body cry out for the living God.

84:3

"Even the sparrow has found a home near your altar." If a sparrow is at home in God’s temple, so is the pilgrim.

84:5–7

"Blessed are those whose hearts are set on pilgrimage. They go from strength to strength." The journey itself is the blessing. Each stage brings more strength, not less.

Verses 8–12 — “Better Is One Day in Your Courts Than a Thousand Elsewhere”
89101112

Hebrew Word Study

חָצֵר ḥāṣēr — courts: "Better is one day in your courts (ḥāṣēr) than a thousand elsewhere; I would rather be a doorkeeper in the house of my God than dwell in the tents of the wicked" (v.10). The psalm’s most quoted verse. The ratio: 1 day with God > 1,000 days without. The lowest position in God’s house (doorkeeper) surpasses the highest position outside it. "The LORD God is a sun and shield; the LORD bestows favour and honour" (v.11).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
84:10

"Better is one day in your courts than a thousand elsewhere." The radical valuation: God’s presence is 1,000x more valuable than anything else. The doorkeeper’s position is better than the king’s tent.

84:11

"The LORD God is a sun and shield; he bestows favour and honour; no good thing does he withhold from those whose walk is blameless." Sun = light and warmth. Shield = protection. Favour + honour + no good thing withheld. Total provision.

84:12

"LORD Almighty, blessed is the one who trusts in you." The psalm’s closing beatitude. Trust in God is the definition of blessedness.
