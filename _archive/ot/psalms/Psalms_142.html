Psalms 142

I Cry Aloud to the LORD

Verses 1–4 — “No One Is Concerned for Me”
1234

Hebrew Word Study

מְעָרָה məʿārâ — cave: A maskil of David, when he was in the cave (məʿārâ). David cries aloud (v.1), pours out his complaint (v.2). "When my spirit grows faint, you know my way" (v.3). Snares are hidden in his path (v.3). "Look and see, there is no one at my right hand; no one is concerned for me. I have no refuge; no one cares for my life" (v.4). The loneliest verse in the Psalter: no one at the right hand, no concern, no refuge, no care. Total isolation.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
142:1–2

"I cry aloud to the LORD; I pour out before him my complaint." The cave prayer: raw, loud, unfiltered. The cave provides no audience but God.

142:3–4

"When my spirit grows faint, you know my way. No one is concerned for me; no one cares for my life." The extremity of isolation: not attacked but IGNORED. No enemy needs to strike when no friend remains.

Verses 5–7 — “Set Me Free from My Prison”
567

Hebrew Word Study

מַסְגֵר masger — prison / confinement: "Set me free from my prison (masger), that I may praise your name" (v.7). The cave is a prison. David cannot praise while confined. The request: freedom FOR praise. "Then the righteous will gather about me because of your goodness to me" (v.7). Liberation produces community: the isolated man surrounded by the righteous. The psalm moves from total loneliness (v.4) to anticipated community (v.7). The cave psalm: confined → freed → surrounded.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
142:5

"I cry to you, LORD; I say, ‘You are my refuge, my portion in the land of the living.’" In the cave, God is the only portion. The ḥēleq theology of Psalms 16 and 73: God himself is enough.

142:7

"Set me free from my prison, that I may praise your name. Then the righteous will gather about me." Freedom’s purpose: praise. Freedom’s result: community. The isolated cave-dweller becomes the centre of a righteous gathering.
