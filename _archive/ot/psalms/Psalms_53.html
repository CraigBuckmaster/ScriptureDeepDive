Psalms 53

The Fool Says There Is No God (II)

Verses 1–3 — “All Have Turned Away”
123

Hebrew Word Study

נָבָל nābāl — fool / morally bankrupt: "The fool (nābāl) says in his heart, “There is no God”" (v.1). Nearly identical to Psalm 14. Both preserved in the Psalter — Psalm 14 uses YHWH; Psalm 53 uses Elohim (part of the Elohistic Psalter). God surveys humanity; all have turned aside; no one does good.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
53:1–3

"The fool says there is no God." The universal corruption diagnosis repeated from Psalm 14. God looks down; all are corrupt. "Not even one."

Verses 4–6 — “Oh, That Salvation Would Come from Zion!”
456

Hebrew Word Study

יְשׁוּעָה yəshūʿâ — salvation / deliverance: "Oh, that salvation (yəshūʿâ) for Israel would come out of Zion!" (v.6). The same cry as Psalm 14:7. The evildoers are struck with dread where there was nothing to dread (v.5) — the key difference from Psalm 14. God scatters the bones of attackers. The messianic longing returns: salvation from Zion.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
53:5

"God scattered the bones of those who attacked you." The Elohistic version adds this detail: the enemy’s defeat is more vivid.

53:6

"Oh, that salvation would come from Zion!" Repeated from Psalm 14. The longing for the Messiah persists through the Psalter.
