Psalms 136

His Love Endures Forever

Verses 1–12 — “To Him Who Made the Great Lights”
123456789101112

Hebrew Word Study

כִּי לְעוֹלָם kî ləʿōlām — for his love endures forever: "His love endures forever (kî ləʿōlām)" — the refrain appears 26 times, once per verse. The GREAT HALLEL. Each verse: a divine act + "his love endures forever." Creation (vv.4–9): great lights, sun, moon, stars. Exodus (vv.10–12): struck down Egypt’s firstborn, brought Israel out, divided the Red Sea. Every act of God is an act of ḥesed. The refrain teaches: EVERYTHING God does is love.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
136:1–3

"Give thanks to the LORD, for he is good. His love endures forever. Give thanks to the God of gods. Give thanks to the Lord of lords." Three titles, three refrains. Good, God of gods, Lord of lords.

136:4–9

"To him who alone does great wonders. Who by his understanding made the heavens. Who spread out the earth upon the waters. Who made the great lights." Creation as ḥesed: every element made is love expressed.

136:10–12

"To him who struck down the firstborn of Egypt. Who brought Israel out from among them. With a mighty hand and outstretched arm." Exodus as ḥesed: liberation is love enacted.

Verses 13–26 — “He Remembered Us in Our Low Estate”
1314151617181920212223242526

Hebrew Word Study

זֵכֶר zēk̅er — to remember: "He remembered (zēk̅er) us in our low estate — his love endures forever — and freed us from our enemies" (vv.23–24). The history continues: Red Sea divided (v.13), Sihon and Og defeated (vv.19–20), their land given to Israel (vv.21–22). Then the universal application: he remembered us in our LOW ESTATE. He gives food to every creature (v.25). "Give thanks to the God of heaven — his love endures forever" (v.26). 26 verses, 26 refrains, one theology: ḥesed is the explanation for everything God does.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
136:23–24

"He remembered us in our low estate. His love endures forever. And freed us from our enemies." The personal application: not just Israel, not just the exodus, but US. In OUR low estate. The refrain makes it universal.

136:25–26

"He gives food to every creature. His love endures forever. Give thanks to the God of heaven." From creation to exodus to us to every creature. The scope: everything alive is fed by ḥesed. The final refrain: the 26th "his love endures forever."
