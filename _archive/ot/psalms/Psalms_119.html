Hebrew Word Study

תּוֹרָה torâ — law / instruction / Torah: The longest chapter in the Bible: 176 verses. An acrostic: 22 stanzas of 8 verses each, one for each Hebrew letter. Every verse contains a synonym for God’s word (law, statutes, precepts, decrees, commands, word, promise, ordinances). The psalm is a sustained meditation on Torah’s sufficiency. Key verses: "How can a young person stay on the path of purity? By living according to your word" (v.9). "I have hidden your word in my heart that I might not sin against you" (v.11). "Open my eyes that I may see wonderful things in your law" (v.18). "Your word is a lamp for my feet, a light on my path" (v.105). "The unfolding of your words gives light; it gives understanding to the simple" (v.130).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
119:1–2

"Blessed are those whose ways are blameless, who walk according to the law of the LORD. Blessed are those who keep his statutes and seek him with all their heart." The double beatitude: blameless way + whole-hearted seeking.

119:9–11

"How can a young person stay on the path of purity? By living according to your word. I have hidden your word in my heart." The youth question answered: the word hidden internally produces purity externally.

119:18

"Open my eyes that I may see wonderful things in your law." The prayer every Bible student should pray: open my eyes. The wonders are IN the law; we need opened eyes to see them.

119:67

"Before I was afflicted I went astray, but now I obey your word." Affliction as pedagogy: suffering teaches obedience. The school of pain produces the student of the word.

119:71

"It was good for me to be afflicted so that I might learn your decrees." The hardest sentence in the Psalter to accept: affliction was GOOD. Not in itself but in what it produced.

Hebrew Word Study

נֵר nēr — lamp: "Your word is a lamp (nēr) for my feet, a light on my path" (v.105). The psalm’s most famous verse. Not a spotlight illuminating the whole road but a lamp for the FEET — the next step. "I am laid low in the dust; preserve my life according to your word" (v.25). "My soul is consumed with longing for your laws at all times" (v.20). "I have more insight than all my teachers, for I meditate on your statutes" (v.99). "How sweet are your words to my taste, sweeter than honey to my mouth!" (v.103). "Great peace have those who love your law, and nothing can make them stumble" (v.165). The psalm ends: "I have strayed like a lost sheep. Seek your servant, for I have not forgotten your commands" (v.176). The final word: a lost sheep who remembers the word.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
119:89–90

"Your word, LORD, is eternal; it stands firm in the heavens. Your faithfulness continues through all generations." The cosmic permanence of God’s word: eternal, firm, unshaken.

119:103–105

"How sweet are your words to my taste, sweeter than honey! Your word is a lamp for my feet, a light on my path." Taste (sweetness) + sight (lamp/light). The word engages every sense.

119:130

"The unfolding of your words gives light; it gives understanding to the simple." Unfolding: as the scroll opens, light emerges. Understanding is not for the clever but for the simple who receive.

119:165

"Great peace have those who love your law, and nothing can make them stumble." The peace promise: love of Torah produces shalom. Nothing causes stumbling. Total stability through the word.

119:176

"I have strayed like a lost sheep. Seek your servant, for I have not forgotten your commands." The psalm’s last verse is confession: I have strayed. But also: I remember. The lost sheep still knows the shepherd’s voice.
