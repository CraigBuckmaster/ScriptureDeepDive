Psalms 29

The Voice of the LORD over the Waters

Verses 1–6 — “The Voice of the LORD Is Powerful”
123456

Hebrew Word Study

קוֹל qōl — voice / thunder: "The voice (qōl) of the LORD is over the waters" (v.3). Seven thunders of the LORD’s voice: over waters (v.3), powerful (v.4), majestic (v.4), breaks cedars (v.5), makes Lebanon skip (v.6). The storm psalm: God’s voice IS the thunder. Each "voice of the LORD" is a thunderclap.

Context

A hymn to God’s power in the storm. The heavenly beings are called to worship (vv.1–2). Then seven "voice of the LORD" declarations trace the storm from sea to land: over waters (v.3), powerful and majestic (v.4), shattering cedars and making mountains skip (vv.5–6).

Cross-Reference

  • Job 37:2–5"Listen! Listen to the roar of his voice, to the rumbling that comes from his mouth" — the same thunder theology.
  • Rev 4:5"From the throne came flashes of lightning, rumblings and peals of thunder" — the heavenly storm.

"The voice of the LORD is over the waters... breaks the cedars... makes Lebanon skip like a calf." Seven thunders. The storm tracks from sea to mountain. God’s voice reshapes geography.

Verses 7–11 — “The LORD Sits Enthroned over the Flood”
7891011

Hebrew Word Study

מַבּוּל mabbūl — flood / deluge: "The LORD sits enthroned over the flood (mabbūl); the LORD is enthroned as King forever" (v.10). Mabbūl is used elsewhere only for Noah’s flood (Genesis 6–9). God who sent the flood also rules over it. Chaos is not uncontrolled; it is governed.

Context

More thunders: flashes of lightning (v.7), shakes the desert (v.8), twists oaks and strips forests (v.9). "In his temple all cry, ‘Glory!’" (v.9). Then the throne: God sits enthroned over the flood — the Noah-flood word. The God who unleashed chaos also governs it. "The LORD gives strength to his people; the LORD blesses his people with peace" (v.11). The storm ends in shalom.

Cross-Reference

  • Gen 6–9The mabbūl (flood) — the only other occurrence of this word.
  • Ps 93:3–4"The seas have lifted up their voice... mightier than the breakers of the sea — the LORD on high is mighty" — the same enthronement-over-waters theology.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
29:9

"In his temple all cry, ‘Glory!’" The storm produces worship. The appropriate response to God’s power is not fear but praise.

29:10–11

"The LORD sits enthroned over the flood. The LORD blesses his people with peace." From storm to peace. From chaos to shalom. The psalm ends where all tempests end: in God’s sovereign calm.
