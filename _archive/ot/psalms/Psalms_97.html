Psalms 97

The LORD Reigns, Let the Earth Be Glad

Verses 1–6 — “Clouds and Thick Darkness Surround Him”
123456

Hebrew Word Study

עֲנָן ʿănān — clouds: "The LORD reigns, let the earth be glad. Clouds and thick darkness (ʿănān) surround him; righteousness and justice are the foundation of his throne" (vv.1–2). The throne’s foundation: not power but righteousness and justice. God’s approach: fire (v.3), lightning (v.4), mountains melt like wax (v.5). The heavens proclaim his righteousness; all peoples see his glory (v.6).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
97:1–2

"The LORD reigns. Clouds and thick darkness surround him; righteousness and justice are the foundation of his throne." The throne stands on moral categories, not military ones.

97:4–6

"His lightning lights up the world; the earth sees and trembles. Mountains melt like wax before the LORD." The theophany: creation responds to the King’s presence with trembling and melting.

Verses 7–12 — “You Who Love the LORD, Hate Evil”
789101112

Hebrew Word Study

אוֹר ʾōr — light: "Light (ʾōr) is sown for the righteous, and joy for the upright in heart" (v.11). The psalm commands: love the LORD, hate evil (v.10). God guards the faithful and delivers them from the wicked (v.10). Light is planted like seed — not yet visible but growing underground, destined to break through. "Rejoice in the LORD, you who are righteous, and praise his holy name" (v.12).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
97:10

"Let those who love the LORD hate evil, for he guards the lives of his faithful ones." Love for God produces hatred of evil. The two are inseparable. Moral neutrality is not an option.

97:11

"Light is sown for the righteous, and joy for the upright in heart." Sown: not yet visible. The righteous may be in darkness NOW, but the light is already planted. It will sprout.

97:12

"Rejoice in the LORD, you who are righteous, and praise his holy name." The enthronement psalm’s closing command: rejoice and praise. The king reigns; therefore joy.
