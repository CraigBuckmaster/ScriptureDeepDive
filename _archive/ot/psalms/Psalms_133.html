Psalms 133

How Good and Pleasant When Brothers Dwell in Unity

Verses 1–2 — “Like Precious Oil on the Head”
12

Hebrew Word Study

יַחַד yaḥad — together / in unity: "How good and pleasant it is when God’s people live together (yaḥad) in unity!" (v.1). Three verses of pure celebration. Unity is compared to two things: precious oil running down Aaron’s beard (v.2) — the anointing oil that consecrated the high priest, flowing abundantly; and the dew of Hermon falling on Zion (v.3) — heaven’s moisture descending on the holy mountain.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
133:1

"How good and pleasant it is when God’s people live together in unity!" The statement is simple; the rarity makes it precious. Unity among God’s people is both good (morally right) and pleasant (aesthetically beautiful).

133:2

"It is like precious oil poured on the head, running down on the beard, running down on Aaron’s beard, down to the collar of his robe." The oil flows from head to beard to collar — abundant, unstoppable, consecrating everything it touches. Unity is an anointing.

Verse 3 — “There the LORD Bestows His Blessing”
3

Hebrew Word Study

בְרָכָה bərāk̅â — blessing: "For there (in unity) the LORD bestows his blessing (bərāk̅â), even life forevermore" (v.3). The dew of Hermon on Zion: refreshment from the highest mountain descending on the holy city. Where unity exists, God commands his blessing. The blessing is specified: life forevermore. Unity is the location of eternal blessing.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
133:3

"It is as if the dew of Hermon were falling on Mount Zion. For there the LORD bestows his blessing, even life forevermore." Where brothers dwell together, there — in that specific place — God commands blessing. Unity is the geography of blessing.
