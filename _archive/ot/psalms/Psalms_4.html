Psalms 4

An Evening Prayer of Trust

Verses 1–5 — “Answer Me When I Call”
12345

Hebrew Word Study

צֶדֶק ṣedeq — righteousness: "Answer me when I call to you, my righteous (ṣedeq) God" (v.1). David addresses God as righteous — the appeal is not to mercy but to justice. "Give me relief when I am in distress." The verb hirhîb means to make wide, to create space. David is cramped by enemies; he asks God to make room.

Context

An evening companion to the morning psalm (Ps 3). David confronts those who turn his glory to shame (v.2) and reminds them: the LORD has set apart the faithful (v.3). "Tremble and do not sin; search your hearts and be silent" (v.4). The counsel is to self-examination and stillness — a rare call to quiet in the Psalter.

Cross-Reference

  • Eph 4:26"In your anger do not sin" — Paul quotes Psalm 4:4 directly.
  • Ps 3:5"I lie down and sleep" — the morning/evening pair.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
4:1

"Answer me when I call." The directness of prayer: no preamble, no formality. David calls; he expects an answer.

4:3

"The LORD has set apart his faithful servant for himself." The ground of confidence: divine election. David is set apart.

4:4

"Tremble and do not sin; when you are on your beds, search your hearts and be silent." Anger is permitted; sin is not. The bed is the place of self-examination.

Alter: "how long will you love delusions?" — David addresses his enemies directly. The psalm alternates between God and opponents.

4:4

Calvin: "be silent" (dōmmū) is the hardest command in the psalm. Calvin: silence before God is the posture of submission. The tongue that would argue must learn to be still.

Verses 6–8 — “In Peace I Will Lie Down and Sleep”
678

Hebrew Word Study

שָׁלוֹם shālōm — peace / wholeness / completeness: "In peace (shālōm) I will lie down and sleep" (v.8). The Psalter’s most famous evening prayer. Shālōm is not just absence of conflict but complete well-being. David sleeps in total peace because God alone makes him dwell in safety. The trust is exclusive: "you alone, LORD."

Context

Many ask "who can show us any good?" (v.6). David answers: God’s face shining on them brings more joy than abundant harvest (v.7). The psalm closes with the perfect sleep: "in peace I will lie down and sleep, for you alone, LORD, make me dwell in safety" (v.8). The emphasis is "you alone" — security comes from one source.

Cross-Reference

  • Num 6:24–26"The LORD make his face shine on you and give you peace" — the Aaronic blessing that David echoes.
  • Phil 4:6–7"The peace of God, which transcends all understanding, will guard your hearts" — Paul’s NT development.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
4:6–7

"Let the light of your face shine on us." The Aaronic blessing personalised. God’s face = God’s favour. One moment of divine favour outweighs a year of harvest.

4:8

"In peace I will lie down and sleep, for you alone, LORD, make me dwell in safety." The psalm’s summit. Peace, sleep, safety — all from one source: "you alone."

Alter — Literary Reading

Robert Alter, The Hebrew Bible: A Translation with Commentary — Scholarly Paraphrase
4:8

Alter: the simplicity of "lie down and sleep" echoes 3:5. The two psalms form an evening/morning pair, both finding rest through trust.

Calvin’s Commentary

John Calvin, Commentaries — Faithful Paraphrase
4:8

Calvin: "you alone" excludes every other ground of security. Calvin: the Christian who can say "you alone" to God has everything; the one who needs additions has nothing.

NET Bible Notes

NET Bible Full Notes Edition — Biblical Studies Press
4:7

The NET: "when their grain and new wine abound" — the comparison is with the best economic news imaginable. God’s favour surpasses even prosperity.

4:8

The NET: "dwell in safety" (lābetaḥ) means to dwell securely, without anxiety. The safety is not the absence of danger but the presence of God.

Kidner Notes

kidner
4:8

Kidner: "you alone" is the psalm’s theological core. Not God plus security measures. Not God plus good circumstances. God alone is sufficient for sleep.

Vangemeren Notes

vangemeren
4:8

VanGemeren: this verse has been the church’s evening prayer for millennia. Compline, evening offices, and bedtime prayers draw from this well: peace, rest, divine protection.

Goldingay Notes

goldingay
4:7

Goldingay: the joy from God’s presence exceeds harvest joy. In an agricultural society, a good harvest was the highest temporal blessing. God’s face beats it.
