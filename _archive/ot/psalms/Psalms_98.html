Psalms 98

Sing to the LORD a New Song, for He Has Done Marvellous Things

Verses 1–3 — “His Right Hand Has Won the Victory”
123

Hebrew Word Study

יְשׁוּעָה yəshūʿâ — salvation / victory: "Sing to the LORD a new song, for he has done marvellous things; his right hand and his holy arm have worked salvation (yəshūʿâ) for him" (v.1). God’s salvation is PUBLIC: made known (v.2), revealed (v.2), remembered (v.3). All the ends of the earth have seen the salvation of our God (v.3). The victory is not hidden but displayed.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
98:1–3

"His right hand has worked salvation. He has revealed his righteousness to the nations. All the ends of the earth have seen the salvation of our God." The visibility of salvation: revealed, seen, proclaimed. Nothing secret.

Verses 4–9 — “Let the Rivers Clap Their Hands”
456789

Hebrew Word Study

מָחֲאוּ māḥăʾū — let them clap: "Let the rivers clap their hands (māḥăʾū), let the mountains sing together for joy" (v.8). Shout, burst into song, make music with harp, trumpets, ram’s horn (vv.4–6). Then nature joins: the sea, the world, the rivers, the mountains — all celebrate before the LORD who comes to judge (v.9). "He will judge the world in righteousness and the peoples with equity" (v.9).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
98:4–6

"Shout for joy to the LORD, all the earth, burst into jubilant song with music. Make music with the harp, with trumpets and the blast of the ram’s horn." Full orchestra + full volume + full earth.

98:7–9

"Let the sea resound. Let the rivers clap their hands; let the mountains sing." Rivers clapping — the most exuberant personification in the Psalter. Nature is not a backdrop; it is a participant. The judge who comes brings equity.
