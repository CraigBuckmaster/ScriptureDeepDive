Nehemiah 11

Repopulation of Jerusalem

Verses 1–2 — Lots Cast; Volunteers Blessed
12

Hebrew Word Study

גּוֹרָל gōrāl — lot / portion: They "cast lots" (gōrāl) to bring one in ten to live in Jerusalem. The lot is not chance but divine appointment (Prov 16:33). Those who volunteered (hanniddəḇîm) beyond the lot were "blessed" by the people — voluntary sacrifice honoured.

Context

The wall is built (ch 6), the covenant sealed (ch 10), but Jerusalem is empty (7:4). The final step: populating the city. One in ten chosen by lot; others volunteer. The combination of compulsion and voluntarism shows practical wisdom — relying solely on volunteers would leave the city short.

Cross-Reference

  • Neh 7:4"The city was large but there were few people in it" — the problem this chapter solves.
  • Prov 16:33"The lot is cast into the lap, but its every decision is from the LORD."

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
11:1

One in ten: a tithe of the population. The same principle as the tithe of income (10:37–38). The community tithes its people as well as its produce.

11:2

"The people commended all who volunteered." The volunteers receive public honour. Moving to Jerusalem meant leaving ancestral farmland for an urban life — a real sacrifice.

Calvin’s Commentary

John Calvin, Commentaries — Faithful Paraphrase
11:2

Calvin: "Those who volunteered were blessed because they chose the harder path without compulsion. Voluntary sacrifice is the highest form of obedience."

Verses 3–36 — Residents by Tribe and Settlements Outside Jerusalem

Hebrew Word Study

יוֹשְׁבֵי yōšəḇê — inhabitants / settlers: The residents (yōšəḇê) are listed by tribal origin: Judah, Benjamin, priests, Levites, gatekeepers. The categories mirror Ezra 2 and Nehemiah 7 — the community consistently defines itself by these classifications.

Historical Context

The population list includes 468 Judahites, 928 Benjaminites, 1,192 priests, 284 Levites, and 172 gatekeepers — approximately 3,044 named personnel plus families. The total population of Jerusalem may have been 10,000–15,000. The outlying settlements (vv.25–36) show the restored community occupying a small fraction of pre-exilic Judah.

Context

The settlement list (vv.25–36) traces Judahite and Benjaminite villages from Beersheba to the Hinnom Valley. The geography defines the restored province: a small territory centered on Jerusalem, nothing like David’s kingdom. The community is modest in size but specific in identity.

Cross-Reference

  • Josh 15–19The original tribal allotments — the ancestral claim these settlers are reasserting.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
11:3–19

The categories are consistent: laypeople (Judah, Benjamin), priests, Levites, gatekeepers. Every tier is needed. A city needs more than walls; it needs a functioning religious and civic structure.

11:22–24

The Levites on duty in Jerusalem include "the overseer of the Levites" and a royal appointee "in all affairs relating to the people." Both religious and civil administration.

11:25–36

Settlements from Beersheba to the Valley of Hinnom — the traditional boundaries of Judah, though the actual territory was much smaller than the pre-exilic kingdom.

11:36

"Some Levites of Judah settled in Benjamin" — tribal mixing for practical reasons. The post-exilic community is less rigidly tribal than its pre-exilic predecessor.

Calvin’s Commentary

John Calvin, Commentaries — Faithful Paraphrase
11:25–36

Calvin: "Every village is a witness to God’s faithfulness. They returned not to an abstract homeland but to specific places with names and histories."

NET Bible Notes

NET Bible Full Notes Edition — Biblical Studies Press
11:25–30

The NET identifies most of the listed towns, noting they cluster in the Judean hills and Shephelah — the agricultural heartland of the restored province.

Williamson Notes

williamson
11:3–19

Williamson compares this list with 1 Chr 9:2–17, finding significant overlap. Both may derive from a census document of the early Persian period.

11:25–36

Williamson maps the settlements and concludes the restored province was approximately 25 miles north-south and 15 miles east-west — tiny by pre-exilic standards.

Kidner Notes

11:25–36
