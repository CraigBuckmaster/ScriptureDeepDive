Nehemiah 10

The Sealed Covenant

Verses 1–27 — The Signatories
123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627

Hebrew Word Study

חֲתוּמִים ḥătûmîm — sealed ones / signatories: Eighty-four names seal the document: Nehemiah first, then priests, Levites, and lay leaders. The naming is the commitment. Each signature binds a family.

Context

The list serves a legal function: these are the guarantors. If the covenant is broken, these families bear responsibility. Public naming creates public accountability.

Cross-Reference

  • Josh 24:25–27Joshua’s covenant with witnesses — the same legal pattern.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
10:1

Nehemiah signs first as governor. Political authority backs religious commitment. Church and state align in covenant purpose.

10:2–27

Twenty-one priests, seventeen Levites, forty-four lay leaders. The three tiers of community leadership are all represented. No class exempts itself.

Verses 28–39 — The Specific Commitments
282930313233343536373839

Hebrew Word Study

לא נַעֲזוֹב lōʾ naʿaẏōḇ — we will not neglect: "We will not neglect the house of our God" (v.39). The covenant’s final sentence and its summary. Everything else — intermarriage, Sabbath, tithes, wood offering — serves this one principle.

Context

The commitments are specific, not vague: no intermarriage (v.30), Sabbath observance including refusing to buy from foreigners on the Sabbath (v.31), temple tax (v.32), wood offering (v.34), firstfruits (vv.35–37), tithes (vv.37–38). Each addresses a failure documented earlier in the book. The covenant is not aspirational but corrective.

Cross-Reference

  • Deut 7:3–4The intermarriage prohibition the covenant reaffirms.
  • Exod 23:10–12The Sabbath and sabbatical year the covenant revives.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
10:30

No intermarriage — the issue from Ezra 9–10 is formally prohibited. The community draws a line it failed to hold before.

10:31

Sabbath enforcement: "We will not buy from foreign merchants on the Sabbath." Economic activity as covenant faithfulness. What you buy and when you buy it is a spiritual act.

10:32–33

A self-imposed temple tax of one-third shekel. The Torah prescribes half a shekel (Exod 30:13); the community adjusts downward because of poverty. Faithfulness within means.

10:39

"We will not neglect the house of our God." The covenant’s last word. Everything else flows from and toward this: the temple as the centre of covenant life.

Calvin’s Commentary

John Calvin, Commentaries — Faithful Paraphrase
10:39

Calvin: "We will not neglect. The simplest and most necessary of all vows. Neglect, not rebellion, is the church’s constant danger."

NET Bible Notes

NET Bible Full Notes Edition — Biblical Studies Press
10:32

The NET discusses the one-third shekel as an adaptation from the Exod 30:13 half-shekel, likely reflecting economic hardship in the post-exilic community.

Williamson Notes

williamson
10:31

Williamson notes the Sabbath commercial prohibition goes beyond Torah’s work-rest framework. The community is legislating against economic pressure to trade on the Sabbath — a specific post-exilic challenge.

Kidner Notes

kidner
10:32–33

Kidner: "One-third shekel instead of half. They were too poor for the full amount but too faithful to give nothing. Grace adjusts the standard to the capacity."

10:39

Kidner: "The final sentence is the covenant in miniature. Not neglect. The opposite of faithfulness is not hostility but inattention."
