Michael V. Fox

JPS Qohelet & Hebrew Philologist

Biography

Michael V. Fox is the Jay C. and Ruth Halls Bascom Professor Emeritus of Hebrew at the University of Wisconsin-Madison. He is one of the leading Hebraists of his generation, with expertise spanning wisdom literature, Egyptian literature, and Semitic philology.

Key Works

His A Time to Tear Down and a Time to Build Up (1999) and the JPS commentary on Qohelet (2004) together constitute the most comprehensive philological analysis of Ecclesiastes in English.

Approach

Fox’s signature contribution is the argument that hebel means “absurd” rather than “meaningless” or “vain.” Life is not empty but irrational — it defies the human expectation that effort should produce proportionate results. This reading transforms Ecclesiastes from nihilism into existential protest, closer in spirit to Camus than to despair.
