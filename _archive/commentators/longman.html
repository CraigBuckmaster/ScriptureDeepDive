Tremper Longman III

NICOT Ecclesiastes & Song of Solomon

Biography

Tremper Longman III is Distinguished Scholar and Professor Emeritus of Biblical Studies at Westmont College. He has authored or co-authored over thirty books, including commentaries, introductions, and theological studies across the Old Testament.

Key Works

His NICOT Ecclesiastes (1998) and NICOT Song of Solomon (2001) are standard evangelical commentaries. He also co-authored An Introduction to the Old Testament with Raymond Dillard, one of the most widely used OT survey texts.

Approach

Longman reads Qoheleth as a character within the book whose pessimistic observations are framed and corrected by the epilogue narrator (12:9–14). The “fear God and keep his commandments” conclusion recontextualises everything Qoheleth said. His approach is literary-canonical: the final form of the text, not just Qoheleth’s voice, carries the theological message.
