Martin J. Selman

Old Testament Scholar & Chronicles Specialist

Biography

Martin J. Selman served as Director of Postgraduate Studies at Spurgeon's College, London, and as a lecturer in Old Testament at the college for many years. His academic focus on the historical books of the Old Testament, particularly Chronicles, made him one of the leading evangelical voices on the Chronicler's theology and purpose.

Key Works

His two-volume commentary on 1 & 2 Chronicles in the Tyndale Old Testament Commentary (TOTC) series (1994) is widely regarded as one of the best mid-level evangelical treatments of Chronicles. Selman excels at making the Chronicler's theological agenda accessible — explaining why the genealogies matter, why David is presented differently than in Samuel, and how the post-exilic community found identity in these texts.

Approach

Selman reads Chronicles as purposeful theological history rather than a defective retelling of Samuel-Kings. He emphasises the Chronicler's distinctive themes: "all Israel" as a united worshipping community, David as liturgist rather than warrior, the centrality of the temple, and the Levites as guardians of Israel's spiritual heritage. His commentary balances careful historical research with pastoral application.
