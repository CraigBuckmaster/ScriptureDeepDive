2 Chronicles 13

Abijah’s Victory Through Legitimate Worship

Verses 1‐12 — Abijah’s Speech: “God Is with Us”
123456789101112

Context

Abijah confronts Jeroboam’s 800,000 troops with his own 400,000. Before battle he delivers a speech from Mount Zemaraim: “Don’t you know that the LORD gave Israel’s kingship to David’s descendants forever? Jeroboam set up golden calves as gods. You drove out the LORD’s priests and made priests of your own. We have not forsaken the LORD.” The speech is found only in Chronicles — the Chronicler’s most extended theological argument for Judah’s legitimacy.

    Verses 13‐22 — Ambush Defeated by Prayer; 500,000 Casualties
    13141516171819202122

    Context

    Jeroboam ambushes from behind while Abijah speaks. Judah cries out to God; the priests blow trumpets. God routs Israel: 500,000 casualties. “The Israelites were subdued... because they relied on the LORD.” The battle-by-worship pattern: legitimate worship + reliance on God = military victory. Abijah grows strong. The Chronicler gives him a positive evaluation that Kings (which calls him Abijam and condemns him) does not.
