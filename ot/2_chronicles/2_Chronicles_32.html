2 Chronicles 32

Sennacherib’s Invasion; Hezekiah’s Faith

Verses 1‐19 — Sennacherib Invades; Hezekiah Encourages the People
12345678910111213141516171819

Context

After Hezekiah’s faithfulness, Sennacherib invades. Hezekiah blocks springs outside the city (the Siloam Tunnel), strengthens the walls, and addresses the people: “Be strong and courageous. With him is only the arm of flesh, but with us is the LORD our God to help us and to fight our battles.” Sennacherib’s officials mock: “No god of any nation has been able to deliver his people from my hand.” The Chronicler condenses 2 Kings 18–19’s Rabshakeh speech into a summary, keeping the theological challenge intact.

    Verses 20‐33 — Hezekiah and Isaiah Pray; the Angel Strikes; Hezekiah’s Final Assessment
    2021222324252627282930313233

    Context

    “King Hezekiah and the prophet Isaiah cried out in prayer to heaven about this.” The LORD sends an angel who annihilates the Assyrian army. Sennacherib returns to Nineveh and is killed by his sons. The Chronicler adds: “So the LORD saved Hezekiah and the people of Jerusalem.” Hezekiah’s illness, Merodach-Baladan’s embassy, and the treasury-showing are compressed into a few verses. “Hezekiah’s heart was proud” but he repents, and wrath is deferred. He dies honoured: “All Judah and the people of Jerusalem honoured him when he died.”
