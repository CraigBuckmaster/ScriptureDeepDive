2 Chronicles 16

Asa’s Failure — Trust in Aram, Not God

Verses 1‐6 — Asa Buys Aramean Help Against Israel
123456

Context

In his 36th year, Baasha of Israel attacks. Instead of praying (as he did against Zerah in ch 14), Asa takes silver and gold from the temple and sends it to Ben-Hadad of Aram: “Break your treaty with Baasha.” The strategy works militarily but fails theologically — Asa seeks a foreign king instead of God.

    Verses 7‐14 — Hanani’s Rebuke; Asa’s Rage; His Death
    7891011121314

    Context

    The seer Hanani confronts Asa: “Because you relied on the king of Aram and not on the LORD your God, the army of the king of Aram has escaped from your hand.” He reminds Asa of the Cushite victory: “The eyes of the LORD range throughout the earth to strengthen those whose hearts are fully committed to him.” Asa rages, imprisons Hanani, and brutalises some people. He develops a foot disease and “even in his illness he did not seek the LORD but only the physicians.” He dies. The man who once sought God with his whole heart ends by seeking only doctors.
