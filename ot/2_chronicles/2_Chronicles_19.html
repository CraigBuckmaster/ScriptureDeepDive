2 Chronicles 19

Jehoshaphat’s Judicial Reform

Verses 1‐3 — Jehu’s Rebuke for the Ahab Alliance
123

Context

Jehu son of Hanani meets Jehoshaphat: “Should you help the wicked and love those who hate the LORD? Because of this, the wrath of the LORD is on you.” But he adds: “There is, however, some good in you, for you have rid the land of the Asherah poles and have set your heart on seeking God.” Partial judgment for partial failure — the Chronicler’s proportional retribution.

    Verses 4‐11 — Judges Appointed; “Judge Carefully, for the LORD Is with You”
    4567891011

    Context

    Jehoshaphat appoints judges throughout Judah with a charge unique to Chronicles: “Consider carefully what you do, because you are not judging for mere mortals but for the LORD, who is with you whenever you give a verdict. Judge carefully, for with the LORD our God there is no injustice or partiality or bribery.” He establishes a central court in Jerusalem with Levites, priests, and clan leaders. Justice is both local and centralised, both divine and institutional.
