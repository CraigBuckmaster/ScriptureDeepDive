2 Chronicles 23

Jehoiada’s Coup — Joash Enthroned

Verses 1‐11 — The Seventh-Year Revolution; Joash Crowned
1234567891011

Context

In the seventh year, Jehoiada the priest executes a carefully prepared coup. He brings military commanders into the conspiracy, stations Levites and gatekeepers around the temple, and presents the boy king. “Long live the king!” The Chronicler adds Levitical detail: the Levites guard every entrance, and only priests and serving Levites may enter the temple. The revolution is both military and liturgical.

    Verses 12‐21 — Athaliah Executed; Covenant Renewed; the City Is Quiet
    12131415161718192021

    Context

    Athaliah hears the commotion, sees the king by the pillar, and screams “Treason!” She is executed outside the temple. Jehoiada makes a covenant between the LORD, the king, and the people. The Baal temple is destroyed, its priest Mattan killed. “All the people of the land rejoiced, and the city was calm.” Order restored through legitimate worship and legitimate kingship.
