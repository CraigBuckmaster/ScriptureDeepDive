Daniel 7

The Four Beasts and the Son of Man

Verses 1–14 — “One Like a Son of Man Coming with the Clouds of Heaven”
1234567891011121314

Hebrew Word Study

בַר אֱנָשׁ bar ʾĕnāsh — son of man: "One like a son of man (bar ʾĕnāsh), coming with the clouds of heaven. He approached the Ancient of Days and was led into his presence. He was given authority, glory and sovereign power; all nations and peoples of every language worshipped him. His dominion is an everlasting dominion that will not pass away, and his kingdom is one that will never be destroyed" (vv.13–14). THE most christologically significant passage in Daniel. The chapter pivots from ch.2’s statue to four beasts: a lion with eagle wings (Babylon), a bear (Medo-Persia), a leopard with four wings and four heads (Greece), and a terrifying fourth beast with iron teeth and ten horns (Rome). Then the vision shifts to heaven: the Ancient of Days takes his seat, fire streaming, books opened (vv.9–10). "One like a son of man" receives eternal dominion. Jesus’ favourite self-designation — "Son of Man" — comes from this verse.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
7:1–4

Four beasts from the sea. The lion with eagle’s wings: Babylon (cf. Jer 4:7, 49:22). Its wings are torn off and it stands like a man — possibly Nebuchadnezzar’s humbling and restoration.

7:7–8

The fourth beast: terrifying, with iron teeth and ten horns. A little horn rises among them with eyes of a man and a boastful mouth. The horn speaks against the Most High and oppresses the saints (v.25). This becomes the primary OT source for NT antichrist theology.

7:9–10

"The Ancient of Days took his seat. His clothing was as white as snow; the hair of his head like pure wool. His throne was flaming with fire. Thousands upon thousands attended him; ten thousand times ten thousand stood before him." The heavenly courtroom. God as judge. The fire is purifying and judicial.

7:13–14

"One like a son of man, coming with the clouds of heaven." Jesus quotes this at his trial before the Sanhedrin (Mark 14:62) — the moment they accuse him of blasphemy. He claims to be this figure: divine authority, eternal kingdom, universal worship. This is the title he uses more than 80 times in the Gospels.

Verses 15–28 — “The Saints Will Receive the Kingdom Forever”

Hebrew Word Study

קַדִּישֵׁי qaddishē — holy ones / saints: "The saints (qaddishē) of the Most High will receive the kingdom and will possess it forever — yes, for ever and ever" (v.18). Daniel is troubled and asks for the interpretation (v.15). The four beasts are four kingdoms (v.17). The little horn wages war against the saints and is defeating them — until the Ancient of Days pronounces judgment in their favour (vv.21–22). The horn will speak against the Most High, oppress the saints, try to change set times and laws, and rule for "a time, times and half a time" (v.25) — 3.5 years, the symbolic period of tribulation that recurs in Revelation (Rev 11:2; 12:6, 14; 13:5). But the court will sit, his power will be taken away, and "the sovereignty, power and greatness of all the kingdoms under heaven will be handed to the holy people of the Most High" (v.27).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
7:17–18

"The four great beasts are four kings that will rise from the earth. But the saints of the Most High will receive the kingdom." The interpretation’s first sentence: four kingdoms rise. Second sentence: the saints receive the kingdom. Beasts are temporary; the saints’ kingdom is forever.

7:21–22

"The horn was waging war against the saints and defeating them — until the Ancient of Days came and pronounced judgment in their favour." The saints lose UNTIL God intervenes. Faithfulness does not guarantee immediate victory; it guarantees ultimate vindication.

7:25

"He will try to change the set times and the laws. The saints will be delivered into his hands for a time, times and half a time." 3.5 years: the template for tribulation’s duration in Revelation. A limit is set on suffering; it will not last forever.

7:27

"Then the sovereignty, power and greatness of all the kingdoms under heaven will be handed to the holy people of the Most High. His kingdom will be an everlasting kingdom." The end: not destruction of the world but transfer of power. The saints inherit what the beasts held temporarily.

Theological Themes Tracker
