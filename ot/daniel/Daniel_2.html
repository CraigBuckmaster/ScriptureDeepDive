Daniel 2

Nebuchadnezzar’s Dream: The Statue of Four Kingdoms

Verses 1–23 — “The King’s Decree: Tell Me the Dream AND Its Interpretation”
1234567891011121314151617181920212223

Hebrew Word Study

רָז rāz — mystery / secret: "The mystery (rāz) was revealed to Daniel in a vision of the night" (v.19). Nebuchadnezzar dreams and demands his wise men tell him both the dream AND its meaning — an impossible test (vv.1–11). The wise men protest: no king has ever asked such a thing (v.10). Nebuchadnezzar orders all wise men executed (v.12). Daniel asks for time, gathers his friends to pray, and God reveals the mystery in a night vision (vv.14–19). Daniel’s prayer of praise (vv.20–23) declares that God "changes times and seasons, removes kings and sets up kings" — the book’s thesis in miniature.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
2:1–6

The impossible demand: tell me the dream I forgot AND what it means. If you can’t, you die. The king tests whether his advisors have genuine access to divine knowledge or are merely clever.

2:10–11

"There is no one on earth who can do what the king asks. No one can reveal it except the gods, and they do not live among humans." The Babylonian wise men inadvertently state the theological truth: only God can reveal mysteries. And he does live among his people — through Daniel.

2:19–23

"Praise be to the name of God for ever and ever; wisdom and power are his. He changes times and seasons; he deposes kings and raises up kings." Daniel’s prayer. The theology of the entire book compressed into four verses.

2:20–21

"He gives wisdom to the wise and knowledge to the discerning. He reveals deep and hidden things; he knows what lies in darkness, and light dwells with him." God as the source of all wisdom. Light in the darkness — literally and metaphorically.

Verses 24–49 — “The God of Heaven Has Set Up a Kingdom That Will Never Be Destroyed”

Hebrew Word Study

אֶבֶן ʾeben — stone: "A rock (ʾeben) was cut out, but not by human hands. It struck the statue on its feet and smashed them" (v.34). Daniel reveals the dream: a colossal statue with a gold head (Babylon), silver chest (Medo-Persia), bronze belly (Greece), iron legs (Rome), and iron-clay feet (divided kingdoms). Then a stone "cut out but not by human hands" strikes the feet and the entire statue collapses. The stone becomes a mountain that fills the earth (vv.34–35). "The God of heaven will set up a kingdom that will never be destroyed" (v.44). Nebuchadnezzar falls prostrate and declares Daniel’s God "the God of gods and the Lord of kings" (v.47). Daniel is promoted; his friends receive positions (v.49).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
2:31–35

The statue: gold, silver, bronze, iron, iron-clay. Each metal is less valuable but harder — the kingdoms decline in glory but increase in military force. The rock strikes the weakest point (the feet) and everything collapses.

2:34–35

"A rock was cut out, not by human hands." The unhewn stone: no human agency. God’s kingdom is not built by political or military means. It comes from outside the system and replaces the entire structure.

2:44

"The God of heaven will set up a kingdom that will never be destroyed, nor will it be left to another people. It will crush all those kingdoms and bring them to an end, but it will itself endure forever." The most important verse in Daniel: God’s kingdom outlasts all empires.

2:47

"Surely your God is the God of gods and the Lord of kings, and a revealer of mysteries." Nebuchadnezzar’s first confession. He calls Daniel’s God supreme. This is the first step in his long journey toward genuine worship (ch.4).
