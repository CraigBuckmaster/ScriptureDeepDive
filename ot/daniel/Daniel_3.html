Daniel 3

The Fiery Furnace

Verses 1–18 — “We Will Not Serve Your Gods”
123456789101112131415161718

Hebrew Word Study

צְלֵם ṣəlēm — image / statue: "King Nebuchadnezzar made an image (ṣəlēm) of gold, sixty cubits high and six cubits wide" (v.1). The golden statue — 90 feet tall. All peoples must bow when the music plays or be thrown into a blazing furnace (vv.4–6). Shadrach, Meshach, and Abednego refuse. Nebuchadnezzar is furious and offers them one more chance (vv.13–15). Their answer is the most defiant statement of faith in the OT: "The God we serve is able to deliver us. But even if he does not, we will not serve your gods or worship the image you have set up" (vv.17–18). The "but even if he does not" is the theological centre: faith is not contingent on rescue.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
3:1

The image: 90 feet of gold. After ch.2 declared the gold head was Nebuchadnezzar, he builds an ENTIRE statue of gold — as if to say: my kingdom won’t decline. It’s a direct rejection of the dream’s message.

3:4–6

The music plays; everyone bows. The compliance is total except for three. The music-bow-worship cycle is Babylon’s liturgy: sensory overload producing uniform submission.

3:16–18

"We do not need to defend ourselves. If we are thrown into the blazing furnace, the God we serve is able to deliver us. BUT EVEN IF HE DOES NOT, we will not serve your gods." Three-part declaration: (1) We don’t need to explain. (2) God CAN rescue. (3) Even if he DOESN’T, we still won’t bow. Faith without guarantee.

Verses 19–30 — “I See Four Men Walking in the Fire”
192021222324252627282930

Hebrew Word Study

בַר אֱלָהִין bar ʾĕlāhîn — son of the gods / divine being: "Look! I see four men walking around in the fire, unbound and unharmed, and the fourth looks like a son of the gods (bar ʾĕlāhîn)" (v.25). The furnace is heated seven times hotter (v.19). The soldiers who throw them in die from the heat (v.22). But inside: four figures walk freely, unbound. The fourth figure — Nebuchadnezzar calls him "a son of the gods" (v.25), then later "his angel" (v.28). The three emerge without even the smell of smoke (v.27). Nebuchadnezzar decrees that no one may speak against their God (v.29). They are promoted (v.30).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
3:19–22

Seven times hotter. The soldiers carrying them die. The intensity of the persecution only magnifies the miracle. Babylon’s own servants are killed by Babylon’s own instrument.

3:25

"The fourth looks like a son of the gods." The christological reading: the pre-incarnate Christ in the furnace. The text leaves the identification open; the church sees Christ. Either way: divine presence in the fire.

3:27

"The fire had not harmed their bodies, nor was a hair singed; their robes were not scorched, and there was no smell of fire on them." The catalogue of what fire didn’t do: no burn, no singe, no scorch, no smell. The deliverance is total.

3:28–30

"Praise be to the God of Shadrach, Meshach and Abednego, who has sent his angel and rescued his servants!" Nebuchadnezzar’s second confession. Stronger than ch.2: now he decrees protection for their God.
