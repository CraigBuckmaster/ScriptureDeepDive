Daniel 12

The Resurrection and the Time of the End

Verses 1–4 — “Multitudes Who Sleep in the Dust Will Awake”
1234

Hebrew Word Study

הָקִיצוּ hāqîṣū — they will awake: "Multitudes who sleep in the dust of the earth will awake (hāqîṣū): some to everlasting life, others to shame and everlasting contempt" (v.2). THE clearest resurrection text in the OT. Michael rises to protect Israel during a time of unprecedented distress (v.1). Everyone written in the book will be delivered (v.1). Then the dead awake — to two destinations: everlasting life or everlasting contempt. "Those who are wise will shine like the brightness of the heavens, and those who lead many to righteousness, like the stars for ever and ever" (v.3). The wise become stars. The righteous become eternal light. Daniel is told: "Close up and seal the words of the scroll until the time of the end. Many will go here and there to increase knowledge" (v.4).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
12:1

"At that time Michael, the great prince who protects your people, will arise. There will be a time of distress such as has not happened from the beginning of nations." The great tribulation: unprecedented distress. Michael as Israel’s guardian. Jesus echoes this in Matthew 24:21.

12:2

"Multitudes who sleep in the dust of the earth will awake: some to everlasting life, others to shame and everlasting contempt." The OT’s clearest resurrection statement. Two destinies: eternal life and eternal contempt. Jesus quotes this in John 5:28–29. The dual resurrection is the basis for the entire NT eschatology of judgment.

12:3

"Those who are wise will shine like the brightness of the heavens, and those who lead many to righteousness, like the stars for ever and ever." The reward of the faithful: luminosity. The wise glow. The righteous-makers become stars. Jesus echoes this in Matthew 13:43: "the righteous will shine like the sun."

12:4

"Close up and seal the words of the scroll until the time of the end." The book is sealed — its full meaning reserved for the future. Revelation 22:10 reverses this: "Do not seal up the words of the prophecy." Daniel seals; Revelation unseals.

Verses 5–13 — “How Long Until the End of These Wonders?”
5678910111213

Hebrew Word Study

קֵץ qēṣ — end: "How long will it be before these astonishing things are fulfilled?" (v.6). Two angels stand on either side of the river. The man in linen (from ch.10) raises both hands to heaven and swears: "It will be for a time, times and half a time" (v.7) — the 3.5-year period from 7:25. Daniel does not understand and asks: "What will the outcome be?" (v.8). The answer: "Go your way, Daniel, because the words are rolled up and sealed until the time of the end. Many will be purified, made spotless and refined, but the wicked will continue to be wicked. None of the wicked will understand, but those who are wise will understand" (vv.9–10). Two additional numbers: 1,290 days and 1,335 days (vv.11–12). "Blessed is the one who waits for and reaches the end of the 1,335 days" (v.12). The book’s final word to Daniel: "You will rest, and then at the end of the days you will rise to receive your allotted inheritance" (v.13). The last verse of Daniel: a personal resurrection promise.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
12:6–7

"How long?" The 3.5 years from 7:25 confirmed with an oath. The man raises BOTH hands and swears by the Eternal One. The period is fixed and sworn.

12:9–10

"The words are sealed until the time of the end. Many will be purified, made spotless and refined, but the wicked will continue to be wicked." The separation: the wise understand; the wicked don’t. Purification is ongoing; wickedness is self-perpetuating.

12:12

"Blessed is the one who waits for and reaches the end of the 1,335 days." The blessing of patience: endurance through the numbered days. The beatitude is for those who survive to the end.

12:13

"As for you, go your way till the end. You will rest, and then at the end of the days you will rise to receive your allotted inheritance." Daniel’s personal commission: go, rest, RISE. The book ends not with a kingdom-vision but with a personal promise: you will be resurrected. The resurrection of 12:2 is applied individually to Daniel. He will receive his inheritance.
