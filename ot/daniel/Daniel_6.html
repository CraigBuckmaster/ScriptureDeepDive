Daniel 6

Daniel in the Lion’s Den

Verses 1–14 — “He Still Prays Three Times a Day”
1234567891011121314

Hebrew Word Study

בָּרֵךְ bārēk̅ — to kneel / to bless: "Three times a day he got down on his knees (bārēk̅) and prayed, giving thanks to his God, just as he had done before" (v.10). Darius appoints 120 satraps with Daniel as one of three administrators. Daniel distinguishes himself (v.3). His rivals find no corruption — "they could find no corruption in him, because he was trustworthy; neither corrupt nor negligent" (v.4). So they target his religion (v.5). They manipulate Darius into signing an irrevocable decree: anyone who prays to any god or human except the king for thirty days will be thrown into the lion’s den (vv.6–9). Daniel’s response: he goes home, opens his window toward Jerusalem, and prays exactly as before (v.10). The phrase "just as he had done before" is the key: Daniel does not escalate or hide. He simply continues.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
6:3–4

"Daniel so distinguished himself that the king planned to set him over the whole kingdom. They could find no corruption in him." His enemies investigate his entire career and find nothing. The only vulnerability is his faithfulness to God.

6:5

"We will never find any basis for charges against this man Daniel unless it has something to do with the law of his God." The enemies’ own testimony to Daniel’s character: his religion is his only point of attack.

6:10

"He went home and three times a day he got down on his knees and prayed, giving thanks to his God, just as he had done before." Windows open toward Jerusalem (cf. 1 Kgs 8:48). He does not add drama or reduce frequency. Continuity IS the resistance.

6:14

"The king was greatly distressed; he was determined to rescue Daniel and made every effort until sundown to save him." Darius is trapped by his own law. The irrevocability that was supposed to exalt him now enslaves him.

Verses 15–28 — “My God Sent His Angel and Shut the Mouths of the Lions”
1516171819202122232425262728

Hebrew Word Study

מַלְאַךְ malʾak̅ — angel / messenger: "My God sent his angel (malʾak̅) and shut the mouths of the lions" (v.22). Daniel is thrown in; the stone is sealed with the king’s signet (v.17). Darius fasts all night, cannot sleep (v.18). At dawn he rushes to the den and calls out: "Daniel, servant of the living God, has your God been able to rescue you?" (v.20). Daniel answers: alive, unhurt, "because I was found innocent in his sight" (v.22). Darius orders Daniel’s accusers thrown in; they are devoured before they reach the floor (v.24). Darius issues a decree: "In every part of my kingdom people must fear and reverence the God of Daniel" (v.26). Daniel prospers through Darius’s reign and into Cyrus’s (v.28).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
6:16–17

"May your God, whom you serve continually, rescue you!" The stone is placed; the king’s own seal closes it. The den is a tomb — stone sealed, authority stamped. The resurrection parallels (Matt 27:60–66) are structural.

6:20–22

"Daniel, has your God been able to rescue you?" The question expects no. Daniel’s answer reverses everything: "My God sent his angel." God’s angel in the furnace (ch.3), God’s angel in the den (ch.6). The pattern: divine presence in the place of death.

6:24

The accusers and their families thrown in. "Before they reached the floor of the den, the lions overpowered them and crushed all their bones." The lions were not tame — they were restrained. God’s intervention was specific and miraculous, not natural.

6:26–27

"He is the living God and he endures forever; his kingdom will not be destroyed. He rescues and he saves; he performs signs and wonders in the heavens and on the earth." Darius’s decree: the third pagan ruler in Daniel to confess God’s sovereignty (after Nebuchadnezzar twice and Belshazzar implicitly).
