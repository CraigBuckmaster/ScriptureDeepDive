Psalms 126

When the LORD Restored the Fortunes of Zion

Verses 1–3 — “Our Mouths Were Filled with Laughter”
123

Hebrew Word Study

שְׂחוֹק səḥōq — laughter: "When the LORD restored the fortunes of Zion, we were like those who dreamed. Our mouths were filled with laughter (səḥōq), our tongues with songs of joy" (vv.1–2). The restoration was so unexpected it felt like a dream. Laughter and singing erupted spontaneously. Even the nations noticed: "The LORD has done great things for them" (v.2). The community confirms: "The LORD has done great things for us, and we are filled with joy" (v.3).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
126:1–2

"We were like those who DREAMED. Our mouths were filled with LAUGHTER." The restoration was surreal — too good to be real. Joy broke out before analysis could begin.

126:2–3

"Then it was said among the nations, ‘The LORD has done great things for them.’ The LORD has done great things for US." Even outsiders recognised God’s work. The testimony is global before it is personal.

Verses 4–6 — “Those Who Sow in Tears Will Reap with Songs of Joy”
456

Hebrew Word Study

דִּמְעָה dimʿâ — tears: "Those who sow with tears (dimʿâ) will reap with songs of joy. Those who go out weeping, carrying seed to sow, will return with songs of joy, carrying sheaves with them" (vv.5–6). The psalm’s most quoted verses. The agricultural metaphor: tears are seed. Weeping is sowing. Joy is harvest. The farmer goes out crying and comes back singing. The proportion is radical: tears produce songs; weeping produces sheaves. Time transforms sorrow into celebration.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
126:4

"Restore our fortunes, LORD, like streams in the Negev." The Negev’s dry wadis suddenly flood with winter rain. Restoration can be sudden and overwhelming.

126:5–6

"Those who SOW in TEARS will REAP with SONGS of joy." The sequence: sowing (labour) + tears (suffering) → reaping (harvest) + songs (joy). Weeping is not wasted; it is planting.
