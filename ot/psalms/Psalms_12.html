Psalms 12

Help, LORD, for the Faithful Are No More

Verses 1–4 — “Everyone Lies to Their Neighbour”
1234

Hebrew Word Study

שָׁוְא shāwʾ — falsehood / emptiness / deception: "Everyone lies (shāwʾ) to their neighbour; they flatter with their lips but harbour deception in their hearts" (v.2). A society where trust has collapsed. The lips say one thing; the heart holds another. David asks God to silence the boastful tongue (v.3) and the proud who say "we will triumph with our tongues" (v.4).

Context

David cries for help in a society where faithfulness has vanished and lies dominate. Every neighbour deceives. Boastful tongues claim autonomy: "who is lord over us?" (v.4). The crisis is linguistic: words have lost their meaning.

Cross-Reference

  • Jer 9:4–5"Every friend is a deceiver" — the prophetic parallel of social breakdown.
  • Jas 3:5–8"The tongue is a fire" — the NT development of dangerous speech.

Verses 5–8 — “The Words of the LORD Are Flawless”
5678

Hebrew Word Study

אֲמָרוֹת ʾamārōṭ — words / sayings: "The words (ʾamārōṭ) of the LORD are flawless, like silver purified in a crucible, refined seven times" (v.6). Against human lies, God’s words are absolutely pure. Seven times refined — complete purification. The contrast: human speech is double-hearted; God’s speech is seven-times pure.

Context

God speaks: "Because the poor are plundered and the needy groan, I will now arise" (v.5). When human words fail, God’s word acts. His words are flawless — seven-times-purified silver (v.6). David trusts God’s protection even as the wicked strut freely (vv.7–8).

Cross-Reference

  • Ps 119:140"Your promises have been thoroughly tested, and your servant loves them" — the same refined-word theology.
  • Prov 30:5"Every word of God is flawless" — the wisdom parallel.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
12:5

"Because the poor are plundered, I will now arise, says the LORD." God speaks in the psalm — direct divine speech responding to the cry for help.

12:6

"The words of the LORD are flawless, like silver refined seven times." Perfect purity. Against the dross of human lies, God’s words have no impurity.

Alter — Literary Reading

Robert Alter, The Hebrew Bible: A Translation with Commentary — Scholarly Paraphrase
12:6

Alter: the industrial metaphor — crucible, refining, silver — grounds the theology in material process. Truth is not abstract; it is tested and proven.

Calvin’s Commentary

John Calvin, Commentaries — Faithful Paraphrase
12:6

Calvin: the seven-fold refining is God’s word tested by every challenge and found pure every time. Calvin: Scripture endures every furnace.

NET Bible Notes

NET Bible Full Notes Edition — Biblical Studies Press
12:6

The NET: "crucible of the earth" (ʺalîl lā-ʾāreṣ) is debated — may mean "on the ground" (the smelting floor) or "to the earth" (purified completely). Either way: thoroughly refined.

Kidner Notes

kidner
12:6

Kidner: the metallurgical image: silver purified seven times has had every trace of dross removed. God’s word contains zero deception — the opposite of the double-hearted speech of v.2.

Vangemeren Notes

vangemeren
12:5–8

VanGemeren: the psalm’s structure pivots on God’s direct speech (v.5). Human deception (vv.1–4) is answered by divine declaration (v.5). Pure words replace corrupt words.

Goldingay Notes

goldingay
12:6

Goldingay: "seven times" is completeness. The refining is not partial. God’s words are as pure as silver can possibly be.
