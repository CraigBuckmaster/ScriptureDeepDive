Psalms 59

Deliver Me from My Enemies

Verses 1–9 — “They Return at Evening, Snarling Like Dogs”
123456789

Hebrew Word Study

כְּלָבִים kəlābîm — dogs: "They return at evening, snarling like dogs (kəlābîm), and prowl about the city" (v.6). Written when Saul sent men to watch David’s house to kill him (1 Sam 19:11). The enemies are compared to prowling dogs — scavengers circling in the dark. The refrain: the dogs return, snarl, prowl (vv.6, 14). David asks God to defend him not because of his own merit but because of the enemies’ wickedness.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
59:1–2

"Deliver me from my enemies. Protect me from those who rise up against me." The siege situation: assassins outside, David trapped inside.

59:6–7

"They return at evening, snarling like dogs. See what they spew from their mouths — swords are on their lips." The dog-pack image: nocturnal, menacing, feral. Their speech is weaponised.

59:8–9

"But you laugh at them, LORD; you scoff at all those nations." God’s response to the threat: laughter (echoing Psalm 2:4). What terrifies David amuses God.

Verses 10–17 — “God Is My Fortress, My God of Unfailing Love”
1011121314151617

Hebrew Word Study

מִשְׂגַבִּי misgabbî — my fortress / my refuge: "You are my fortress (misgabbî), my refuge in times of trouble" (v.16). David asks God not to kill the enemies immediately but to scatter them as a lesson (vv.11–12). Let them wander and be consumed (v.13). The refrain returns: the dogs prowl (v.14), but David sings of God’s strength in the morning (v.16). "I will sing of your love; for you are my fortress, my refuge in times of trouble" (v.16).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
59:11–12

"Do not kill them, Lord, or my people will forget; scatter them by your power." A remarkable request: don’t destroy them instantly — let their wandering be a visible lesson. Memory requires visible evidence.

59:16–17

"I will sing of your strength, in the morning I will sing of your love. For you are my fortress, my refuge in times of trouble." Morning follows the night of the prowling dogs. David’s song replaces their snarling.
