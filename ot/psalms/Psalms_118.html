Psalms 118

The Stone the Builders Rejected

Verses 1–18 — “The LORD Is on My Side; I Will Not Fear”
123456789101112131415161718

Hebrew Word Study

הוֹדוּ hōdū — give thanks: "Give thanks (hōdū) to the LORD, for he is good; his love endures forever" (v.1). The climax of the Egyptian Hallel (113–118), sung at Passover. "The LORD is with me; I will not be afraid. What can mere mortals do to me?" (v.6). Nations surrounded me, but in the name of the LORD I cut them down (vv.10–12). "The LORD has disciplined me severely, but he has not given me over to death" (v.18). Jesus sang this psalm at the Last Supper (Matt 26:30).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
118:1

"Give thanks to the LORD, for he is good; his love endures forever." The refrain that opens and closes the psalm. The Hallel’s frame.

118:6–7

"The LORD is with me; I will not be afraid. What can mere mortals do to me?" The confidence declaration. Hebrews 13:6 quotes this.

118:10–12

"All the nations surrounded me, but in the name of the LORD I cut them down. They swarmed around me like bees, but they were consumed as quickly as burning thorns." Three times "they surrounded me" — three times "in the name of the LORD I cut them down."

Verses 19–29 — “This Is the Day the LORD Has Made”
1920212223242526272829

Hebrew Word Study

רֹאשׁ rōʾash — head / chief / cornerstone: "The stone the builders rejected has become the cornerstone (rōʾash)" (v.22). Jesus applies this to himself (Matt 21:42; Mark 12:10; Luke 20:17). Peter quotes it (Acts 4:11; 1 Pet 2:7). The rejected stone becomes the building’s most important element. "This is the day the LORD has made; let us rejoice and be glad in it" (v.24). "Blessed is he who comes in the name of the LORD" (v.26) — the crowd’s cry at the triumphal entry (Matt 21:9). The psalm Jesus sang, quoted, fulfilled, and was greeted with.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
118:22–23

"The stone the builders rejected has become the cornerstone. The LORD has done this, and it is marvellous in our eyes." Jesus applies this to himself three times in the Gospels. Peter makes it foundational (Acts 4:11; 1 Pet 2:7). The rejected one becomes the essential one.

118:24

"This is the day the LORD has made; let us rejoice and be glad in it." The most-quoted celebration verse in the church. Every day is the LORD’s; this day specifically is for joy.

118:25–26

"“Lord, save us!” “Blessed is he who comes in the name of the LORD.”" Hosanna ("save us!") and the blessing: the crowd quotes these verses as Jesus enters Jerusalem on Palm Sunday (Matt 21:9). The Hallel became the triumphal-entry script.

118:29

"Give thanks to the LORD, for he is good; his love endures forever." The psalm closes as it opened: ḥesed endures. The Hallel is complete.

Moses↗ People
Prophet, lawgiver, and covenant mediator
Moses stands at the centre of the Pentateuch’s narrative — the one man who spoke with God face to face (Exod 33:11; Num 12:8) and mediated the Sinai covenant to Israel. In Leviticus he receives the priestly legislation; in Numbers he leads, intercedes, and ultimately fails at Meribah (Num 20). The writer of Hebrews calls him "faithful in all God’s house" (Heb 3:5) while distinguishing him from Christ, the Son over the house.
