Psalms 34

Taste and See That the LORD Is Good

Verses 1–10 — “I Sought the LORD and He Answered Me”
12345678910

Hebrew Word Study

טַעֲמוּ ṭaʿămū — taste: "Taste (ṭaʿămū) and see that the LORD is good; blessed is the one who takes refuge in him" (v.8). An invitation to experiential knowledge. God’s goodness is not argued but tasted — proven through direct encounter. Peter quotes this in 1 Peter 2:3.

Context

An acrostic psalm. David praises God at all times (v.1), tells of seeking and finding (v.4), testifies that the afflicted are heard (v.6), and issues the famous invitation: taste and see (v.8). "The lions may grow weak and hungry, but those who seek the LORD lack no good thing" (v.10).

Cross-Reference

  • 1 Pet 2:2–3"Like newborn babies, crave pure spiritual milk... now that you have tasted that the Lord is good" — Peter quoting Psalm 34:8.
  • 1 Sam 21:10–15David pretends to be insane before Abimelek/Achish — the superscription’s historical context.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
34:4

"I sought the LORD, and he answered me; he delivered me from all my fears." Simple testimony: sought, found, delivered.

34:8

"Taste and see that the LORD is good." Not "believe and see" but "taste and see." Experiential, sensory, personal. You have to try it to know it.

34:10

"The lions may grow weak and hungry, but those who seek the LORD lack no good thing." Even the mightiest predators experience lack. God’s seekers do not.

Alter — Literary Reading

Robert Alter, The Hebrew Bible: A Translation with Commentary — Scholarly Paraphrase
34:8

Alter: the imperative is plural: taste (you all) and see. The invitation is communal, not just individual.

Calvin’s Commentary

John Calvin, Commentaries — Faithful Paraphrase
34:8

Calvin: "taste and see" is the invitation to every uncertain soul. Calvin: you do not need to understand God fully before you experience God really. Taste first; theology follows.

NET Bible Notes

NET Bible Full Notes Edition — Biblical Studies Press
34:8

The NET: ṭaʿămū (taste) is the same verb used for Jonathan tasting honey (1 Sam 14:29). The analogy is physical: as honey proves its sweetness on the tongue, God proves his goodness to the seeker.

Kidner Notes

kidner
34:8

Kidner: "taste" is a daring word for theology. It demands experience, not just assent. God’s goodness is verifiable by encounter.

Vangemeren Notes

vangemeren
34:4–10

VanGemeren: the psalm alternates between testimony (what God did for David) and instruction (what God will do for all who seek). Private experience becomes public teaching.

Goldingay Notes

goldingay
34:8

Goldingay: "taste and see" bridges the gap between information and knowledge. You can know about food; tasting it is different. Theology becomes doxology through experience.

Verses 11–22 — “The LORD Is Close to the Brokenhearted”
111213141516171819202122

Hebrew Word Study

נִשְׁבְרֵי לֵב nishbərē-lēb — brokenhearted: "The LORD is close to the brokenhearted (nishbərē-lēb) and saves those who are crushed in spirit" (v.18). The most comforting verse in the Psalter for the suffering. God’s proximity is inversely proportional to human wholeness: the more broken you are, the closer God comes.

Context

David teaches the fear of the LORD (v.11), which consists in guarding the tongue, turning from evil, doing good, and seeking peace (vv.13–14). The LORD’s eyes and ears attend to the righteous (v.15). "The righteous person may have many troubles, but the LORD delivers him from them all" (v.19). "He protects all his bones, not one of them will be broken" (v.20) — John 19:36 applies this to Christ on the cross.

Cross-Reference

  • John 19:36"Not one of his bones will be broken" — applied to Christ at the crucifixion.
  • 1 Pet 3:10–12Peter quotes Psalm 34:12–16 for the Christian ethical life.
  • Isa 61:1"He has sent me to bind up the brokenhearted" — Christ’s mission defined by this psalm’s theology.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
34:18

"The LORD is close to the brokenhearted and saves those who are crushed in spirit." The promise: God’s presence intensifies in proportion to human brokenness. The most shattered person is nearest to God.

34:19

"The righteous person may have many troubles, but the LORD delivers him from them all." Not from HAVING troubles but from being destroyed BY them. Troubles are many; deliverances match.

34:20

"He protects all his bones, not one of them will be broken." Applied to Christ in John 19:36 — not a bone broken despite crucifixion.

Alter — Literary Reading

Robert Alter, The Hebrew Bible: A Translation with Commentary — Scholarly Paraphrase
34:18

Alter: "close" (qārōb) is spatial language for relational reality. God draws near physically, as it were, to the emotionally devastated.

Calvin’s Commentary

John Calvin, Commentaries — Faithful Paraphrase
34:18

Calvin: the brokenhearted have one advantage: they have stopped pretending. Calvin: God is close to the broken because the broken have nothing left to hide.

34:20

Calvin: the unbroken-bones promise finds its ultimate fulfilment at the cross. Calvin: the soldiers broke the legs of the two criminals but not Christ’s. Psalm 34:20 was being kept.

NET Bible Notes

NET Bible Full Notes Edition — Biblical Studies Press
34:18

The NET: nishbərē-lēb (broken of heart) uses the same root as "broken pottery" in Psalm 31:12. The breakage is real, not metaphorical.

34:20

The NET: John 19:36 cites this verse as fulfilled in Christ’s crucifixion. The Passover lamb’s unbroken bones (Exod 12:46) and this psalm’s promise converge at the cross.

Kidner Notes

kidner
34:18

Kidner: "close to the brokenhearted" reverses every expectation. God is not close to the successful, the strong, or the proud. He is close to the broken.

Vangemeren Notes

vangemeren
34:18–20

VanGemeren: the psalm’s theology of divine proximity to brokenness is foundational for pastoral care. God models where the church should be: near the shattered.

Goldingay Notes

goldingay
34:18

Goldingay: "crushed in spirit" (dak̅ʾē-rūaḥ) is the opposite of the proud spirit God resists. Brokenness is the qualification for nearness.
