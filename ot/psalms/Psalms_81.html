Psalms 81

Sing for Joy to God Our Strength

Verses 1–7 — “Blow the Trumpet at the New Moon”
1234567

Hebrew Word Study

שׁוֹפָר shōfār — trumpet / ram’s horn: "Sound the ram’s horn (shōfār) at the New Moon, and when the moon is full, on the day of our festival" (v.3). A festival psalm — likely Tabernacles or New Year. God removed the burden from Israel’s shoulder in Egypt (v.6), answered from the thundercloud (v.7), tested them at Meribah (v.7).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
81:1–3

"Sing for joy to God our strength. Begin the music, strike the timbrel, play the harp and lyre. Sound the ram’s horn." The fullest orchestra in the Psalter: voice, tambourine, harp, lyre, trumpet.

81:6–7

"I removed the burden from their shoulders. In your distress you called and I rescued you. I tested you at the waters of Meribah." God speaks in first person: I freed you, I answered you, I tested you.

Verses 8–16 — “If My People Would Only Listen to Me”
8910111213141516

Hebrew Word Study

לוּ lū — if only / oh that: "If my people would only listen (lū) to me, if Israel would only follow my ways!" (v.13). God’s lament over Israel. The divine "if only" — God wishes his people had obeyed. He would have subdued their enemies (v.14), fed them with finest wheat and honey from the rock (v.16). The psalm ends with God’s unfulfilled desire: "but my people would not listen" (v.11). Divine longing for obedience.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
81:10

"Open wide your mouth and I will fill it." God offers abundance; the only requirement is receptivity. Open the mouth — that’s all.

81:11–14

"But my people would not listen. If my people would only listen to me! How quickly I would subdue their enemies." The divine "if only." God’s grief over squandered provision.

81:16

"You would be fed with the finest of wheat; with honey from the rock I would satisfy you." The unreceived feast: finest wheat, rock-honey. All available; none taken.
