Psalms 106

Give Thanks to the LORD, for He Is Good

Verses 1–27 — “We Have Sinned, Even as Our Ancestors Did”
123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627

Hebrew Word Study

חָטְאוּ ḥāṭəʾū — we have sinned: "We have sinned (ḥāṭəʾū), even as our ancestors did; we have done wrong and acted wickedly" (v.6). The companion to Psalm 105: where 105 told God’s faithfulness, 106 tells Israel’s unfaithfulness. Same history, opposite perspective. At the Red Sea they rebelled (v.7); yet God saved them (v.8). They forgot his works (v.13), tested God (v.14), envied Moses (v.16), made a golden calf (v.19), forgot the God who saved them (v.21).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
106:1–2

"Give thanks to the LORD, for he is good; his love endures forever." The opening matches Psalm 107, 118, and 136 — the great ḥesed refrain.

106:6–7

"We have sinned. Our ancestors in Egypt did not consider your miracles; they did not remember your many kindnesses, and they rebelled at the sea." National confession. The sins are named and owned.

106:19–23

"At Horeb they made a calf and worshipped an idol cast from metal. They exchanged their glorious God for an image of a bull. So he said he would destroy them — had not Moses stood in the breach to turn his wrath away." The golden calf — the paradigmatic sin. Moses as intercessor stands between Israel and destruction.

Verses 28–48 — “Save Us, O LORD Our God” — Book IV Doxology
282930313233343536373839404142434445464748

Hebrew Word Study

הוֹשִׁיעֵנוּ hōshîʿēnū — save us: "Save us (hōshîʿēnū), LORD our God, and gather us from the nations, that we may give thanks to your holy name" (v.47). The sins continue: Baal Peor (v.28), Meribah (v.32), Canaan’s nations not destroyed (v.34), child sacrifice (v.37), defilement (v.39). Yet: "Many times he delivered them" (v.43). "He remembered his covenant" (v.45). "He caused all who held them captive to show them mercy" (v.46). BOOK IV DOXOLOGY: "Praise be to the LORD, the God of Israel, from everlasting to everlasting. Let all the people say, ‘Amen!’ Praise the LORD" (v.48). The fourth doxology.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
106:43–46

"Many times he delivered them, but they were bent on rebellion. Yet he took note of their distress. For their sake he remembered his covenant and out of his great love he relented." The pattern: sin, cry, rescue, repeat. God’s mercy outlasts Israel’s sin. His covenant memory is stronger than their rebellion habit.

106:47

"Save us, LORD our God, and gather us from the nations." The exile prayer: gather us. The psalm assumes Israel is scattered.

106:48

"Praise be to the LORD, the God of Israel, from everlasting to everlasting. Let all the people say, ‘Amen!’ Praise the LORD." Book IV’s closing doxology. The fourth of five. Despite 47 verses of national sin, the final word is praise.

People of the Chapter

Key biblical figure

Moses↗ People
Prophet, lawgiver, and covenant mediator
Moses stands at the centre of the Pentateuch’s narrative — the one man who spoke with God face to face (Exod 33:11; Num 12:8) and mediated the Sinai covenant to Israel. In Leviticus he receives the priestly legislation; in Numbers he leads, intercedes, and ultimately fails at Meribah (Num 20). The writer of Hebrews calls him "faithful in all God’s house" (Heb 3:5) while distinguishing him from Christ, the Son over the house.
