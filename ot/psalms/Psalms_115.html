Psalms 115

Not to Us, LORD, Not to Us

Verses 1–8 — “Their Idols Are Silver and Gold”
12345678

Hebrew Word Study

לֹא לָנוּ lōʾ lānū — not to us: "Not to us (lōʾ lānū), LORD, not to us but to your name be the glory" (v.1). The glory-deflection psalm: NOT US, but YOU. Then the idol-critique: they have mouths but cannot speak, eyes but cannot see, ears but cannot hear, noses but cannot smell, hands but cannot feel, feet but cannot walk (vv.4–7). "Those who make them will be like them, and so will all who trust in them" (v.8). You become what you worship.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
115:1

"Not to us, LORD, not to us but to your name be the glory." The double denial: not us, NOT us. All glory directed away from self toward God.

115:4–8

"Their idols are silver and gold, made by human hands. They have mouths but cannot speak. Those who make them will be like them." The most sustained idol-critique in the Psalter. The final line is the theology: you become what you worship. Worship idols, become senseless.

Verses 9–18 — “We Will Extol the LORD Both Now and Forevermore”
9101112131415161718

Hebrew Word Study

בָטַח bāṭaḥ — to trust: "All you Israelites, trust (bāṭaḥ) in the LORD — he is their help and shield" (v.9). Three groups summoned to trust: Israel (v.9), house of Aaron (v.10), those who fear the LORD (v.11). God remembers and will bless (v.12). "The highest heavens belong to the LORD, but the earth he has given to mankind" (v.16). The living praise God; the dead do not (v.17). "We will extol the LORD, both now and forevermore. Praise the LORD" (v.18).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
115:9–11

"Trust in the LORD — he is their help and shield." Three groups, one command, one identity: help and shield. Repeated three times for Israel, Aaron, and all who fear.

115:16

"The highest heavens belong to the LORD, but the earth he has given to mankind." The divine-human allocation: heaven is God’s; earth is humanity’s stewardship. Dominion (Gen 1:28) restated.

115:17–18

"It is not the dead who praise the LORD. But it is we who extol the LORD, both now and forevermore." The living have the privilege and responsibility of praise. Hallelujah.
