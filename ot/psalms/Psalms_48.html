Psalms 48

Great Is the LORD in the City of Our God

Verses 1–8 — “Mount Zion, Beautiful in Its Loftiness”
12345678

Hebrew Word Study

צִיוֹן Ṣiyyōn — Zion: "Great is the LORD, and most worthy of praise, in the city of our God, his holy mountain. Beautiful in its loftiness, the joy of the whole earth — Mount Zion (Ṣiyyōn)" (vv.1–2). A Zion hymn. The city is described as the joy of the whole earth, the city of the Great King. Kings assembled against it and were terrified (vv.4–6). God establishes it forever (v.8).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
48:1–2

"Beautiful in its loftiness, the joy of the whole earth — Mount Zion." The earthly Zion is a shadow of the heavenly one (Heb 12:22). The beauty is both physical and theological.

48:4–8

"Kings joined forces, but when they saw it, they were astounded; they fled in terror." Zion’s enemies are overwhelmed by God’s presence within it. The city is invincible because God is inside.

Verses 9–14 — “This God Is Our God for Ever and Ever”
91011121314

Hebrew Word Study

עוֹלָם ʿōlām — forever / eternity: "This God is our God for ever and ever (ʿōlām); he will be our guide even to the end" (v.14). The psalm’s closing confession: permanent possession of a permanent God. Walk around Zion, count her towers, note her ramparts (vv.12–13). Tell the next generation: this God — this specific God, the one of Zion — is ours forever.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
48:9–10

"Within your temple, O God, we meditate on your unfailing love. Like your name, O God, your praise reaches to the ends of the earth." Temple meditation produces worldwide testimony.

48:12–14

"Walk about Zion, count her towers. Note well her ramparts. Tell it to the next generation: this God is our God forever." Physical observation leads to theological confession leads to intergenerational transmission.
