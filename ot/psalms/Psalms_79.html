Psalms 79

The Nations Have Invaded Your Inheritance

Verses 1–7 — “They Have Laid Jerusalem in Ruins”
1234567

Hebrew Word Study

נַחֲלָה naḥălâ — inheritance / heritage: "O God, the nations have invaded your inheritance (naḥălâ); they have defiled your holy temple, they have reduced Jerusalem to rubble" (v.1). A communal lament over Jerusalem’s destruction (either 586 BC or another devastation). The dead are given to birds and beasts (v.2), blood poured out like water (v.3). Israel is scorned by neighbours (v.4). "How long, LORD? How long will your anger burn?" (v.5).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
79:1–3

"They have defiled your holy temple; they have reduced Jerusalem to rubble. The dead bodies of your servants have been given to birds; blood has been poured out like water." The devastation is total: temple, city, corpses, blood.

79:5

"How long, LORD? Will your jealousy burn like fire?" The "how long" cry — the default question of suffering Israel.

Verses 8–13 — “Help Us, God Our Saviour, for the Glory of Your Name”
8910111213

Hebrew Word Study

שֵׁם shēm — name: "Help us, God our Saviour, for the glory of your name (shēm)" (v.9). The appeal shifts: rescue us not for OUR sake but for YOUR name’s reputation. "Why should the nations say, ‘Where is their God?’" (v.10). God’s honour is at stake. "We your people, the sheep of your pasture, will praise you forever" (v.13).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
79:9–10

"Help us for the glory of your name. Why should the nations say, ‘Where is their God?’" The argument from divine reputation: God’s honour is bound to Israel’s fate.

79:13

"Then we your people, the sheep of your pasture, will praise you forever; from generation to generation we will proclaim your praise." The vow: rescue produces praise that lasts through generations. Suffering now; worship forever.
