Psalms 104

The Creation Hymn

Verses 1’18 — “He Wraps Himself in Light”
123456789101112131415161718

Hebrew Word Study

יְסוֹד yəsōd — he founded / he established: "He set the earth on its foundations (yəsōd); it can never be moved" (v.5). The Psalter’s great creation hymn, paralleling Genesis 1 in poetic form. God wraps himself in light (v.2), stretches out the heavens like a tent (v.2), walks on the wings of the wind (v.3). He set earth’s foundations (v.5), covered it with the deep (v.6), assigned the waters their boundary (v.9). Springs flow into valleys (v.10), birds nest by the waters (v.12), grass grows for cattle, plants for people (vv.14–15). Wine gladdens, oil makes faces shine, bread strengthens hearts (v.15). Trees and mountains, storks and wild goats, moon and sun — each has its place and purpose (vv.16–18).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
104:1–4

"Praise the LORD, my soul. LORD my God, you are very great; you are clothed with splendour and majesty. The LORD wraps himself in light as with a garment; he stretches out the heavens like a tent." Light is God’s clothing. The heavens are his tent. Creation is God’s wardrobe and architecture.

104:5–9

"He set the earth on its foundations; it can never be moved. You covered it with the watery depths. At your rebuke the waters fled. You set a boundary they cannot cross." Order from chaos: boundaries set, waters controlled, land established.

104:14–15

"He makes grass grow for the cattle, and plants for people to cultivate — bringing forth food from the earth: wine that gladdens human hearts, oil to make their faces shine, and bread that sustains their hearts." The theology of daily provision: wine (joy), oil (beauty), bread (strength). God provides delight, not just survival.

Verses 19–35 — “How Many Are Your Works, LORD!”
1920212223242526272829303132333435

Hebrew Word Study

לִוְיָתָן liwyāṭān — Leviathan: "There is the leviathan (liwyāṭān), which you formed to frolic there" (v.26). The moon marks seasons, the sun knows when to set (v.19). Darkness brings night-creatures; dawn sends them back and humans go to work (vv.20–23). "How many are your works, LORD! In wisdom you made them all" (v.24). The sea teems with creatures; Leviathan frolics in it (v.26). "All creatures look to you to give them their food at the proper time" (v.27). God’s breath gives life; withdrawing it brings death; sending it forth renews creation (vv.29–30). The psalm closes: "May my meditation be pleasing to him, as I rejoice in the LORD" (v.34).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
104:24

"How many are your works, LORD! In wisdom you made them all; the earth is full of your creatures." The exclamation at creation’s abundance: too many to count, all made wisely, filling the earth.

104:26

"There is the leviathan, which you formed to frolic there." Leviathan — the chaos-monster of Job 41 — is God’s pet that plays in the sea. Terror becomes toy. What Job feared, the psalmist celebrates.

104:29–30

"When you hide your face, they are terrified; when you take away their breath, they die. When you send your Spirit, they are created, and you renew the face of the ground." The Spirit as life-giver: breath withdrawn = death; breath sent = new creation. The cycle of life depends entirely on God’s Spirit.

104:35

"Praise the LORD, my soul. Praise the LORD." The psalm ends as it began: personal praise. Hallelujah — the first occurrence of this word in the Psalter.
