Psalms 68

May God Arise, Let His Enemies Be Scattered

Verses 1’18 — “A Father to the Fatherless”
123456789101112131415161718

Hebrew Word Study

רָכֶב rek̅eb — chariots: "The chariots (rek̅eb) of God are tens of thousands and thousands of thousands; the Lord has come from Sinai into his sanctuary" (v.17). The great processional psalm — the longest and most complex in the Psalter. God arises (v.1, echoing the ark-march of Num 10:35). He is a father to the fatherless, a defender of widows (v.5). He sets the lonely in families, leads out prisoners with singing (v.6). The Sinai theophany is recalled: earth shook, heavens poured rain (vv.7–8). God ascends the mountain with captives (v.18). Paul quotes v.18 in Ephesians 4:8 for Christ’s ascension.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
68:1

"May God arise, may his enemies be scattered." The ark-march cry (Num 10:35). God goes to war.

68:5–6

"A father to the fatherless, a defender of widows, is God in his holy dwelling. God sets the lonely in families, he leads forth the prisoners with singing." God’s first action: care for the vulnerable. Before the military triumph, the social justice.

68:17–18

"The chariots of God are tens of thousands. The Lord has come from Sinai into his sanctuary. When you ascended on high, you took many captives." Paul applies v.18 to Christ’s ascension (Eph 4:8): Christ ascended and gave gifts to humanity.

Verses 19–35 — “Praise Be to the Lord, Who Daily Bears Our Burdens”
1920212223242526272829303132333435

Hebrew Word Study

עֹז ʿōz — strength / power: "Proclaim the power (ʿōz) of God, whose majesty is over Israel, whose power is in the heavens" (v.34). God daily bears burdens (v.19), provides escape from death (v.20), and marches in procession into the sanctuary (vv.24–27). The tribes of Israel gather (v.27). Kings bring tribute (v.29). God is praised in the great assembly (v.26). "Sing to God, you kingdoms of the earth" (v.32). The psalm closes: "he gives power and strength to his people" (v.35).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
68:19

"Praise be to the Lord, to God our Saviour, who daily bears our burdens." Not once but DAILY. Every day’s weight is carried by God.

68:24–27

"Your procession, God, has come into view... the singers before, the musicians after, with them the young women playing timbrels." The processional scene: music, movement, celebration. All twelve tribes represented.

68:34–35

"Proclaim the power of God, whose majesty is over Israel, whose power is in the heavens. You, God, are awesome in your sanctuary; the God of Israel gives power and strength to his people." The psalm’s climactic doxology: heavenly power channelled to earthly people.
