Psalms 107

Give Thanks to the LORD, for He Is Good

Verses 1–22 — “Let the Redeemed of the LORD Tell Their Story”
12345678910111213141516171819202122

Hebrew Word Study

גְאוּלֵי gəʾūlē — redeemed ones: "Let the redeemed (gəʾūlē) of the LORD tell their story" (v.2). BOOK V OPENS (Psalms 107–150). The refrain psalm: four groups in trouble cry to God, and four times the psalm responds: "Then they cried out to the LORD in their trouble, and he delivered them" (vv.6, 13, 19, 28). "Let them give thanks to the LORD for his unfailing love" (vv.8, 15, 21, 31). The four groups: wanderers in the desert (vv.4–9), prisoners in darkness (vv.10–16), fools made sick by sin (vv.17–22), and sailors in a storm (vv.23–32).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
107:1–2

"Give thanks to the LORD, for he is good; his love endures forever. Let the redeemed of the LORD tell their story." The opening: gratitude + testimony. The redeemed have a story; they must tell it.

107:4–9

The first group: wanderers. Hungry, thirsty, life ebbing away. They cried; he delivered. He led them by a straight way to a city where they could settle.

107:10–16

The second group: prisoners. Darkness, deepest gloom, iron chains. They cried; he brought them out. He broke the gates of bronze and cut through bars of iron.

107:17–22

The third group: fools sick from sin. Their sin made them ill; they loathed all food. They cried; he sent his WORD and healed them. "Let them sacrifice thank offerings."

Verses 23–43 — “He Stilled the Storm to a Whisper”
232425262728293031323334353637383940414243

Hebrew Word Study

דְמָמָה dəmāmâ — calm / stillness / whisper: "He stilled the storm to a whisper (dəmāmâ); the waves of the sea were hushed" (v.29). The fourth group: sailors. Waves mount to the heavens and plunge to the depths; courage melts; they reel like drunkards (vv.25–27). They cried; he stilled the storm. He guided them to their desired haven (v.30). The psalm then shifts to wisdom reflection (vv.33–43): God turns rivers into desert and desert into pools; he settles the hungry there. "Let the one who is wise heed these things and ponder the loving deeds of the LORD" (v.43).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
107:23–30

The fourth group: sailors. "They went up to the heavens and down to the depths; in their peril their courage melted away. They cried; he stilled the storm to a whisper." The storm-stilling: Jesus in Mark 4:39 ("Quiet! Be still!") enacts Psalm 107.

107:31–32

"Let them give thanks to the LORD for his unfailing love. Let them exalt him in the assembly of the people." The fourth refrain. Each rescue demands public testimony.

107:43

"Let the one who is wise heed these things and ponder the loving deeds of the LORD." The psalm’s closing instruction: wisdom means noticing God’s pattern of rescue. Pay attention.
