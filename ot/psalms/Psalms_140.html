Psalms 140

Rescue Me, LORD, from Evildoers

Verses 1–7 — “Keep Me Safe from the Hands of the Violent”
1234567

Hebrew Word Study

חָמָס ḥāmās — violence: "Rescue me, LORD, from evildoers; protect me from the violent (ḥāmās)" (v.1). A David lament. The enemies devise evil (v.2), sharpen tongues like serpents, have cobra venom on their lips (v.3). "Keep me safe from the hands of the violent, who devise ways to trip my feet" (v.4). Hidden snares, ropes, nets along the path (v.5). David’s confession: "You are my God" (v.6). "Sovereign LORD, my strong deliverer, you shield my head in the day of battle" (v.7).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
140:1–3

"Rescue me from evildoers. They sharpen their tongues like serpents; the poison of vipers is on their lips." The serpent imagery: forked tongues, venom. Speech as poison.

140:6–7

"I say to the LORD, ‘You are my God.’ Sovereign LORD, my strong deliverer, you shield my head in the day of battle." The confession and the confidence: you are my God; you shield my head.

Verses 8–13 — “I Know That the LORD Secures Justice for the Poor”
8910111213

Hebrew Word Study

מִשְׁפָּט mishpāṭ — justice: "I know that the LORD secures justice (mishpāṭ) for the poor and upholds the cause of the needy" (v.12). David asks God to frustrate the wicked’s desires (v.8). May burning coals fall on them (v.10). The slanderer will not be established (v.11). The psalm’s theological declaration: "I KNOW that the LORD secures justice for the poor" (v.12). Not hope, not wish — I KNOW. "Surely the righteous will praise your name, and the upright will live in your presence" (v.13).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
140:10–11

"May burning coals fall on them; may they be thrown into the fire." The imprecation: fire for the fire-starters. Coals for those who heated hatred.

140:12–13

"I KNOW that the LORD secures justice for the poor. Surely the righteous will praise your name, and the upright will live in your presence." Confidence: justice will come. The poor will be vindicated. The righteous will dwell with God.
