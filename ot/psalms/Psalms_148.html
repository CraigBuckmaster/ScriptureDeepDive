Psalms 148

Praise the LORD from the Heavens

Verses 1–6 — “Praise Him, All His Angels”
123456

Hebrew Word Study

הַלְלוּ halləlū — praise: "Praise (halləlū) the LORD from the heavens; praise him in the heights above. Praise him, all his angels; praise him, all his heavenly hosts. Praise him, sun and moon; praise him, all you shining stars" (vv.1–3). THE cosmic praise psalm. The summons goes to heaven first: angels, hosts, sun, moon, stars, highest heavens, waters above the skies (vv.1–4). "Let them praise the name of the LORD, for at his command they were created" (v.5). Everything that exists was commanded into being and is therefore obligated to praise.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
148:1–4

"Praise him from the heavens. Praise him, angels. Praise him, sun and moon and stars. Praise him, highest heavens." The summons begins at the top of creation and works down. Celestial praise first, terrestrial praise next.

148:5–6

"Let them praise the name of the LORD, for at his command they were created. He set them in place for ever and ever; he issued a decree that will never pass away." Creation as permanent decree: what God commanded into existence stays. The stars obey forever.

Verses 7–14 — “Praise the LORD from the Earth”
7891011121314

Hebrew Word Study

הוֹד hōd — splendour / majesty: "He has raised up for his people a horn, the praise (hōd) of all his faithful servants" (v.14). Now the earthly summons: sea creatures and ocean depths (v.7), lightning and hail, snow and clouds, stormy winds (v.8), mountains and hills, fruit trees and cedars (v.9), wild animals and cattle, small creatures and flying birds (v.10), kings and all nations, princes and rulers (v.11), young men and women, old men and children (v.12). EVERYTHING: from sea-monsters to sparrows, from kings to children. "Let them praise the name of the LORD, for his name alone is exalted" (v.13). His splendour is above the earth and the heavens — he is bigger than both. Hallelujah.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
148:7–10

"Praise the LORD from the earth: sea creatures, lightning, hail, snow, clouds, wind, mountains, hills, trees, animals, birds." The catalogue descends: ocean → sky → land → vegetation → animals → birds. Nothing omitted.

148:11–13

"Kings and all nations, princes and rulers, young men and women, old men and children. Let them praise the name of the LORD, for his name alone is exalted." The human summons: every political rank, every gender, every age. Universal human praise added to universal natural praise.

148:14

"He has raised up for his people a horn — the praise of all his faithful servants, of Israel, the people close to his heart. Hallelujah." The psalm ends at Israel: among all praising creatures, Israel is closest to God’s heart.
