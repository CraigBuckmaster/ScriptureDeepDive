Psalms 109

Do Not Remain Silent, O God My Praise

Verses 1’15 — “Let His Days Be Few”
123456789101112131415

Hebrew Word Study

פְקֻדָּה pəquddâ — office / charge / position: "Let another take his office (pəquddâ)" (v.8). The harshest imprecatory psalm. David is attacked by liars (v.2), hated without cause (v.3), rewarded evil for good (v.5). The imprecations (vv.6–15): appoint an evil man over him, let his prayer condemn him, let his days be few, let another take his position (v.8 — Peter applies this to Judas in Acts 1:20), let his children be fatherless and his wife a widow. The ferocity reflects the scale of the betrayal.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
109:1–5

"They repay me evil for good and hatred for my friendship." The betrayal: David showed love and received hatred. The psalms of betrayal (41, 55, 109) share this pattern.

109:6–8

"Let an evil man be appointed over him. When he is tried, let him be found guilty. Let his days be few; let another take his position." Peter quotes v.8 in Acts 1:20 for replacing Judas.

109:14–15

"May the iniquity of his fathers be remembered. May their sins always remain before the LORD." The imprecation extends to family legacy. This is the psalm Christians find most difficult.

Verses 16–31 — “Help Me, LORD My God; Save Me”
16171819202122232425262728293031

Hebrew Word Study

חֶסֶד ḥesed — unfailing love: "Help me, LORD my God; save me according to your unfailing love (ḥesed)" (v.26). After the imprecations, David turns to God: "I am poor and needy; my heart is wounded within me" (v.22). "I fade away like an evening shadow; I am shaken off like a locust" (v.23). The appeal is to ḥesed (v.26). "Let them know that it is your hand, that you, LORD, have done it" (v.27). The psalm closes: "With my mouth I will greatly extol the LORD; in the great throng of worshippers I will praise him. For he stands at the right hand of the needy" (vv.30–31).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
109:21–22

"But you, Sovereign LORD, help me for your name’s sake. For I am poor and needy, and my heart is wounded within me." The personal confession: poverty, need, inner wound.

109:26–27

"Help me, LORD my God; save me according to your unfailing love. Let them know that it is YOUR hand." The appeal: let the rescue be visible, so enemies recognise God’s action.

109:30–31

"I will greatly extol the LORD. For he stands at the right hand of the needy." The closing image: God stands at the needy person’s right hand — the position of defence in court.
