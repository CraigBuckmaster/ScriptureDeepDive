Psalms 89

I Will Sing of the LORD’s Great Love Forever

Verses 1’37 — “I Will Maintain My Love to Him Forever”
12345678910111213141516171819202122232425262728293031323334353637

Hebrew Word Study

בְרִית bərîṭ — covenant: "You said, ‘I have made a covenant (bərîṭ) with my chosen one, I have sworn to David my servant’" (v.3). The Davidic covenant psalm. The first half celebrates God’s faithfulness (ḥesed appears 7 times) and the covenant with David: his throne endures, his line lasts, the heavens praise, God rules the sea, God’s arm is mighty. "I will maintain my love to him forever, and my covenant with him will never fail" (v.28). "His line will continue forever and his throne endure before me like the sun" (v.36).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
89:1–2

"I will sing of the LORD’s great love forever; with my mouth I will make your faithfulness known through all generations. I will declare that your love stands firm forever." The psalm opens with the strongest ḥesed-declaration in the Psalter.

89:3–4

"I have made a covenant with my chosen one. I will establish your line forever and make your throne firm through all generations." The Davidic covenant: dynasty and throne, forever.

89:34–37

"I will not violate my covenant or alter what my lips have uttered. His throne will endure before me like the sun; it will be established forever like the moon." God’s oath: unbreakable, unalterable, permanent.

Verses 38–52 — “But You Have Rejected, You Have Spurned” — Book III Doxology
383940414243444546474849505152

Hebrew Word Study

זָנַחְתָ zānaḥtā — you have rejected / spurned: "But you have rejected (zānaḥtā), you have spurned, you have been very angry with your anointed one" (v.38). The most dramatic turn in the Psalter. After 37 verses celebrating the eternal Davidic covenant, the psalm pivots to devastation: the covenant seems broken, the crown defiled, the walls breached, the throne cast to the ground (vv.39–45). "How long, LORD? Will you hide yourself forever?" (v.46). The crisis: if the covenant is forever, why has the dynasty collapsed? This is the theological crisis of the exile. BOOK III DOXOLOGY: "Praise be to the LORD forever! Amen and Amen" (v.52). The third of five doxologies — spoken in the darkest moment.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
89:38–45

"But you have rejected your anointed. You have renounced the covenant. You have hurled his crown to the ground." The reversal: everything vv.1–37 celebrated is now contradicted. The covenant seems voided.

89:46–49

"How long, LORD? Will you hide yourself forever? Remember how fleeting is my life." The plea from within the crisis: the covenant stands on God’s word, not on current circumstances.

89:52

"Praise be to the LORD forever! Amen and Amen." Book III’s closing doxology. Remarkably, it follows the darkest lament. Praise survives the collapse of the dynasty. The doxology is an act of defiant faith.
