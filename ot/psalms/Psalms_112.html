Psalms 112

Blessed Is the One Who Fears the LORD

Verses 1–5 — “Their Righteousness Endures Forever”
12345

Hebrew Word Study

צַדִּיק ṣaddîq — righteous one: "Hallelujah! Blessed is the one who fears the LORD. Their righteousness (ṣaddîq) endures forever" (vv.1, 3). The companion to Psalm 111: where 111 describes God’s character, 112 describes the God-fearer’s character. Also an acrostic. The God-fearer: delights in commands (v.1), mighty descendants (v.2), wealth in the house (v.3), light in darkness (v.4), generous and just (v.5).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
112:1–2

"Blessed is the one who fears the LORD, who finds great delight in his commands. Their children will be mighty in the land." The fear of the LORD produces blessed families across generations.

112:4–5

"Even in darkness light dawns for the upright, for those who are gracious and compassionate and righteous." Light in darkness: the upright are not immune to darkness but they have light within it.

Verses 6–10 — “They Will Have No Fear of Bad News”
678910

Hebrew Word Study

לֹא יִירָא lōʾ yîrāʾ — they will not fear: "They will have no fear (lōʾ yîrāʾ) of bad news; their hearts are steadfast, trusting in the LORD" (v.7). Fearlessness rooted in trust. The righteous are secure (v.6), generous to the poor (v.9), their horn lifted high (v.9). The wicked see this and are grieved; they gnash their teeth (v.10). The contrast: the righteous endure; the wicked’s longings come to nothing.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
112:7–8

"They will have no fear of bad news; their hearts are steadfast, trusting in the LORD. Their hearts are secure, they will have no fear." The double "no fear": bad news does not destabilise because trust is the foundation.

112:9–10

"They have freely scattered their gifts to the poor; their righteousness endures forever. The wicked will see and be vexed." Paul quotes v.9 in 2 Corinthians 9:9 for Christian generosity. Scattering (giving) is how righteousness endures.

Moses↗ People
Prophet, lawgiver, and covenant mediator
Moses stands at the centre of the Pentateuch’s narrative — the one man who spoke with God face to face (Exod 33:11; Num 12:8) and mediated the Sinai covenant to Israel. In Leviticus he receives the priestly legislation; in Numbers he leads, intercedes, and ultimately fails at Meribah (Num 20). The writer of Hebrews calls him "faithful in all God’s house" (Heb 3:5) while distinguishing him from Christ, the Son over the house.
