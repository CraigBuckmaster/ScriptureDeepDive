Psalms 96

Sing to the LORD a New Song

Verses 1–6 — “Declare His Glory Among the Nations”
123456

Hebrew Word Study

שִׁיר חָדָשׁ shîr ḥādāsh — new song: "Sing to the LORD a new song (shîr ḥādāsh); sing to the LORD, all the earth" (v.1). Three times "sing to the LORD" in two verses. Proclaim his salvation daily (v.2). Declare his glory among the nations (v.3). The gods of the nations are idols (ʾĕlîlîm), but the LORD made the heavens (v.5). Splendour and majesty before him; strength and glory in his sanctuary (v.6). Parallel to 1 Chronicles 16:23–33.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
96:1–3

"Sing to the LORD a new song. Proclaim his salvation day after day. Declare his glory among the nations." Daily proclamation, global declaration. Worship is missionary.

96:4–5

"Great is the LORD and most worthy of praise; he is to be feared above all gods. For all the gods of the nations are idols, but the LORD made the heavens." The comparison: idols vs Creator. No contest.

Verses 7–13 — “Let the Earth Be Glad”
78910111213

Hebrew Word Study

בְצֶדֶק bəṣedeq — in righteousness: "He will judge the world in righteousness (bəṣedeq) and the peoples in his faithfulness" (v.13). The call goes cosmic: let the heavens rejoice, let the earth be glad (v.11). Let the sea resound, the fields be jubilant, the trees of the forest sing (vv.11–12). Creation worships because the Judge is coming. "He comes to judge the earth" (v.13). Judgment is not feared but celebrated — because this Judge is righteous and faithful.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
96:10–12

"Say among the nations, ‘The LORD reigns.’ Let the heavens rejoice, let the earth be glad, let the sea resound, let the fields be jubilant, let all the trees of the forest sing for joy." The most comprehensive worship summons in the Psalter: heaven, earth, sea, fields, forests — all join.

96:13

"He comes to judge the earth. He will judge the world in righteousness and the peoples in his faithfulness." Judgment is good news. When the Judge is righteous, his coming produces celebration, not terror.
