Psalms 85

Will You Not Revive Us Again?

Verses 1–7 — “Restore Us Again, God Our Saviour”
1234567

Hebrew Word Study

תְחַיֵנוּ təḥayyēnū — revive us / give us life again: "Will you not revive us (təḥayyēnū) again, that your people may rejoice in you?" (v.6). The community recalls past restoration: God forgave sin, covered transgressions, set aside wrath (vv.1–3). Now they ask: do it again. Revive us AGAIN. The request assumes God has done this before and can repeat it.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
85:1–3

"You restored the fortunes of Jacob; you forgave the iniquity of your people." Past grace as argument for present grace.

85:6–7

"Will you not revive us again? Show us your unfailing love and grant us your salvation." Revival = life restored. The path: ḥesed → salvation → joy.

Verses 8–13 — “Love and Faithfulness Meet Together”
8910111213

Hebrew Word Study

חֶסֶד ḥesed — love / steadfast love: "Love (ḥesed) and faithfulness meet together; righteousness and peace kiss each other" (v.10). The psalm’s most famous verse — four divine attributes personified and reunited: love + faithfulness, righteousness + peace. In the fall, these were torn apart (you could have justice without mercy, or mercy without justice). At the cross, they are reconciled: all four meet and kiss.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
85:10

"Love and faithfulness meet together; righteousness and peace kiss each other." The four attributes of God that seemed to conflict are harmonised. The cross is where this verse finds its ultimate fulfilment: justice satisfied AND mercy extended.

85:11–12

"Faithfulness springs forth from the earth, and righteousness looks down from heaven. The LORD will indeed give what is good." Earth and heaven meet: faithfulness rises, righteousness descends. The vertical reunion.

85:13

"Righteousness goes before him and prepares the way for his steps." Righteousness as God’s advance party. The way is prepared.
