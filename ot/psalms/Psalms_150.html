Psalms 150

Let Everything That Has Breath Praise the LORD

Verses 1–3 — “Praise God in His Sanctuary”
123

Hebrew Word Study

הַלְלוּ halləlū — praise: "Praise God in his sanctuary; praise him in his mighty heavens. Praise him for his acts of power; praise him for his surpassing greatness" (vv.1–2). THE FINAL PSALM. THE PSALTER’S LAST WORD. The location: sanctuary + heavens (v.1). The reasons: power + greatness (v.2). The instruments begin: trumpet, harp, lyre (v.3). Everything is building toward the final verse.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
150:1–2

"Praise God in his sanctuary; praise him in his mighty heavens." Two locations: the temple on earth and the throne in heaven. Praise fills both simultaneously.

150:3

"Praise him with the sounding of the trumpet, praise him with the harp and lyre." The orchestra assembles. Trumpet: the call. Harp and lyre: the melody. More instruments follow.

Verses 4–6 — “LET EVERYTHING THAT HAS BREATH PRAISE THE LORD”
456

Hebrew Word Study

נְשָׁמָה nəshāmâ — breath / everything that breathes: "LET EVERYTHING THAT HAS BREATH (nəshāmâ) PRAISE THE LORD" (v.6). More instruments: timbrel and dancing, strings and pipe, resounding cymbals, clashing cymbals (vv.4–5). Every instrument the temple possessed is summoned. Then the final command — the Psalter’s last sentence: "Let everything that has breath praise the LORD." Not just Israel, not just the nations, not just nature — everything that BREATHES. The nəshāmâ is the breath God breathed into Adam (Gen 2:7). Every living thing exists because God breathed. Every breath is borrowed from God. The only appropriate use of that breath: praise. HALLELUJAH. The Psalter is complete.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
150:4–5

"Praise him with timbrel and dancing, praise him with strings and pipe, praise him with resounding cymbals, praise him with clashing cymbals." Six instruments + dancing: timbrel, dance, strings, pipe, soft cymbals, loud cymbals. The full orchestra. Every sound-maker in the temple.

150:6

"LET EVERYTHING THAT HAS BREATH PRAISE THE LORD. HALLELUJAH." The Psalter’s final word. The scope: everything breathing. The reason: God gave the breath. The response: return it as praise. The word: Hallelujah — the last word of the last psalm. The 150-psalm journey from "Blessed is the one" (Ps 1:1) to "Let everything praise" (Ps 150:6). From individual blessing to universal worship. From the tree by water to the breath of every creature. The Psalter is an arc from one person’s Torah-delight to all creation’s hallelujah.
