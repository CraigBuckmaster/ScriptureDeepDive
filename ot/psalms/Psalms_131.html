Psalms 131

My Heart Is Not Proud

Verses 1–2 — “I Have Calmed and Quieted Myself Like a Weaned Child”
12

Hebrew Word Study

גָּמַל gāmal — weaned: "I have calmed and quieted myself, like a weaned child (gāmal) with its mother; like a weaned child I am content" (v.2). Three verses of humility. David’s heart is not proud, his eyes not haughty (v.1). He does not concern himself with great matters beyond him (v.1). The weaned child image: not nursing (demanding), but resting (content). The child no longer needs the breast; it rests simply because the mother is near. Presence, not provision, is enough.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
131:1

"My heart is not proud, LORD; my eyes are not haughty. I do not concern myself with great matters." Three negatives: not proud, not haughty, not overreaching. The psalm of chosen smallness.

131:2

"Like a weaned child with its mother." The weaned child has stopped demanding and started resting. Contentment is not getting what you want but wanting what you have: the mother’s presence.

Verse 3 — “Israel, Put Your Hope in the LORD”
3

Hebrew Word Study

יַחֵל yaḥēl — to hope / to wait: "Israel, put your hope (yaḥēl) in the LORD both now and forevermore" (v.3). One verse of national application. David’s personal humility becomes the nation’s counsel: hope in the LORD. The smallest psalm of the Ascents (3 verses) carries one of the deepest theologies: contentment through chosen smallness.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
131:3

"Israel, put your hope in the LORD both now and forevermore." The personal (vv.1–2) becomes communal (v.3). David’s quieted soul models what Israel should be.
