Psalms 49

The Redemption of a Soul Is Costly

Verses 1–12 — “No One Can Redeem the Life of Another”
123456789101112

Hebrew Word Study

פֶּדֶה fedeh — ransom / redemption price: "No one can redeem (fedeh) the life of another or give to God a ransom for them — the ransom for a life is costly, no payment is ever enough" (vv.7–8). A wisdom psalm on death and wealth. Wise and foolish alike die (v.10). Their graves are their eternal homes (v.11). No amount of wealth can buy off death. "People who have wealth but lack understanding are like the beasts that perish" (v.12).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
49:7–9

"No one can redeem the life of another. The ransom for a life is costly, no payment is ever enough — so that they should live on forever and not see decay." Human wealth cannot purchase immortality. Only God can pay the ransom — the cross is the answer this psalm cannot yet see.

49:10–12

"Wise and foolish alike die. Their tombs will remain their houses forever. People who have wealth but lack understanding are like beasts that perish." Death is the great equaliser, echoing Ecclesiastes 2:14–16.

Verses 13–20 — “But God Will Redeem Me from the Realm of the Dead”
1314151617181920

Hebrew Word Study

יִפְדֵנִי yifdēnî — he will redeem me: "But God will redeem me (yifdēnî) from the realm of the dead; he will surely take me to himself" (v.15). The psalm’s turning point. No HUMAN can pay the ransom (v.7), but GOD will redeem from Sheol. The word "take" (lāqaḥ) is the same verb used for Enoch’s translation (Gen 5:24) and Elijah’s assumption (2 Kgs 2:3). God will take the psalmist beyond death.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
49:15

"But God will redeem me from the realm of the dead; he will surely take me to himself." The psalm’s theological centre. What humans cannot do (v.7), God does (v.15). The ransom no person can pay, God pays. The cross is the ransom.

49:16–20

"Do not be overawed when others grow rich. For they will take nothing with them when they die." The conclusion echoes Job 1:21 and Ecclesiastes 5:15: naked we came, naked we depart. But the redeemed have v.15: God takes them to himself.
