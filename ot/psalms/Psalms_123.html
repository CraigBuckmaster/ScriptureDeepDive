Psalms 123

I Lift My Eyes to You

Verses 1–2 — “As the Eyes of Slaves Look to Their Master”
12

Hebrew Word Study

עֵינֵינוּ ʿēnēnū — our eyes: "I lift my eyes (our eyes, ʿēnēnū) to you, to you who sit enthroned in heaven. As the eyes of slaves look to the hand of their master, as the eyes of a female slave look to the hand of her mistress, so our eyes look to the LORD our God, till he shows us his mercy" (vv.1–2). The gaze psalm. Eyes fixed on God as a servant watches the master’s hand for the slightest signal. Complete attentiveness.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
123:1–2

"As the eyes of slaves look to the hand of their master, so our eyes look to the LORD our God." The analogy: servants watch the master’s hand for instruction, provision, or dismissal. The pilgrim watches God for mercy.

Verses 3–4 — “We Have Endured No End of Contempt”
34

Hebrew Word Study

בוּז būz — contempt / scorn: "We have endured no end of contempt (būz). We have endured no end of ridicule from the arrogant, of contempt from the proud" (vv.3–4). The occasion for the upward gaze: suffering. The proud and arrogant mock; the pilgrims endure. The psalm is only four verses: two of watching, two of suffering. The watching is the response to the suffering.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
123:3–4

"Have mercy on us, LORD, have mercy. We have endured no end of ridicule from the arrogant." The double mercy plea. The endurance is real; the mercy is needed. Four verses: fixed gaze + suffered contempt = plea for mercy.
