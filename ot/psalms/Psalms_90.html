Psalms 90

A Prayer of Moses: Teach Us to Number Our Days

Verses 1’11 — “A Thousand Years Are Like a Day”
1234567891011

Hebrew Word Study

מָעוֹן māʿōn — dwelling place / refuge: "Lord, you have been our dwelling place (māʿōn) throughout all generations" (v.1). BOOK IV OPENS (Psalms 90–106). The only psalm attributed to Moses — the oldest in the Psalter. God is eternal; humans are dust returned (v.3). "A thousand years in your sight are like a day that has just gone by" (v.4). Peter quotes this in 2 Peter 3:8. We are swept away like a dream, like grass that springs up in the morning and withers by evening (vv.5–6). "Who knows the power of your anger?" (v.11). The brevity of life against the infinity of God.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
90:1–2

"Lord, you have been our dwelling place throughout all generations. Before the mountains were born, you are God." Eternal refuge: God precedes creation.

90:3–4

"You turn people back to dust. A thousand years in your sight are like a day." The scale differential: God’s time and human time are incommensurable. Peter quotes v.4 (2 Pet 3:8).

90:5–6

"People are like the new grass of the morning — in the morning it springs up new, but by evening it is dry and withered." The grass metaphor: humanity’s entire lifespan is one day of vegetation.

90:10

"Our days may come to seventy years, or eighty, if our strength endures; yet the best of them are but trouble and sorrow." The honest assessment of a long life: mostly difficulty.

Verses 12–17 — “Teach Us to Number Our Days”
121314151617

Hebrew Word Study

חָכְמָה ḥāk̅mâ — wisdom: "Teach us to number our days, that we may gain a heart of wisdom (ḥāk̅mâ)" (v.12). The psalm’s most quoted verse and its theological heart. Numbering days is not counting them but weighing them. Awareness of mortality produces wisdom. Moses asks: satisfy us in the morning with ḥesed (v.14), make us glad for as many days as we have been afflicted (v.15). "May the favour of the Lord our God rest on us; establish the work of our hands for us" (v.17).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
90:12

"Teach us to number our days, that we may gain a heart of wisdom." Mortality-awareness as the path to wisdom. Ecclesiastes and Psalm 90 share this conviction: knowing you will die teaches you how to live.

90:14

"Satisfy us in the morning with your unfailing love, that we may sing for joy and be glad all our days." The request: let ḥesed meet us at dawn. If the morning is satisfied, the day is redeemed.

90:17

"May the favour of the Lord our God rest on us; establish the work of our hands." The prayer for significance: let our brief work have lasting impact. Establish what our mortality would erase.

Chapter-Level Scholarship

People of the Chapter

God
Key biblical figure
Before the mountains were born, you are God…
Moses↗ People
Prophet, lawgiver, and covenant mediator
Moses stands at the centre of the Pentateuch’s narrative — the one man who spoke with God face to face (Exod 33:11; Num 12:8) and mediated the Sinai covenant to Israel. In Leviticus he receives the priestly legislation; in Numbers he leads, intercedes, and ultimately fails at Meribah (Num 20). The writer of Hebrews calls him "faithful in all God’s house" (Heb 3:5) while distinguishing him from Christ, the Son over the house.
