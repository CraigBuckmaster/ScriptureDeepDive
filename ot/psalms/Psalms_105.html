Psalms 105

He Remembers His Covenant Forever

Verses 1–22 — “The Covenant with Abraham”
12345678910111213141516171819202122

Hebrew Word Study

בְרִית bərîṭ — covenant: "He remembers his covenant (bərîṭ) forever, the promise he made, for a thousand generations — the covenant he made with Abraham, the oath he swore to Isaac" (vv.8–9). The great history-of-salvation psalm. Give thanks, make known, sing, tell (vv.1–2). Remember his wonders (v.5). He is the LORD our God; his judgments are in all the earth (v.7). The story: Abraham called (v.6), covenant established (vv.8–11), patriarchs protected (vv.12–15 — "Do not touch my anointed ones"), Joseph sent ahead (vv.17–22).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
105:1–4

"Give thanks to the LORD, proclaim his name; make known among the nations what he has done." The opening commands: thanksgiving, proclamation, seeking. Worship as missional activity.

105:8–11

"He remembers his covenant forever — the covenant with Abraham. ‘To you I will give the land of Canaan.’" Covenant memory: God does not forget. The Abrahamic promise is the psalm’s theological engine.

105:16–22

"He called down famine on the land. He sent a man before them — Joseph, sold as a slave. Till what he foretold came to pass, the word of the LORD proved him true." Joseph: the suffering that preceded provision. The word tested Joseph before it vindicated him.

Verses 23–45 — “That They Might Keep His Precepts”
2324252627282930313233343536373839404142434445

Hebrew Word Study

הַלְלוּיָה halləlū-yāh — praise the LORD / hallelujah: "Praise the LORD (halləlū-yāh)!" (v.45). The history continues: Israel in Egypt (v.23), plagues recounted (vv.28–36), exodus (v.37), wilderness provision (vv.39–41), entry into the promised land (v.44). The purpose: "that they might keep his precepts and observe his laws" (v.45). History’s goal: obedience. Salvation is FOR faithfulness, not merely FROM trouble.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
105:37

"He brought out Israel, laden with silver and gold, and from among their tribes no one faltered." The exodus: no one stumbled. The entire nation walked out intact, enriched.

105:39–41

"He spread out a cloud as a covering, and a fire to give light at night. They asked, and he brought them quail; he fed them well with the bread of heaven." Cloud, fire, quail, manna. The wilderness provision catalogue.

105:44–45

"He gave them the lands of the nations. That they might keep his precepts. Hallelujah!" The purpose clause: the land was given for obedience. Gift and responsibility. Grace and law. Hallelujah.
