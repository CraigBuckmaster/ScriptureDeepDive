Psalms 17

A Prayer for Vindication

Verses 1–7 — “Test Me and Find Nothing”
1234567

Hebrew Word Study

צֶדֶק ṣedeq — righteousness / just cause: "Hear me, LORD, my plea is just (ṣedeq)" (v.1). David opens with a claim of righteousness — not sinlessness but innocence in this specific case. He invites God’s examination: "test me and you will find nothing" (v.3).

Context

David asks God to hear his righteous cause (v.1), examine his heart by night (v.3), and protect him like the apple of God’s eye (v.8). He has kept his feet from wrong paths (v.5). The psalm is a prayer for vindication against specific enemies.

Cross-Reference

  • Ps 26:1–2"Vindicate me, LORD... test me" — the same examination-invitation.
  • Deut 32:10"He guarded him as the apple of his eye" — the same intimate protection image.

Verses 8–15 — “Keep Me as the Apple of Your Eye”
89101112131415

Hebrew Word Study

אִישׁוֹן בַת ʾishōn bat — apple of the eye / pupil (lit. "little man of the daughter [of the eye]"): "Keep me as the apple of your eye (ʾishōn bat); hide me in the shadow of your wings" (v.8). The "apple of the eye" is the pupil — the most reflexively protected part of the body. David asks to be guarded as instinctively as the eye guards its centre. The "shadow of wings" adds the mother-bird image.

Context

David asks for the most intimate protection: apple of the eye, shadow of wings (v.8). The enemies are described as fat, arrogant, lion-like (vv.10–12). David asks God to confront them (v.13). The psalm’s closing contrast: the wicked have their reward in this life; David will be satisfied "with seeing your face when I awake" (v.15) — another hint of resurrection.

Cross-Reference

  • Zech 2:8"Whoever touches you touches the apple of his eye" — the same intimate protection language applied to Israel.
  • Ruth 2:12"Under whose wings you have come to take refuge" — the same wing-shelter image.
  • 1 John 3:2"When Christ appears, we shall be like him, for we shall see him as he is" — seeing God’s face on waking.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
17:8

"Keep me as the apple of your eye; hide me in the shadow of your wings." Double protection imagery: the most instinctive (eye-guarding) and the most tender (wing-sheltering).

17:14–15

"People of this world whose reward is in this life... as for me, I will be vindicated and will see your face; when I awake, I will be satisfied with seeing your likeness." The ultimate contrast: they have their reward now; David’s reward is seeing God’s face after death.

Alter — Literary Reading

Robert Alter, The Hebrew Bible: A Translation with Commentary — Scholarly Paraphrase
17:8

Alter: "apple of the eye" literally "ʾishōn-bat-ʿayin" — "little man, daughter of the eye." The pupil reflects a tiny image of whoever looks into it. David asks to be reflected in God’s eye.

Calvin’s Commentary

John Calvin, Commentaries — Faithful Paraphrase
17:15

Calvin: "when I awake" is the resurrection morning. Calvin: the contrast with v.14 is eschatological: the wicked have their belly-full in this life; the righteous will be satisfied only when they see God face to face.

NET Bible Notes

NET Bible Full Notes Edition — Biblical Studies Press
17:8

The NET: "apple of the eye" is idiomatic for the most precious, most protected thing. The pupil is automatically shielded by the eyelid at any threat.

17:15

The NET: "when I awake" (ḥāqîṣ) can mean waking from sleep or waking from death. The context (contrasted with the wicked’s this-life reward) favours the latter.

Kidner Notes

kidner
17:15

Kidner: "when I awake" suggests resurrection: the righteous person wakes to God’s presence. The wicked had their portion in this life; the righteous have theirs in the next.

Vangemeren Notes

vangemeren
17:15

VanGemeren: "seeing your face" when awakened connects to Psalm 16’s resurrection hope. The Psalter’s theology of the afterlife develops through these early psalms.

Goldingay Notes

goldingay
17:15

Goldingay: "satisfied with seeing your likeness" — the satisfaction the wicked found in wealth (v.14), the righteous find in God’s face. Different currencies of fulfilment.
