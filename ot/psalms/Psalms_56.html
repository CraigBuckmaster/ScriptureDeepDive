Psalms 56

When I Am Afraid, I Put My Trust in You

Verses 1–7 — “In God I Trust; I Am Not Afraid”
1234567

Hebrew Word Study

בָטַחְתִּי bāṭaḥtî — I trust / I have trusted: "When I am afraid, I put my trust (bāṭaḥtî) in you" (v.3). Written when the Philistines seized David in Gath (1 Sam 21:10–15). The psalm’s refrain: "In God, whose word I praise, in God I trust and am not afraid. What can mere mortals do to me?" (vv.4, 10–11). Fear is acknowledged ("when I am afraid"), then overcome by trust. Fear is not denied; it is redirected.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
56:3–4

"When I am afraid, I put my trust in you. In God, whose word I praise — in God I trust; I am not afraid. What can mere mortals do to me?" The sequence: fear acknowledged → trust chosen → fear defeated. The refrain is the psalm’s heartbeat.

56:5–7

"All day long they twist my words; all their schemes are for my ruin." The enemy’s weapon is distortion of speech. They conspire, lurk, watch David’s steps.

Verses 8–13 — “Record My Misery; List My Tears on Your Scroll”
8910111213

Hebrew Word Study

נֹדִי nōdî — my wanderings / my tossings: "Record my misery; list my tears on your scroll (nōdî) — are they not in your record?" (v.8). God keeps a book of tears. Every tear is catalogued. David’s suffering is not anonymous — it is documented by God. The theology: nothing is wasted; every grief is recorded.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
56:8

"Record my misery; list my tears on your scroll." The tear-bottle or tear-scroll: God preserves every tear as evidence, testimony, and memory. Nothing is lost.

56:9

"Then my enemies will turn back when I call for help. By this I will know that God is for me." The knowledge: God is FOR me. Not neutral, not distant — for me.

56:12–13

"I am under vows to you, my God. For you have delivered me from death and my feet from stumbling, that I may walk before God in the light of life." The destination: the light of life. Death averted, stumbling prevented, walking in light.
