Psalms 25

An Acrostic Prayer for Guidance

Verses 1–11 — “Show Me Your Ways”
1234567891011

Hebrew Word Study

דְרָכֶיךָ dərāk̅ek̅ā — your ways / your paths: "Show me your ways (dərāk̅ek̅ā), LORD, teach me your paths" (v.4). An alphabetic acrostic prayer. David asks for guidance, forgiveness, and instruction. Key themes: trust (v.2), teaching (vv.4–5), mercy (v.6), forgiveness (v.7), humility (v.9).

Context

The first acrostic psalm. Each verse begins with a successive letter of the Hebrew alphabet. David asks God to lead, teach, guide, and forgive. The mood is humble and teachable.

Cross-Reference

  • Prov 3:5–6"Trust in the LORD with all your heart... he will make your paths straight" — the wisdom parallel.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
25:4–5

"Show me your ways, teach me your paths, guide me in your truth." Three requests: show, teach, guide. David wants direction, not just information.

25:6–7

"Remember your mercy and love, for they are from of old. Do not remember the sins of my youth." Mercy remembered; sins forgotten. David asks God for selective memory.

Verses 12–22 — “The LORD Confides in Those Who Fear Him”
1213141516171819202122

Hebrew Word Study

סוֹד sōd — counsel / intimate friendship / secret: "The LORD confides (sōd) in those who fear him" (v.14). Sōd is intimate counsel — the inner circle’s conversation. God shares his secrets with the reverent. Fear of the LORD produces not distance but intimacy.

Context

God guides the humble (v.9), confides in the God-fearing (v.14), frees from snares (v.15). David’s eyes are always on the LORD (v.15). The psalm closes with a plea for Israel’s redemption (v.22).

Cross-Reference

  • John 15:15"I no longer call you servants... I have called you friends" — the NT fulfilment of God’s intimate counsel.
  • Prov 3:32"He takes the upright into his confidence" — the same sōd theology.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
25:14

"The LORD confides in those who fear him." The paradox: fear produces intimacy. Reverence opens the door to friendship.

25:15

"My eyes are ever on the LORD." Sustained attention. David’s gaze is fixed. The direction of the eyes determines the direction of the life.
