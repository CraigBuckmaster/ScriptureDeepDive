Psalms 143

Hear My Prayer, O LORD

Verses 1–6 — “No One Living Is Righteous Before You”
123456

Hebrew Word Study

צַדִּיק ṣaddîq — righteous: "Do not bring your servant into judgment, for no one living is righteous (ṣaddîq) before you" (v.2). The SEVENTH and final penitential psalm (6, 32, 38, 51, 102, 130, 143). David appeals NOT to his own righteousness but to God’s faithfulness (v.1). No one is righteous before God — the same theology as Psalm 14:3 and Romans 3:10. His spirit grows faint, his heart dismayed (v.4). He remembers God’s former deeds, meditates, reaches out like parched land (vv.5–6).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
143:1–2

"Hear my prayer. In your faithfulness and righteousness come to my relief. Do not bring your servant into judgment, for no one living is righteous before you." The appeal is to GOD’s righteousness, not David’s. Universal sinfulness acknowledged.

143:5–6

"I remember the days of long ago; I meditate on all your works. I spread out my hands to you; I thirst for you like a parched land." Memory, meditation, outstretched hands, thirst: four postures of desperate seeking.

Verses 7–12 — “Teach Me to Do Your Will”
789101112

Hebrew Word Study

רוּחֲךָ rūaḥăk̅ā — your Spirit: "Let your good Spirit (rūaḥăk̅ā) lead me on level ground" (v.10). Seven requests in rapid succession: answer quickly (v.7), show love in the morning (v.8), rescue from enemies (v.9), teach me to do your will (v.10), let your good Spirit lead me (v.10), preserve my life (v.11), silence my enemies (v.12). "Teach me to do your will, for you are my God" (v.10) — the request that summarises all penitential psalms: not just forgiveness but transformation.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
143:8

"Let the morning bring me word of your unfailing love. Show me the way I should go, for to you I entrust my life." Morning ḥesed: the first light brings the first word from God. Direction flows from entrustment.

143:10

"Teach me to do your will, for you are my God; may your good Spirit lead me on level ground." The final penitential psalm’s request: not just pardon but obedience. Not just mercy but transformation. The Spirit as guide on level ground.
