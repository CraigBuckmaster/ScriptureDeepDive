Psalms 67

May God Be Gracious to Us and Bless Us

Verses 1–4 — “May Your Ways Be Known on Earth”
1234

Hebrew Word Study

יְבָרֶכְנוּ yəbārek̅ēnū — may he bless us: "May God be gracious to us and bless us (yəbārek̅ēnū) and make his face shine on us — so that your ways may be known on earth, your salvation among all nations" (vv.1–2). The Aaronic blessing (Num 6:24–26) turned outward: Israel is blessed SO THAT the nations may know God. Blessing is not terminal but instrumental. The refrain: "May the peoples praise you, God; may all the peoples praise you" (vv.3, 5).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
67:1–2

"May God bless us... so that your ways may be known on earth." The "so that" makes Israel’s blessing missionary. Blessed to be a blessing (Gen 12:2–3).

67:3–4

"May the peoples praise you, God. May the nations be glad and sing for joy, for you rule the peoples with equity." Universal praise, universal joy, universal justice. The psalm’s vision is global.

Verses 5–7 — “The Earth Has Yielded Its Harvest”
567

Hebrew Word Study

יְבוּל yəbūl — produce / harvest / yield: "The land yields its harvest (yəbūl); God, our God, blesses us" (v.6). The harvest as evidence of blessing. But the point is not the harvest itself: "May God bless us still, so that all the ends of the earth will fear him" (v.7). The final word is global: all the ends of the earth. The harvest is a sign; the mission is the point.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
67:5–6

"May the peoples praise you. The land yields its harvest; God blesses us." Harvest → blessing → praise. The agricultural cycle is theological.

67:7

"May God bless us still, so that all the ends of the earth will fear him." The psalm’s final vision: blessing Israel until the entire world recognises God. The most missionary psalm in Book II.
