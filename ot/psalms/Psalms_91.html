Psalms 91

He Who Dwells in the Shelter of the Most High

Verses 1–8 — “He Will Cover You with His Feathers”
12345678

Hebrew Word Study

סֵתֶר sēṭer — shelter / secret place / hiding place: "Whoever dwells in the shelter (sēṭer) of the Most High will rest in the shadow of the Almighty" (v.1). THE protection psalm. Four divine names in two verses: Most High (ʿelyōn), Almighty (shadday), LORD (YHWH), God (ʾĕlōhîm). Four images of protection: shelter (v.1), shadow (v.1), refuge/fortress (v.2), feathers/wings (v.4). No fear of night-terror, day-arrow, dark-pestilence, or noon-destruction (vv.5–6). A thousand fall at your side, ten thousand at your right hand, but it will not come near you (v.7). Satan quotes vv.11–12 to tempt Jesus (Matt 4:6).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
91:1–2

"Whoever dwells in the shelter of the Most High will rest in the shadow of the Almighty. I will say of the LORD, ‘He is my refuge and my fortress, my God, in whom I trust.’" Four names, four protections, one trust declaration.

91:3–4

"He will save you from the fowler’s snare and from the deadly pestilence. He will cover you with his feathers, and under his wings you will find refuge." The bird-mother image: God’s wings as shelter. The feathers are soft; the protection is absolute.

91:5–7

"You will not fear the terror of night, nor the arrow that flies by day. A thousand may fall at your side, ten thousand at your right hand, but it will not come near you." Four threats (night, day, dark, noon) matched by total immunity. The numbers escalate: one thousand, ten thousand — still safe.

Verses 9–16 — “He Will Command His Angels Concerning You”
910111213141516

Hebrew Word Study

מַלְאָכָיו malʾāk̅āyw — his angels / his messengers: "He will command his angels (malʾāk̅āyw) concerning you to guard you in all your ways; they will lift you up in their hands, so that you will not strike your foot against a stone" (vv.11–12). Satan quotes these exact verses to tempt Jesus to jump from the temple pinnacle (Matt 4:6; Luke 4:10–11). Jesus responds: "Do not put the Lord your God to the test." The promise is real; Satan’s application is twisted. "You will tread on the lion and the cobra" (v.13). God speaks in first person (vv.14–16): "Because he loves me, I will rescue him. He will call on me, and I will answer. I will be with him in trouble. With long life I will satisfy him and show him my salvation."

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
91:11–12

"He will command his angels concerning you to guard you in all your ways." Satan quotes this to Jesus (Matt 4:6). The promise is genuine; the temptation is to presume upon it. Protection is promised to those who dwell, not to those who test.

91:13

"You will tread on the lion and the cobra; you will trample the great lion and the serpent." Dominion over predators and poisons. Jesus gives the seventy-two similar authority (Luke 10:19).

91:14–16

"Because he loves me, says the LORD, I will rescue him. He will call on me, and I will answer him; I will be with him in trouble. I will deliver him and honour him. With long life I will satisfy him and show him my salvation." God’s direct speech closes the psalm with seven promises: rescue, protect, answer, accompany, deliver, honour, satisfy.
