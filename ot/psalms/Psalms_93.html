Psalms 93

The LORD Reigns

Verses 1–2 — “The LORD Is Robed in Majesty”
12

Hebrew Word Study

מָלָךְ mālāk̅ — he reigns / he has become king: "The LORD reigns (mālāk̅). He is robed in majesty; the LORD is robed in majesty and armed with strength" (v.1). The first enthronement psalm (93, 95–99). God’s reign is described through clothing metaphors: robed in majesty, armed with strength. The world is established; it cannot be moved (v.1). God’s throne is established from of old; he is from all eternity (v.2).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
93:1

"The LORD reigns. He is robed in majesty." Enthronement declaration. God does not merely exist; he REIGNS. The world is stable because the king is dressed for action.

93:2

"Your throne was established long ago; you are from all eternity." The throne predates creation. The king was reigning before anything existed to reign over.

Verses 3–5 — “Mightier Than the Breakers of the Sea”
345

Hebrew Word Study

אַדִּיר ʾaddîr — majestic / mighty: "Mightier (ʾaddîr) than the thunder of the great waters, mightier than the breakers of the sea — the LORD on high is mighty" (v.4). The seas raise their voice (v.3) — chaos threatens. But God is mightier than the waves. The psalm closes: "Your statutes, LORD, stand firm; holiness adorns your house for endless days" (v.5). Two permanences: God’s word stands; God’s house is holy.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
93:3–4

"The seas have lifted up their voice; the seas have lifted up their pounding waves. Mightier than the thunder of the great waters — the LORD on high is mighty." Chaos vs sovereignty. The waves roar; God is louder.

93:5

"Your statutes stand firm; holiness adorns your house for endless days." Permanence of word + permanence of holiness. Both endure.
