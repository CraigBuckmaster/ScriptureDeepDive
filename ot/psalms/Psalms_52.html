Psalms 52

Why Do You Boast of Evil?

Verses 1–4 — “Your Tongue Plots Destruction”
1234

Hebrew Word Study

גִבּוֹר gibbōr — mighty man / hero: "Why do you boast of evil, you mighty man (gibbōr)?" (v.1). Addressed to Doeg the Edomite, who reported David to Saul and slaughtered the priests of Nob (1 Sam 22). The "mighty man" is sarcastic: Doeg’s might is used for treachery, not valour. His tongue is a sharpened razor (v.2). He loves evil more than good, falsehood more than truth (v.3).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
52:1–2

"Why do you boast of evil? Your tongue plots destruction; it is like a sharpened razor." The tongue as weapon — the same speech-as-violence theme from Psalms 5, 10, 12.

52:3–4

"You love evil rather than good. You love every harmful word." The preference for destruction: the boaster chooses evil deliberately.

Verses 5–9 — “I Am Like an Olive Tree Flourishing”
56789

Hebrew Word Study

זַיִת zayit — olive tree: "But I am like an olive tree (zayit) flourishing in the house of God; I trust in God’s unfailing love for ever and ever" (v.8). The contrast: the boaster is uprooted (v.5); David is planted and flourishing. The olive tree image recalls Psalm 1’s tree by water. Trust in ḥesed produces rootedness; trust in wealth produces ruin (v.7).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
52:5–7

"God will bring you down to everlasting ruin. The righteous will see and fear and laugh at you: ‘Here is the one who did not make God his stronghold but trusted in great wealth.’" The contrast between Doeg’s fate and David’s.

52:8–9

"I trust in God’s unfailing love for ever and ever. I will praise you forever." The olive tree: productive, enduring, beautiful. Rooted in God’s house.
