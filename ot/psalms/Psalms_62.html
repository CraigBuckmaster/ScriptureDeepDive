Psalms 62

Truly My Soul Finds Rest in God

Verses 1–6 — “He Alone Is My Rock and My Salvation”
123456

Hebrew Word Study

אַךְ ʾak̅ — truly / surely / only: "Truly (ʾak̅) my soul finds rest in God; my salvation comes from him. Truly he is my rock and my salvation; he is my fortress, I will never be shaken" (vv.1–2). The word ʾak̅ (truly/only) appears six times in the psalm — the keyword of exclusivity. ONLY God is rock, salvation, fortress. The refrain repeats (vv.1–2, 5–6) with one change: "never be shaken" (v.2) becomes "I will not be shaken" (v.6). The confidence deepens.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
62:1–2

"Truly my soul finds rest in God. He alone is my rock." The soul’s rest is not in resolution of circumstances but in the character of God. Six times "truly/only" (ʾak̅) emphasises exclusivity.

62:3–4

"How long will you assault me? You are all like a leaning wall, a tottering fence." The enemies are structurally unsound — they look standing but are about to collapse.

62:5–6

"Yes, my soul, find rest in God; my hope comes from him." David preaches to his own soul (as in Ps 42–43). Self-counsel: direct your soul toward God.

Verses 7–12 — “Power Belongs to God”
789101112

Hebrew Word Study

עֹז ʿōz — power / strength: "One thing God has spoken, two things I have heard: ‘Power (ʿōz) belongs to God’ and ‘with you, Lord, is unfailing love’" (vv.11–12). The psalm’s theological summit: God has BOTH power and love. Power without love is tyranny; love without power is impotence. God possesses both simultaneously. David warns: do not trust in extortion or stolen wealth (v.10). If riches increase, do not set your heart on them (v.10). Trust God alone.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
62:8

"Trust in him at all times, you people; pour out your hearts before him. God is our refuge." The invitation: all times, all people, all hearts poured out. Prayer is not selective; it is constant.

62:9–10

"Surely the lowborn are but a breath, the highborn a delusion. On a balance they go up; they are together lighter than a breath." Both social classes weigh nothing. And: do not trust in extortion or riches.

62:11–12

"Power belongs to God, and with you, Lord, is unfailing love." The two-things declaration: absolute power + absolute love. This combination exists only in God.
