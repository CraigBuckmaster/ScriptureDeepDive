Psalms 43

Send Me Your Light and Your Truth

Verses 1–2 — “Vindicate Me, My God”
12

Hebrew Word Study

אוֹר ʾōr — light: "Send me your light (ʾōr) and your faithful care, let them lead me" (v.3). Psalm 43 is the companion to Psalm 42 (possibly originally one psalm — they share the refrain). The psalmist asks for vindication against an ungodly nation and for light and truth to guide him back to God’s dwelling.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
43:1–2

"Vindicate me, my God, and plead my cause. You are God my stronghold — why have you rejected me?" The "why" questions continue from Psalm 42. The exiled psalmist feels both attacked by enemies and abandoned by God.

Verses 3–5 — “Then I Will Go to the Altar of God”
345

Hebrew Word Study

מִזְבֵּחַ mizbēaḥ — altar: "Then I will go to the altar (mizbēaḥ) of God, to God, my joy and my delight. I will praise you with the lyre, O God, my God" (v.4). The destination of the guided journey: the altar, which means God’s presence. The refrain returns one final time: "Why, my soul, are you downcast? Put your hope in God" (v.5). Third and last occurrence. The exile psalm trilogy (42–43) ends in hope, not resolution.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
43:3–4

"Send me your light and your faithful care, let them lead me; let them bring me to your holy mountain." Light and truth are personified guides leading the exile home. The destination: the altar of God, who is "my joy and my delight."

43:5

"Why, my soul, are you downcast?" Third refrain. The exile continues, but so does the hope. The psalm ends in trust, not triumph.
