Psalms 80

Hear Us, Shepherd of Israel

Verses 1–7 — “Restore Us, O God; Make Your Face Shine on Us”
1234567

Hebrew Word Study

הָשִׁיבֵנוּ hashîbēnū — restore us / turn us back: "Restore us (hashîbēnū), O God; make your face shine on us, that we may be saved" (v.3). The refrain appears three times (vv.3, 7, 19), intensifying: "Restore us, O God" → "Restore us, God Almighty" → "Restore us, LORD God Almighty." Each repetition adds a divine title. The Shepherd of Israel (v.1), who leads Joseph like a flock, is asked to awaken and come to rescue. "You have made us an object of derision; our enemies mock us" (v.6).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
80:1

"Hear us, Shepherd of Israel, you who lead Joseph like a flock." The shepherd-king imagery from Psalms 23, 78, 79. God leads Israel as shepherd leads sheep.

80:3

"Restore us, O God; make your face shine on us, that we may be saved." The refrain: restoration = God’s shining face. Salvation is not political rescue but divine presence.

80:4–6

"How long will you be angry? You have fed them with the bread of tears. You have made us an object of derision." Tears as food; mockery as identity. The nation is defined by its suffering.

Verses 8–19 — “Return to Us, God Almighty!”
8910111213141516171819

Hebrew Word Study

גֶפֶן gefen — vine / grapevine: "You transplanted a vine (gefen) from Egypt; you drove out the nations and planted it" (v.8). Israel as vine: God brought it from Egypt, cleared the ground, planted it (v.9), it took root and filled the land (v.9). But now: "Why have you broken down its walls?" (v.12). Boars ravage it, insects devour it (v.13). "Return to us, God Almighty! Watch over this vine, the root your right hand has planted, the son you have raised up for yourself" (vv.14–15). The "son" (בן, bēn) is both Israel and the messianic king.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
80:8–11

"You transplanted a vine from Egypt. It took root and filled the land. Its shade covered the mountains; its branches reached the Mediterranean." The vine metaphor: Israel’s growth from Egyptian slavery to Promised Land abundance. God is the gardener.

80:12–13

"Why have you broken down its walls? Boars from the forest ravage it." The garden is now unprotected. The walls are God’s decision to remove.

80:17–19

"Let your hand rest on the man at your right hand, the son of man you have raised up for yourself. Restore us, LORD God Almighty." The "son of man at your right hand" is messianic. The final refrain adds all three divine titles.
