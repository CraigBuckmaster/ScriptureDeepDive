Psalms 46

God Is Our Refuge and Strength

Verses 1–6 — “Though the Earth Give Way”
123456

Hebrew Word Study

מַחֲסֶה maḥăseh — refuge / shelter: "God is our refuge (maḥăseh) and strength, an ever-present help in trouble" (v.1). Luther’s psalm — the inspiration for "A Mighty Fortress Is Our God." The scenarios of total collapse: earth gives way, mountains fall into the sea, waters roar and foam, mountains quake (vv.2–3). Yet: "God is within her, she will not fall" (v.5). A river brings joy to the city of God (v.4). The city stands because God is in it.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
46:1

"God is our refuge and strength, an ever-present help in trouble." The most quoted verse of confidence in the Psalter. Three identities: refuge (shelter), strength (power), ever-present help (availability).

46:2–3

"Therefore we will not fear, though the earth give way and the mountains fall into the heart of the sea." The worst imaginable scenarios: geological, oceanic, tectonic. None produces fear because God is present.

46:4–5

"There is a river whose streams make glad the city of God. God is within her, she will not fall." Jerusalem had no river — this is a theological river: God’s presence as life-giving water within the city.

Verses 7–11 — “Be Still, and Know That I Am God”
7891011

Hebrew Word Study

הַרְפּוּ harpū — be still / cease striving / let go: "Be still (harpū), and know that I am God; I will be exalted among the nations, I will be exalted in the earth" (v.10). The psalm’s climax. Harpū means "stop fighting," "let go of your weapons," "cease your own efforts." It is a command to surrender control, not merely to be quiet. The refrain: "The LORD Almighty is with us; the God of Jacob is our fortress" (vv.7, 11). God’s title "LORD Almighty" (YHWH ṣəbāʾōṭ) — commander of heaven’s armies.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
46:8–9

"Come and see what the LORD has done. He makes wars cease to the ends of the earth. He breaks the bow and shatters the spear; he burns the shields with fire." God’s peace is not negotiated but imposed. He destroys the weapons themselves.

46:10

"Be still, and know that I am God." Not a whisper but a command. Stop striving. Stop managing. Stop fighting. Know that I am God.

46:11

"The LORD Almighty is with us; the God of Jacob is our fortress." The refrain. Two names: LORD of armies (cosmic power) + God of Jacob (personal covenant). Both applied to "us."
