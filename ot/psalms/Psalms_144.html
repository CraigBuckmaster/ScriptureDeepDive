Psalms 144

Praise Be to the LORD My Rock

Verses 1–8 — “What Are Human Beings That You Care for Them?”
12345678

Hebrew Word Study

הֶבֶל hebel — breath / vapour: "They are like a breath (hebel); their days are like a fleeting shadow" (v.4). David’s battle psalm. God is his rock, fortress, stronghold, deliverer, shield, refuge (vv.1–2). Then the Psalm-8 question: "What are human beings that you care for them?" (v.3). The answer: we are hebel and shadow — but God trains our hands for battle (v.1) and parts the heavens to rescue (v.5).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
144:1–2

"Praise be to the LORD my Rock, who trains my hands for war. He is my loving God and my fortress, my stronghold and my deliverer, my shield, in whom I take refuge." Six military-refuge titles in two verses.

144:3–4

"LORD, what are human beings that you care for them? They are like a breath; their days are like a fleeting shadow." The Psalm 8 question with Ecclesiastes’ answer: we are hebel. Yet God cares.

144:5–8

"Part your heavens, LORD, and come down; touch the mountains so that they smoke. Send forth lightning and scatter the enemy." Theophany-request: come down, smoke mountains, scatter with lightning. The prayer for cosmic intervention.

Verses 9–15 — “Blessed Is the People Whose God Is the LORD”
9101112131415

Hebrew Word Study

אַשְׁרֵי ʾashre — blessed / happy: "Blessed (ʾashre) is the people whose God is the LORD" (v.15). David sings a new song on the ten-stringed lyre (v.9). After the battle: prosperous sons and daughters, overflowing barns, abundant flocks, strong oxen, no breach in walls, no cry of distress in the streets (vv.12–14). The vision of shalom: not just the absence of war but the presence of abundance. The final beatitude: blessed is the people whose God is the LORD.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
144:12–14

"Our sons in their youth like well-nurtured plants; our daughters like pillars carved to adorn a palace. Our barns full, our flocks numbering thousands. No breach, no captivity, no cry of distress." The shalom vision: every dimension of life — family, agriculture, security — flourishing.

144:15

"Blessed is the people whose God is the LORD." The psalm’s closing beatitude. National blessedness defined by one relationship: whose God is the LORD.
