Psalms 28

The LORD Is My Strength

Verses 1–5 — “Do Not Be Deaf to My Cry”
12345

Hebrew Word Study

צוּרִי ṣūrî — my rock: "To you, LORD, I call; you are my rock (ṣūrî), do not turn a deaf ear to me" (v.1). David cries to God as rock — solid, immovable, reliable. Without God’s response, David is like those going down to the pit (v.1). He lifts his hands toward God’s inner sanctuary (v.2) and asks not to be dragged away with the wicked (vv.3–4).

Context

A lament that pivots to praise. David cries out (vv.1–2), asks for separation from the wicked (vv.3–4), and declares that God will repay the wicked for their deeds (v.5).

Cross-Reference

  • Ps 18:2"The LORD is my rock" — the same ṣūr imagery.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
28:1–2

"To you I call, my rock. Hear my cry for mercy as I lift my hands toward your Most Holy Place." Prayer directed physically toward the temple — embodied worship.

28:5

"Because they have no regard for the deeds of the LORD, he will tear them down and never build them up." Ignoring God’s work leads to permanent destruction.

Verses 6–9 — “The LORD Is My Strength and My Shield”
6789

Hebrew Word Study

מָגֵן māgēn — shield: "The LORD is my strength and my shield (māgēn); my heart trusts in him, and he helps me" (v.7). The pivot: God heard, God helped, David’s heart leaps with joy. The personal becomes national: "save your people and bless your inheritance; be their shepherd and carry them forever" (v.9).

Context

Thanksgiving erupts: "Praise be to the LORD, for he has heard my cry" (v.6). David’s heart trusts, and he is helped (v.7). The psalm expands: save your people, bless your inheritance, shepherd and carry them forever (v.9).

Cross-Reference

  • Isa 40:11"He tends his flock like a shepherd; he gathers the lambs in his arms" — the shepherd-carrying image.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
28:7

"The LORD is my strength and my shield; my heart trusts in him." Strength (offensive), shield (defensive), trust (relational). The full package.

28:9

"Be their shepherd and carry them forever." The psalm ends with the shepherd image from Psalm 23 applied to the entire nation.
