Psalms 71

Do Not Cast Me Away When I Am Old

Verses 1–13 — “You Have Been My Hope Since My Youth”
12345678910111213

Hebrew Word Study

מִנְעוּרַי minnəʿūray — from my youth: "Since my youth (minnəʿūray), God, you have taught me, and to this day I declare your marvellous deeds" (v.17). An elder’s psalm — the speaker looks back on a lifetime of God’s faithfulness. From the womb (v.6), from youth (v.5), to old age (v.9) and grey hairs (v.18). The plea: do not cast me away when I am old and my strength is gone (v.9). The enemies say "God has forsaken him" (v.11). The elder replies: you have been my hope, my confidence, my praise from birth.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
71:5–6

"You have been my hope, Sovereign LORD, my confidence since my youth. From birth I have relied on you." A lifetime of trust: from womb to present. The elder’s testimony spans decades.

71:9

"Do not cast me away when I am old; do not forsake me when my strength is gone." The fear unique to ageing: becoming useless, being abandoned. The plea is for continuity of care.

71:11

"They say, ‘God has forsaken him.’" The cruelest taunt against the elderly believer: you trusted God all your life and now look at you.

Verses 14–24 — “Even When I Am Old and Grey”
1415161718192021222324

Hebrew Word Study

צִדְקָתְךָ ṣidqāṭək̅ā — your righteousness: "My mouth will tell of your righteous deeds (ṣidqāṭək̅ā), of your saving acts all day long" (v.15). The elder’s mission: to declare God’s power to the next generation (v.18). "Even when I am old and grey, do not forsake me, my God, till I declare your power to the next generation" (v.18). Old age has a purpose: testimony. The grey-haired generation carries the story forward. "You have done great things; who is like you, O God?" (v.19).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
71:17–18

"Since my youth, God, you have taught me. Even when I am old and grey, do not forsake me till I declare your power to the NEXT GENERATION." The elder’s purpose: bridge. They connect past faithfulness to future faith.

71:19–20

"You have done great things. Though you have made me see troubles, many and bitter, you will restore my life again." The honest testimony: troubles were many AND God was faithful. Both are true.

71:22–24

"I will praise you with the harp; my lips will shout for joy; my tongue will tell of your righteous acts." The elder worships: harp, shout, tongue. Old age does not silence praise — it deepens it.
