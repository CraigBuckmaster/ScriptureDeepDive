Psalms 125

Those Who Trust in the LORD Are Like Mount Zion

Verses 1–3 — “As the Mountains Surround Jerusalem”
123

Hebrew Word Study

הַר har — mountain: "Those who trust in the LORD are like Mount Zion (har), which cannot be shaken but endures forever. As the mountains surround Jerusalem, so the LORD surrounds his people both now and forevermore" (vv.1–2). The geography of trust: the pilgrim arriving in Jerusalem sees the surrounding mountains and draws the analogy: as these hills surround the city, God surrounds his people. The sceptre of the wicked will not remain over the righteous (v.3).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
125:1–2

"Like Mount Zion, which cannot be shaken. As the mountains surround Jerusalem, so the LORD surrounds his people." The unshakable mountain and the surrounding mountains: two geographic truths as theology.

125:3

"The sceptre of the wicked will not remain over the land allotted to the righteous." Wicked rule is temporary. The land belongs to the righteous; oppression will not last.

Verses 4–5 — “Peace Be on Israel”
45

Hebrew Word Study

שָׁלוֹם shālōm — peace: "Peace (shālōm) be on Israel" (v.5). God does good to the good and upright (v.4). Those who turn to crooked ways will be banished with the evildoers (v.5). The psalm ends with the shalom blessing — the third time shalom closes a Song of Ascent (120:7; 122:8; 125:5). The pilgrimage moves from war (120) through prayer-for-peace (122) to bestowed peace (125).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
125:4–5

"LORD, do good to those who are good. But those who turn to crooked ways the LORD will banish. Peace be on Israel." The binary: good → good; crooked → banished. Then: peace on the whole.

Moses↗ People
Prophet, lawgiver, and covenant mediator
Moses stands at the centre of the Pentateuch’s narrative — the one man who spoke with God face to face (Exod 33:11; Num 12:8) and mediated the Sinai covenant to Israel. In Leviticus he receives the priestly legislation; in Numbers he leads, intercedes, and ultimately fails at Meribah (Num 20). The writer of Hebrews calls him "faithful in all God’s house" (Heb 3:5) while distinguishing him from Christ, the Son over the house.
