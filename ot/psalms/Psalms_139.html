Psalms 139

You Have Searched Me and Known Me

Verses 1–12 — “Where Can I Go from Your Spirit?”
123456789101112

Hebrew Word Study

חָקַרְתָנִי ḥāqartānî — you have searched me: "You have searched me (ḥāqartānî), LORD, and you know me" (v.1). THE psalm of divine omniscience and omnipresence. God knows when David sits and rises (v.2), his thoughts from afar (v.2), his words before he speaks them (v.4). "Where can I go from your Spirit? Where can I flee from your presence?" (v.7). If I go to heaven, you are there; to the depths, you are there; to the far side of the sea, your hand guides me (vv.8–10). "Even the darkness will not be dark to you; the night will shine like the day" (v.12). No location, no time, no darkness can hide from God.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
139:1–4

"You have searched me and you know me. You know when I sit and when I rise; you perceive my thoughts from afar. Before a word is on my tongue you, LORD, know it completely." Total knowledge: actions, thoughts, words — all known before they happen.

139:7–10

"Where can I go from your Spirit? If I go up to the heavens, you are there; if I make my bed in the depths, you are there. If I rise on the wings of the dawn, if I settle on the far side of the sea, even there your hand will guide me." Seven destinations: heaven, Sheol, dawn, sea’s end. God is in every one.

139:11–12

"If I say, ‘Surely the darkness will hide me,’ even the darkness will not be dark to you." Darkness cannot conceal. For God, night IS day. There is no hiding place.

Verses 13–24 — “I Am Fearfully and Wonderfully Made”
131415161718192021222324

Hebrew Word Study

נוֹרָא nōrāʾ — awesome / wonderful / fearful: "I praise you because I am fearfully and wonderfully made (nōrāʾ); your works are wonderful, I know that full well" (v.14). The most quoted verse on human dignity. God knit David together in his mother’s womb (v.13). "My frame was not hidden from you when I was made in the secret place" (v.15). "Your eyes saw my unformed body; all the days ordained for me were written in your book before one of them came to be" (v.16). Prenatal knowledge + life’s days pre-written = total divine foreknowledge. The psalm closes: "Search me, God, and know my heart; test me and know my anxious thoughts. See if there is any offensive way in me, and lead me in the way everlasting" (vv.23–24). The ending mirrors the opening: searched and known. But now David INVITES the searching.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
139:13–14

"You created my inmost being; you knit me together in my mother’s womb. I praise you because I am fearfully and wonderfully made." Prenatal intimacy: God as weaver in the womb. Every human is hand-crafted.

139:15–16

"My frame was not hidden from you when I was made in the secret place. Your eyes saw my unformed body; all the days ordained for me were written in your book." The book of life: every day pre-written. Human existence is not accidental but authored.

139:23–24

"Search me, God, and know my heart; test me. See if there is any offensive way in me, and lead me in the way everlasting." The psalm’s closing prayer: the one who was searched (v.1) now invites the searching (v.23). What was described becomes desired.
