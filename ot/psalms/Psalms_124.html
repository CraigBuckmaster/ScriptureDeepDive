Psalms 124

If the LORD Had Not Been on Our Side

Verses 1–5 — “They Would Have Swallowed Us Alive”
12345

Hebrew Word Study

לוּלֵי lūlē — if not / were it not for: "If (lūlē) the LORD had not been on our side — let Israel say — if the LORD had not been on our side when people attacked us, they would have swallowed us alive" (vv.1–3). The counterfactual psalm: what IF God hadn’t been there? The flood would have engulfed us (v.4), the torrent swept us away (v.4), the raging waters overwhelmed us (v.5). Three images of drowning, all prevented by one fact: God was on our side.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
124:1–3

"If the LORD had not been on our side — they would have swallowed us alive." The double "if" (vv.1–2) emphasises the hypothetical: without God, destruction was certain.

124:4–5

"The flood would have engulfed us, the torrent would have swept over us, the raging waters would have swept us away." Three flood images, escalating: engulf → sweep → rage. All hypothetical — all prevented.

Verses 6–8 — “Our Help Is in the Name of the LORD”
678

Hebrew Word Study

פַח faḥ — snare / trap: "We have escaped like a bird from the fowler’s snare (faḥ); the snare has been broken, and we have escaped" (v.7). The image shifts from flood to trap. The bird is caught — then the snare breaks. Freedom is not the bird’s doing but the snare’s failure. God broke the trap. "Our help is in the name of the LORD, the Maker of heaven and earth" (v.8). The psalm ends with the same declaration as Psalm 121:2: help comes from the Creator.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
124:6–7

"Praise be to the LORD, who has not let us be torn by their teeth. We have escaped like a bird from the fowler’s snare." Teeth (predator) + snare (trapper): two danger-images, both escaped.

124:8

"Our help is in the name of the LORD, the Maker of heaven and earth." The pilgrimage’s creed: our help = God’s name = Creator’s power. The same formula as 121:2.
