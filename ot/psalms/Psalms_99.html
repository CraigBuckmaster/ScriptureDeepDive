Psalms 99

The LORD Reigns; Let the Nations Tremble

Verses 1–5 — “Holy Is He”
12345

Hebrew Word Study

קָדוֹשׁ qādōsh — holy: "The LORD reigns, let the nations tremble. He is great in Zion; he is exalted over all the nations. Let them praise your great and awesome name — he is holy (qādōsh)!" (vv.1–3). The refrain "he is holy" appears three times (vv.3, 5, 9) — the trisagion (triple holy). The King loves justice (v.4); he has established equity and executed justice in Jacob (v.4). "Exalt the LORD our God and worship at his footstool; he is holy" (v.5).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
99:1–3

"The LORD reigns. He is great in Zion, exalted over all the nations. He is HOLY." The third enthronement psalm. The distinctive addition: holiness. The reigning God is the holy God.

99:4–5

"The King is mighty, he loves justice. Exalt the LORD and worship at his footstool; he is holy." Power + justice + holiness. The triad that defines God’s reign.

Verses 6–9 — “He Answered Them; He Was a Forgiving God”
6789

Hebrew Word Study

נֹשֵׂא nōsēʾ — forgiving / pardoning: "You were to Israel a forgiving (nōsēʾ) God, though you punished their misdeeds" (v.8). Moses, Aaron, and Samuel called on God and he answered (v.6). He spoke from the pillar of cloud (v.7). The remarkable combination: God who forgives AND punishes. Mercy and discipline are not opposites but companions. "Exalt the LORD our God and worship at his holy mountain, for the LORD our God is holy" (v.9). Third "holy" — the trisagion complete.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
99:6–7

"Moses and Aaron were among his priests, Samuel among those who called on his name. They called on the LORD and he answered them. He spoke to them from the pillar of cloud." Named people who prayed and were answered. The God of history is the God of prayer.

99:8

"You were a forgiving God to them, though you punished their misdeeds." Forgiveness and discipline coexist. God does not choose between mercy and justice; he exercises both.

99:9

"Exalt the LORD our God and worship at his holy mountain, for the LORD our God is holy." The third and final "holy." The trisagion: holy (v.3), holy (v.5), holy (v.9). Three declarations for one God.

Chapter-Level Scholarship

People of the Chapter

Key biblical figure

Moses↗ People
Prophet, lawgiver, and covenant mediator
Moses stands at the centre of the Pentateuch’s narrative — the one man who spoke with God face to face (Exod 33:11; Num 12:8) and mediated the Sinai covenant to Israel. In Leviticus he receives the priestly legislation; in Numbers he leads, intercedes, and ultimately fails at Meribah (Num 20). The writer of Hebrews calls him "faithful in all God’s house" (Heb 3:5) while distinguishing him from Christ, the Son over the house.
