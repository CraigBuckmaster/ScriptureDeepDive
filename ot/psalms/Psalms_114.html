Psalms 114

When Israel Came Out of Egypt

Verses 1–4 — “The Sea Looked and Fled”
1234

Hebrew Word Study

נָס nās — to flee: "The sea looked and fled (nās); the Jordan turned back" (v.3). The exodus in eight verses — the most compressed retelling in the OT. Judah became God’s sanctuary, Israel his dominion (v.2). The sea fled, the Jordan reversed, the mountains skipped like rams, the hills like lambs (vv.3–4). Nature personified: the sea is afraid, the river retreats, the mountains dance.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
114:1–2

"When Israel came out of Egypt, Judah became God’s sanctuary, Israel his dominion." The entire nation becomes temple and kingdom. Not a building but a people.

114:3–4

"The sea looked and fled; the Jordan turned back. The mountains leaped like rams, the hills like lambs." The most playful exodus retelling: the sea runs away, the river backs up, mountains bounce like sheep.

Verses 5–8 — “Tremble, Earth, at the Presence of the Lord”
5678

Hebrew Word Study

חוּלִי ḥūlî — tremble / writhe / dance: "Tremble (ḥūlî), earth, at the presence of the Lord" (v.7). The psalm asks the sea and mountains WHY they fled and leaped (vv.5–6). The answer: the presence of the Lord (v.7). The God of Jacob turns rock into pool and flint into springs (v.8). Creation recognises its Creator and responds: flee, dance, tremble, yield water from stone.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
114:5–6

"Why was it, sea, that you fled? Why, Jordan, did you turn back? Why, mountains, did you leap?" Personified questions to nature. The playful theology: even the sea needs a reason to flee.

114:7–8

"Tremble, earth, at the presence of the Lord. He turned the rock into a pool, the hard rock into springs of water." The exodus and wilderness combined: sea-splitting + rock-water. The earth trembles because its Creator is present.
