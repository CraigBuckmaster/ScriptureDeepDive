Psalms 130

Out of the Depths I Cry to You

Verses 1–4 — “If You Kept a Record of Sins, Who Could Stand?”
1234

Hebrew Word Study

מַעֲמַקִּים maʿamaqqîm — depths / deep places: "Out of the depths (maʿamaqqîm) I cry to you, LORD" (v.1). The sixth penitential psalm. De profundis — the Latin title that has made this psalm the church’s prayer at funerals and in seasons of darkness. The depths are not specified: sin, suffering, depression, grief — any abyss qualifies. "If you, LORD, kept a record of sins, LORD, who could stand?" (v.3). The question expects one answer: no one. "But with you there is forgiveness, so that we can, with reverence, serve you" (v.4). The purpose of forgiveness: not relief but reverence. Forgiveness produces worship, not complacency.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
130:1–2

"Out of the depths I cry to you, LORD. Lord, hear my voice; let your ears be attentive to my cry for mercy." The depths: the lowest possible position. The cry: upward. The distance between them is the psalm’s drama.

130:3–4

"If you kept a record of sins, who could stand? But with you there is forgiveness, so that we can serve you with reverence." The logic: universal guilt (no one could stand) → divine forgiveness → reverent service. Forgiveness produces awe, not licence.

Verses 5–8 — “I Wait for the LORD More Than Watchmen Wait for the Morning”
5678

Hebrew Word Study

קִוִּיתִי qiwwîṭî — I wait / I hope: "I wait (qiwwîṭî) for the LORD, my whole being waits, and in his word I put my hope. I wait for the Lord more than watchmen wait for the morning, more than watchmen wait for the morning" (vv.5–6). The double "watchmen wait for the morning" is not a typo but emphasis. The night watchman’s certainty: morning WILL come. The psalm’s hope is that certain. "Israel, put your hope in the LORD, for with the LORD is unfailing love and with him is full redemption. He himself will redeem Israel from all their sins" (vv.7–8). The closing promise: full redemption from ALL sins. Not partial, not gradual — full.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
130:5–6

"I wait for the LORD more than watchmen wait for the morning." The night watchman knows the sun will rise. He doesn’t hope; he expects. The psalmist’s waiting is that kind: certain, patient, dawn-expecting.

130:7–8

"With the LORD is unfailing love and with him is FULL redemption. He himself will redeem Israel from ALL their sins." The psalm’s climax: ḥesed + full redemption + all sins covered. The scope is total.

Prophet, lawgiver, and covenant mediator
Moses stands at the centre of the Pentateuch’s narrative — the one man who spoke with God face to face (Exod 33:11; Num 12:8) and mediated the Sinai covenant to Israel. In Leviticus he receives the priestly legislation; in Numbers he leads, intercedes, and ultimately fails at Meribah (Num 20). The writer of Hebrews calls him "faithful in all God’s house" (Heb 3:5) while distinguishing him from Christ, the Son over the house.
