Psalms 57

Be Exalted, O God, Above the Heavens

Verses 1–5 — “In the Shadow of Your Wings”
12345

Hebrew Word Study

כְנָפֶיךָ kənāfek̅ā — your wings: "Have mercy on me, my God, have mercy on me, for in you my soul takes refuge. I will take refuge in the shadow of your wings (kənāfek̅ā) until the disaster has passed" (v.1). Written in the cave (1 Sam 22:1 or 24:3). The cave becomes a temple; God’s wings become the shelter. The refrain: "Be exalted, O God, above the heavens; let your glory be over all the earth" (v.5, 11).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
57:1

"I will take refuge in the shadow of your wings until the disaster has passed." Temporary shelter for a temporary storm. The wings provide cover UNTIL the danger passes.

57:4

"I am in the midst of lions; my teeth are spears and arrows, their tongues sharp swords." Surrounded by predators whose weapons are speech.

57:5

"Be exalted, O God, above the heavens." The refrain. From the cave, David looks up — past the lions, past the danger, to God’s exaltation.

Verses 6–11 — “My Heart Is Steadfast”
67891011

Hebrew Word Study

נָכוֹן nāk̅ōn — steadfast / established / firm: "My heart is steadfast (nāk̅ōn), O God, my heart is steadfast; I will sing and make music" (v.7). Steadfastness produces song. The enemies set a net, but fell into it themselves (v.6, the boomerang principle). David sings, plays the lyre, and wakes the dawn (v.8). The psalm ends with the refrain: "Be exalted above the heavens; let your glory be over all the earth" (v.11).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
57:6

"They spread a net for my feet — but they have fallen into it themselves." The boomerang principle from Psalms 7, 9, 35.

57:7–8

"My heart is steadfast. I will sing and make music. Awake, my soul! I will awaken the dawn." The musician-king: David’s art is worship. He sings before the sun rises — his praise precedes the light.

57:11

"Be exalted above the heavens; let your glory be over all the earth." The final refrain. From a cave, David prays for God’s glory to fill the cosmos.
