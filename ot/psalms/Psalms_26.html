Psalms 26

A Plea of Innocence

Verses 1–6 — “Vindicate Me, LORD”
123456

Hebrew Word Study

שָׁפְטֵנִי shāfṭēnî — vindicate me / judge me: "Vindicate me (shāfṭēnî), LORD, for I have led a blameless life" (v.1). Another innocence plea (cf. Ps 7, 17). David invites examination: test me, try me (v.2). He walks in God’s faithfulness, avoids the deceitful, hates evildoers’ company (vv.3–5). He washes his hands in innocence (v.6).

Context

David claims integrity and invites God’s examination. He has not sat with the deceitful or associated with hypocrites. He washes his hands — the ritual gesture of innocence (cf. Pilate, Matt 27:24).

Cross-Reference

  • Matt 27:24Pilate washes his hands — the same gesture, without the substance.

Verses 7–12 — “I Love the House Where You Live”
789101112

Hebrew Word Study

מְעוֹן məʿōn — dwelling place / habitation: "LORD, I love the house where you live (məʿōn), the place where your glory dwells" (v.8). The temple is David’s joy. His feet stand on level ground; in the assemblies he praises the LORD (v.12).

Context

David loves God’s house (v.8), asks not to be swept away with sinners (v.9), and declares: "my feet stand on level ground; in the great congregation I will praise the LORD" (v.12).

Cross-Reference

  • Ps 27:4"One thing I ask: to dwell in the house of the LORD" — the same temple-longing.
  • Ps 84:10"Better is one day in your courts than a thousand elsewhere" — the same temple-love.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
26:8

"I love the house where you live." Temple devotion. The physical space matters because God’s glory dwells there.

26:12

"My feet stand on level ground." Stability through integrity. The concluding image: firm footing.
