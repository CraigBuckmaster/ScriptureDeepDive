Psalms 146

Praise the LORD, My Soul

Verses 1–5 — “Do Not Put Your Trust in Princes”
12345

Hebrew Word Study

נְדִיבִים nədîbîm — princes / nobles: "Do not put your trust in princes (nədîbîm), in human beings, who cannot save. When their spirit departs, they return to the ground; on that very day their plans come to nothing" (vv.3–4). The first of five final hallelujah psalms. Princes die; God endures. "Blessed is the one whose help is the God of Jacob, whose hope is in the LORD their God" (v.5). The Maker of heaven and earth, sea and everything in them — he remains faithful forever (v.6).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
146:1–2

"Praise the LORD, my soul. I will praise the LORD all my life; I will sing praise to my God as long as I live." Lifelong praise: the commitment that opens the final five.

146:3–5

"Do not put your trust in princes. When their spirit departs, their plans come to nothing. Blessed is the one whose help is the God of Jacob." The contrast: mortal princes vs eternal God. Plans that die with the planner vs help that outlasts everything.

Verses 6–10 — “The LORD Sets Prisoners Free”
678910

Hebrew Word Study

מָלַךְ mālak̅ — he reigns: "The LORD reigns (mālak̅) forever, your God, O Zion, for all generations. Praise the LORD" (v.10). What God does: upholds the cause of the oppressed (v.7), gives food to the hungry (v.7), sets prisoners free (v.7), gives sight to the blind (v.8), lifts up those bowed down (v.8), loves the righteous (v.8), watches over the foreigner (v.9), sustains the fatherless and the widow (v.9). Jesus reads Isaiah 61 in Nazareth and describes this exact ministry (Luke 4:18–19).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
146:7–9

"He upholds the oppressed, gives food to the hungry, sets prisoners free, gives sight to the blind, lifts up those bowed down, loves the righteous, watches over the foreigner, sustains the fatherless and widow." EIGHT divine actions for the vulnerable. Jesus’ mission statement (Luke 4:18–19) echoes this catalogue.

146:10

"The LORD reigns forever, your God, O Zion, for all generations. Praise the LORD." The hallelujah conclusion. The reign is forever; the praise is forever; the generations are forever.
