Psalms 27

The LORD Is My Light

Verses 1–6 — “Whom Shall I Fear?”
123456

Hebrew Word Study

אוֹרִי ʾōrî — my light: "The LORD is my light (ʾōrî) and my salvation — whom shall I fear?" (v.1). Light (revelation), salvation (rescue), stronghold (refuge) — three identities of God produce fearlessness. David asks one thing: to dwell in God’s house all his days, to gaze on God’s beauty and seek him in his temple (v.4).

Context

David’s confidence: even if an army besieges him, his heart will not fear (v.3). "One thing I ask: to dwell in the house of the LORD all the days of my life, to gaze on the beauty of the LORD" (v.4). The "one thing" is singular focus: God’s presence above all else.

Cross-Reference

  • John 8:12"I am the light of the world" — Jesus claims to be the light of Psalm 27:1.
  • Phil 1:21"For to me, to live is Christ" — Paul’s "one thing" echoes David’s.

Verses 7–14 — “Wait for the LORD”
7891011121314

Hebrew Word Study

קַוֵּה qawwēh — wait / hope / expect: "Wait (qawwēh) for the LORD; be strong and take heart and wait for the LORD" (v.14). The psalm’s final word is spoken twice: wait. Active, confident waiting — not passive resignation but expectant endurance. The one who asked to "gaze on the beauty of the LORD" (v.4) now waits for that beauty to be revealed.

Context

The psalm’s second half shifts to petition: hear me, do not hide your face, do not reject me (vv.7–9). "Though my father and mother forsake me, the LORD will receive me" (v.10). Then the declaration: "I remain confident of this: I will see the goodness of the LORD in the land of the living" (v.13). The psalm closes with the double imperative: wait for the LORD.

Cross-Reference

  • Isa 40:31"Those who wait on the LORD will renew their strength" — the same waiting-produces-strength theology.
  • Lam 3:25"The LORD is good to those whose hope is in him, to the one who seeks him" — the same confident waiting.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
27:10

"Though my father and mother forsake me, the LORD will receive me." The ultimate safety net: when human relationships fail, divine love holds.

27:13–14

"I remain confident: I will see the goodness of the LORD in the land of the living. Wait for the LORD." Confidence + patience. See goodness + wait. The psalm ends with active hope.
