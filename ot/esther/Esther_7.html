Esther 7

Esther’s Second Banquet; Haman’s Fall

Verses 1–6 — Esther Reveals Her Identity and Haman’s Plot
123456

Hebrew Word Study

צַר ṣar — adversary / enemy: The adversary and enemy is this vile Haman. In one sentence Esther unmasks herself (Jewish) and Haman (enemy). The double revelation is the climax.

Historical Context

The second banquet follows the sleepless night and Haman’s humiliation. The king is primed; Esther’s timing is providential.

Context

Esther reveals everything simultaneously: she is Jewish, her people are condemned, the architect is present. She frames it as a threat to the king’s own queen, making it personal before political.

Cross-Reference

  • 2 Sam 12:1–7Nathan’s unmasking — the guilty party present when the trap springs.
  • Gen 44:18–34Judah’s identity-revealing plea before Joseph.

Verses 7–10 — Haman Falls; Hanged on His Own Gallows
78910

Hebrew Word Study

תָּלָה tālâ — to hang / impale: They hanged Haman on the gallows he prepared for Mordecai. The poetic justice is exact. The gallows is the book’s supreme symbol of reversal.

Historical Context

Covering the face was the Persian signal for a condemned man. Harbona’s mention of the gallows provides immediate justice.

Context

The reversal is complete. Every intention boomerangs. The book’s structure is a mirror: everything reflects back to its source.

Cross-Reference

  • Ps 7:15–16His mischief returns upon his own head.
  • Dan 6:24Daniel’s accusers thrown to the lions — same pattern.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase

7:9

Harbona: a pole stands by Haman’s house, prepared for Mordecai. The instrument is ready.

7:10
