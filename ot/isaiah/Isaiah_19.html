Isaiah 19

Oracle Against Egypt: Blessed Be Egypt My People

Verses 1–15 — “The LORD Rides on a Swift Cloud into Egypt”
123456789101112131415

Hebrew Word Study

עָב ʿāb — cloud: "See, the LORD rides on a swift cloud (ʿāb) and is coming to Egypt. The idols of Egypt tremble before him, and the hearts of the Egyptians melt with fear" (v.1). God comes to Egypt as judge: civil war (v.2), failed counsel (v.3), a cruel master (v.4), the Nile dries up (vv.5‐7), fishermen groan (v.8), the linen workers despair (v.9), the wise counsellors of Pharaoh give stupid advice (vv.11–13). Egypt’s foundations are shaken (v.10). "The LORD has poured into them a spirit of dizziness; they make Egypt stagger in all that she does" (v.14).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
19:1

"The LORD rides on a swift cloud into Egypt." God arrives on his war-chariot — a cloud. The imagery of Sinai-theophany applied to Egypt.

19:5‐7

"The waters of the river will dry up, and the riverbed will be parched." The Nile — Egypt’s lifeline. When God judges Egypt, he strikes the source of life.

19:11–14

"The officials of Zoan are nothing but fools. The LORD has poured a spirit of dizziness into them." God sabotages Egypt’s famous wisdom. The nation that educated Moses cannot think straight.

Verses 16–25 — “Blessed Be Egypt My People, Assyria My Handiwork, Israel My Inheritance”
16171819202122232425

Hebrew Word Study

בְּרוּךְ bərūk̅ — blessed: "The LORD Almighty will bless them, saying, ‘Blessed (bərūk̅) be Egypt my people, Assyria my handiwork, and Israel my inheritance’" (v.25). THE most stunning verse in Isaiah’s oracle-against-the-nations section. Five “in that day” prophecies (vv.16, 18, 19, 21, 23): Egypt will fear the LORD (v.16), five Egyptian cities will speak Hebrew and swear allegiance (v.18), an altar to the LORD in Egypt (v.19), the LORD will send a saviour and defender (v.20), Egyptians will worship with sacrifice and offering (v.21), there will be a HIGHWAY between Egypt and Assyria — Israel’s two historic enemies united in worship (v.23). Then v.25: the three-nation blessing. Egypt = “my people” (the covenant title previously reserved for Israel). Assyria = “my handiwork.” Israel = “my inheritance.” The enemy nations receive covenant names.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
19:19–20

"In that day there will be an altar to the LORD in the heart of Egypt. It will be a sign and witness to the LORD Almighty." An altar in EGYPT — the land of Israel’s slavery. The exodus’s destination reversed: God goes TO Egypt.

19:23

"In that day there will be a highway from Egypt to Assyria. Egyptians and Assyrians will worship together." Israel’s two greatest enemies connected by a highway of worship. Geopolitical reconciliation through shared worship.

19:24–25

"Israel will be the third, along with Egypt and Assyria, a blessing on the earth. The LORD will bless them, saying: Blessed be Egypt MY PEOPLE, Assyria MY HANDIWORK, Israel MY INHERITANCE." The covenant titles redistributed. Egypt receives “my people” — the phrase from Exodus. Assyria receives “my handiwork” — the phrase from creation. The most universalist vision in the OT before Romans 9–11.
