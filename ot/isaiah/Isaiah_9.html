Isaiah 9

For to Us a Child Is Born

Verses 1–7 — “Wonderful Counsellor, Mighty God, Everlasting Father, Prince of Peace”
1234567

Hebrew Word Study

שַׂר שָׁלוֹם sar shālōm — prince of peace: "For to us a child is born, to us a son is given, and the government will be on his shoulders. And he will be called Wonderful Counsellor, Mighty God, Everlasting Father, Prince of Peace (sar shālōm)" (v.6). THE Christmas text. The people walking in darkness have seen a great light (v.2) — Matthew 4:15–16 applies this to Jesus beginning his ministry in Galilee. Joy multiplied like harvest-joy and battle-plunder-joy (v.3). The warrior’s boot and blood-stained garment will be fuel for the fire (v.5) — because this child ends war. Four throne-names: Wonderful Counsellor (divine wisdom), Mighty God (ʾēl gibbōr — deity!), Everlasting Father (eternal care), Prince of Peace (shalom-ruler). "Of the greatness of his government and peace there will be no end. He will reign on David’s throne" (v.7).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
9:1–2

"The people walking in darkness have seen a great light; on those living in the land of deep darkness a light has dawned." Matthew 4:15–16: Jesus moves to Capernaum in Galilee to fulfil this. The darkness is both Assyrian invasion and spiritual blindness. The light is both political hope and the Messiah.

9:6

"For to us a child is born, to us a son is given. Wonderful Counsellor, Mighty God, Everlasting Father, Prince of Peace." Four names that no human king could bear. ʾEl gibbōr (Mighty God) is used of God himself in 10:21. This child IS God. Handel’s Messiah makes this the centrepiece of the oratorio.

9:7

"Of the greatness of his government and peace there will be no end. He will reign on David’s throne with justice and righteousness from that time on and forever." Davidic-covenant fulfilment: the throne that 2 Samuel 7 promised and Psalm 89 questioned. The zeal of the LORD Almighty will accomplish this.

Verses 8–21 — “Yet for All This, His Anger Is Not Turned Away”
89101112131415161718192021

Hebrew Word Study

אַף ʾaf — anger / wrath: "Yet for all this, his anger (ʾaf) is not turned away, his hand is still upraised" (refrain: vv.12, 17, 21; repeated in 10:4). The refrain of extended judgment. Against Israel’s arrogance: they say “bricks have fallen but we will rebuild with dressed stone; fig trees are cut down but we will replace them with cedars” (v.10). Pride that rebuilds without repentance. God raises Aram and Philistia against them (v.12). The people do not return to the one who struck them (v.13). Leaders mislead; the led are swallowed up (v.16). Wickedness burns like fire; no one spares his brother (v.19). Ephraim devours Manasseh; together they turn against Judah (v.21). The refrain hammers four times: his anger is NOT turned away.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
9:10

"The bricks have fallen down, but we will rebuild with dressed stone." Defiance without repentance. The American response after 9/11 quoted this verse as resilience; Isaiah’s context is judgment against such pride.

9:12–13

"Yet for all this, his anger is not turned away. But the people have not returned to him who struck them." The four-fold refrain: judgment continues because repentance doesn’t come. God strikes; Israel rebuilds instead of repenting.

9:19–21

"By the wrath of the LORD the land will be scorched. No one will spare his brother. Ephraim devours Manasseh; together they turn against Judah." Civil war: the northern tribes consume each other, then attack Judah. Self-destruction precedes foreign invasion.
