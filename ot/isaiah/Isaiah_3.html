Isaiah 3

Judgment on Jerusalem and Judah

Verses 1–12 — “The LORD Is Taking Away from Jerusalem Every Support”
123456789101112

Hebrew Word Study

מַשְׁעֵן mashʿēn — support / staff / stay: "The LORD is about to take from Jerusalem and Judah both supply and support (mashʿēn): all supplies of food and water, the hero and the warrior, the judge and the prophet" (vv.1–2). God removes every category of leader: military, judicial, prophetic, political, religious, commercial (vv.1–3). Boys will govern; anarchy follows (v.4). The people oppress each other (v.5). A man grabs his brother and says "You have a cloak — be our leader!" and the brother refuses: "I have no food or clothing; don’t make me the leader of this people" (vv.6–7). No one WANTS to lead. Jerusalem staggers because their words and deeds are against the LORD (v.8). They parade their sin like Sodom (v.9).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
3:1–3

"The LORD is about to take away every support." The catalogue of leaders removed: hero, warrior, judge, prophet, diviner, elder, captain, counsellor, skilled craftsman, clever enchanter. Society’s entire infrastructure, gone.

3:6–7

"You have a cloak — be our leader! Don’t make me the leader; I have no food." Leadership collapse: no one will take responsibility. The cloak is the minimal qualification — and even that isn’t enough.

3:10–11

"Tell the righteous it will be well with them. Woe to the wicked! Disaster is upon them." The binary: the righteous eat the fruit of their deeds; the wicked receive what their hands have done.

Verses 13–26 — “The LORD Takes His Place in Court”
1314151617181920212223242526

Hebrew Word Study

שָׁפַט shāfaṭ — to judge: "The LORD takes his place in court (shāfaṭ); he rises to judge the people" (v.13). God as prosecutor: "It is you who have ruined my vineyard; the plunder from the poor is in your houses. What do you mean by crushing my people and grinding the faces of the poor?" (vv.14–15). Then judgment on Zion’s women: their finery itemised in devastating detail — bangles, headbands, crescents, earrings, bracelets, veils, headdresses, ankle chains, sashes, perfume, signet rings, fine robes, cloaks, purses, mirrors, linen garments, tiaras, shawls (vv.18–23). Instead of fragrance, stench; instead of a sash, a rope; instead of styled hair, baldness; instead of fine clothing, sackcloth (v.24). The men will fall by the sword (v.25). Zion’s gates will lament and mourn; she will sit on the ground, destitute (v.26).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
3:14–15

"What do you mean by crushing my people and grinding the faces of the poor?" God’s accusation against the elders and leaders. The metaphor: the poor ground like grain between millstones. Systemic oppression.

3:16–24

The catalogue of finery: 21 luxury items listed. The detail is satirical — each item will be replaced by its opposite. Beauty becomes horror. The specificity makes the reversal concrete.
