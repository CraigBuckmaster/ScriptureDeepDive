Isaiah 43

When You Pass Through the Waters, I Will Be with You

Verses 1–13 — “You Are Precious and Honoured in My Sight”
12345678910111213

Hebrew Word Study

גָּאַל gāʾal — to redeem / to act as kinsman-redeemer: "Do not fear, for I have redeemed (gāʾal) you; I have summoned you by name; you are mine" (v.1). THE chapter of divine tenderness. "When you pass through the waters, I will be with you; and when you pass through the rivers, they will not sweep over you. When you walk through the fire, the flames will not set you ablaze" (v.2). Water and fire: the two universal destroyers, both neutralised by divine presence. "You are precious and honoured in my sight, and I love you" (v.4). Nations are given as ransom in exchange for Israel (v.3). "Do not be afraid, for I am with you" (v.5). From east, west, north, south — God gathers everyone called by his name (vv.5–7). "I, even I, am the LORD, and apart from me there is no saviour" (v.11).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
43:1–2

"I have redeemed you; I have summoned you by name; you are mine. When you pass through the waters, I will be with you." The three claims: redeemed, named, possessed. The three promises: waters, rivers, fire. The presence-promise is not prevention but accompaniment.

43:4

"You are precious and honoured in my sight, and because I love you." The most direct declaration of divine love for Israel in the OT. Precious, honoured, loved. Three words that define the relationship.

43:10–11

"You are my witnesses. Before me no god was formed, nor will there be one after me. I, even I, am the LORD, and apart from me there is no saviour." Monotheism stated absolutely. Israel as witness to the only God.

Verses 14–28 — “Forget the Former Things; I Am Doing a New Thing!”
141516171819202122232425262728

Hebrew Word Study

חֲדָשָׁה ḥădāshâ — new thing: "Forget the former things; do not dwell on the past. See, I am doing a new thing (ḥădāshâ)! Now it springs up; do you not perceive it? I am making a way in the wilderness and streams in the wasteland" (vv.18–19). God promises Babylon’s fall (v.14). Then the stunning command: forget the Exodus! The new thing surpasses the old. "The people I formed for myself that they may proclaim my praise" (v.21). But Israel has not called on God; they have burdened him with sins and wearied him with offences (vv.22–24). "I, even I, am he who blots out your transgressions, for my own sake, and remembers your sins no more" (v.25). Forgiveness for God’s own sake — not because of Israel’s merit but because of God’s character.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
43:18–19

"Forget the former things! See, I am doing a new thing!" The command to forget the Exodus is revolutionary. The new exodus will so surpass the first that the first becomes forgettable.

43:21

"The people I formed for myself that they may proclaim my praise." Purpose statement: Israel exists FOR praise. The national purpose is doxological.

43:25

"I, even I, am he who blots out your transgressions, for my own sake, and remembers your sins no more." For MY sake. Forgiveness is not earned but flows from God’s own character. He remembers sins no more — divine forgetting as grace.
