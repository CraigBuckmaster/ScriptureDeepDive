Isaiah 13

The Oracle Against Babylon

Verses 1–16 — “The Day of the LORD Is Coming: Cruel, with Wrath and Fierce Anger”
12345678910111213141516

Hebrew Word Study

מַשָּא massāʾ — oracle / burden / pronouncement: "A prophecy (massāʾ) against Babylon that Isaiah son of Amoz saw" (v.1). The oracles-against-the-nations section begins (chs.13–23). Babylon first — even though Assyria, not Babylon, is the current threat. The Day of the LORD comes with wrath (v.9). Stars, sun, and moon go dark (v.10) — Jesus echoes this in the Olivet Discourse (Matt 24:29). “I will punish the world for its evil” (v.11). People are scarcer than gold (v.12). Heavens tremble, earth shaken from its place (v.13). Everyone flees to their own land (v.14). Infants dashed, houses looted, wives violated (vv.15–16).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
13:1

"An oracle against Babylon." Babylon is not yet the dominant power (that’s Assyria). Isaiah sees past the immediate to the ultimate enemy of God’s people.

13:9–10

"See, the day of the LORD is coming — cruel, with wrath and fierce anger. The stars of heaven will not show their light; the sun will be darkened; the moon will not give its light." Cosmic collapse: Jesus quotes this imagery (Matt 24:29; Mark 13:24–25). The Day of the LORD is the template for NT eschatology.

13:11–13

"I will punish the world for its evil. The heavens will tremble; the earth will shake from its place." Not just Babylon but the world. The oracle telescopes from historical Babylon to cosmic judgment.

Verses 17–22 — “Babylon Will Be Overthrown Like Sodom”
171819202122

Hebrew Word Study

שְׁמָמָה shəmāmâ — desolation / horror: "Babylon, the jewel of kingdoms, the pride and glory of the Babylonians, will be overthrown by God like Sodom and Gomorrah. She will never be inhabited" (vv.19–20). God raises the Medes (v.17) — not interested in silver or gold, they cannot be bought off. Babylon’s desolation is permanent: no Arab will pitch his tent there, no shepherd rest his flocks (v.20). Desert creatures, owls, wild goats will occupy it (vv.21–22). “Her time is at hand” (v.22). Revelation 18 develops the fall of Babylon from this chapter. The historical fall (539 BC) and the eschatological fall (Rev 18) share the same imagery.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
13:17–19

"I will stir up against them the Medes, who do not care for silver. Babylon, the jewel of kingdoms, will be overthrown like Sodom." Historically fulfilled: the Medo-Persian conquest of 539 BC. The Sodom comparison: sudden, total, irreversible.

13:20–22

"She will never be inhabited. Desert creatures will lie there; owls will dwell there; wild goats will leap about. Her time is at hand." Babylon’s archaeological reality: ruins in the Iraqi desert, never rebuilt as a living city. Revelation 18 draws on this for the eschatological Babylon’s fall.
