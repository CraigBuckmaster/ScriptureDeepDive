Isaiah 12

Songs of Praise: The Conclusion of First Isaiah

Verses 1–3 — “God Is My Salvation; I Will Trust and Not Be Afraid”
123

Hebrew Word Study

יְשׁוּעָה yəshūʿâ — salvation: "Surely God is my salvation (yəshūʿâ); I will trust and not be afraid. The LORD, the LORD himself, is my strength and my defence; he has become my salvation" (v.2). A brief psalm of thanksgiving closing the Immanuel section (chs.7–12). The name Isaiah means “YHWH is salvation” — the same root as yəshūʿâ and Yeshua (Jesus). “With joy you will draw water from the wells of salvation” (v.3). The Exodus-song echo: v.2 quotes Exodus 15:2 almost verbatim (Moses’ song at the sea).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
12:1

"In that day you will say: I will praise you, LORD. Although you were angry with me, your anger has turned away." Judgment to praise: the anger was real; its turning is celebrated.

12:2

"God is my salvation. The LORD himself is my strength and my song." Quotes Exodus 15:2 — the Red Sea song. The new exodus (ch.11) produces a new Exodus-song (ch.12).

12:3

"With joy you will draw water from the wells of salvation." The water-drawing image: salvation as a well, accessed with joy. At Sukkot, the Jews performed a water-drawing ceremony; Jesus stood up at that feast and said, “Let anyone who is thirsty come to me” (John 7:37–38).

Verses 4–6 — “Shout Aloud and Sing for Joy, People of Zion”
456

Hebrew Word Study

קְדוֹשׁ qədōsh — Holy One: "Shout aloud and sing for joy, people of Zion, for great is the Holy One (qədōsh) of Israel among you" (v.6). “Make known among the nations what he has done” (v.4). “Sing to the LORD, for he has done glorious things; let this be known to all the world” (v.5). The section closes outward: from personal praise (v.1) to national singing (v.6) to global proclamation (vv.4–5). The “Holy One of Israel” — Isaiah’s signature title for God (used 25 times in Isaiah, only 5 times elsewhere in the OT). Six verses of pure worship close the Immanuel section before the oracles against the nations begin.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
12:4–5

"Make known among the NATIONS what he has done. Let this be known to all the WORLD." The doxology is missionary: praise produces proclamation; worship drives witness.

12:6

"Great is the Holy One of Israel among you." Isaiah’s signature name for God. The God who is “holy, holy, holy” (6:3) is also “among you.” Transcendence and immanence in one title. The Immanuel section ends where ch.6 began: with the Holy One.
