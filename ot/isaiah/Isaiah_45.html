Isaiah 45

Cyrus: Anointed for God’s Purpose

Verses 1–13 — “I Summon You by Name Though You Do Not Acknowledge Me”
12345678910111213

Hebrew Word Study

מָשִׁיחַ māshîḥa — anointed one / messiah: "This is what the LORD says to his anointed (māshîḥa), to Cyrus, whose right hand I take hold of to subdue nations before him" (v.1). Cyrus is called māshîḥa — MESSIAH. The only non-Israelite in the entire Bible to receive this title. "I summon you by name and bestow on you a title of honour, though you do not acknowledge me" (v.4). God uses people who don’t know him for purposes they don’t understand. "I am the LORD, and there is no other; apart from me there is no God. I will strengthen you, though you have not acknowledged me" (v.5). "I form the light and create darkness, I bring prosperity and create disaster; I, the LORD, do all these things" (v.7). The most comprehensive sovereignty claim in the Bible: light AND darkness, prosperity AND disaster.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
45:1

"To his anointed, to Cyrus." A pagan king called Messiah. The title reserved for Davidic kings given to a Persian emperor. The theological scandal is deliberate: God’s purposes exceed Israel’s categories.

45:4–5

"I summon you by name, though you do not acknowledge me." The God who names stars (40:26) now names a king. Cyrus doesn’t know YHWH; YHWH knows Cyrus. Sovereignty operates through unknowing instruments.

45:7

"I form the light and create darkness, I bring prosperity and create disaster." The most absolute sovereignty statement in the Bible. No dualism: good and evil both under God’s hand. This does not make God the author of sin but refuses to exempt him from governance over all that happens.

Verses 14–25 — “Turn to Me and Be Saved, All You Ends of the Earth”
141516171819202122232425

Hebrew Word Study

יֵשַׁע yēshaʿ — salvation / deliverance: "Turn to me and be saved (yēshaʿ), all you ends of the earth; for I am God, and there is no other" (v.22). The products of Egypt, Cush, and the Sabeans will come over to Israel in chains, acknowledging: "Surely God is with you, and there is no other" (v.14). "Truly you are a God who has been hiding himself, the God and Saviour of Israel" (v.15). Then the potter-clay exchange: "Does the clay say to the potter, ‘What are you making?’" (v.9) — Paul echoes this in Romans 9:20. "I have not spoken in secret, from somewhere in a land of darkness" (v.19). God’s revelation is public, not occult. Then the global invitation: "Turn to me and be saved, ALL you ends of the earth" (v.22). "Before me every knee will bow; by me every tongue will swear" (v.23) — Paul quotes this in Philippians 2:10–11 and Romans 14:11 for Christ.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
45:15

"Truly you are a God who hides himself." The hiddenness of God: working through Cyrus who doesn’t know him, through exile that looks like abandonment. The hidden God is the saving God.

45:18–19

"I have not spoken in secret. I, the LORD, speak the truth; I declare what is right." The contrast with pagan oracles that speak in riddles and darkness. God’s word is public and clear.

45:22–23

"Turn to me and be saved, all you ends of the earth. Before me every knee will bow; by me every tongue will swear." Phil 2:10–11 applies this to Jesus: "at the name of Jesus every knee should bow." Rom 14:11 quotes it for final judgment. The universal invitation: ALL the earth. The universal submission: EVERY knee.
