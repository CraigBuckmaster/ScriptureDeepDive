Isaiah 7

The Sign of Immanuel

Verses 1–9 — “If You Do Not Stand Firm in Your Faith, You Will Not Stand at All”
123456789

Hebrew Word Study

אָמַן ʾāman — to believe / to stand firm: "If you do not stand firm (ʾāman) in your faith, you will not stand at all" (v.9). The Syro-Ephraimite crisis (c.735 BC): Syria and northern Israel march against Judah. King Ahaz panics. God sends Isaiah with his son Shear-Jashub (“a remnant shall return”) to meet Ahaz at the aqueduct. The message: don’t fear these two smouldering stubs of firewood (v.4). Within 65 years Ephraim will be shattered (v.8). The wordplay on ʾāman is untranslatable: “if you do not heʾĕmînū (believe), you will not heʾāmēnū (be established).” Faith and stability share the same root.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
7:1–2

Syria and Ephraim ally against Judah. The hearts of Ahaz and his people shake like trees in the wind. The political crisis is real; the theological test is whether Ahaz will trust God or Assyria.

7:4

"Say to him: Be careful, keep calm, do not be afraid. Do not lose heart because of these two smouldering stubs of firewood." The enemy is already burning out. Fear magnifies a dying threat.

7:9

"If you do not stand firm in your faith, you will not stand at all." The ʾāman wordplay. Belief and stability are the same word. Unbelief is structural collapse.

Verses 10–25 — “The Virgin Will Conceive and Give Birth to a Son”
10111213141516171819202122232425

Hebrew Word Study

עִמָּנוּאֵל ʿimmānūʾēl — God with us: "The virgin will conceive and give birth to a son, and will call him Immanuel (ʿimmānūʾēl) — God with us" (v.14). God tells Ahaz to ask for any sign — as deep as Sheol or as high as heaven (v.11). Ahaz refuses, cloaking faithlessness in piety: “I will not test the LORD” (v.12). Isaiah responds: God will give a sign anyway. The ʿalmâ (young woman / virgin) will conceive. Before the child knows right from wrong, the two threatening kings will be gone (v.16). But Assyria will come — the razor Ahaz hired will shave him too (vv.17–20). Matthew 1:22–23 identifies Jesus’ birth as the ultimate fulfilment. The immediate context: a child born in Isaiah’s time as a near-sign. The ultimate context: God literally WITH us in the incarnation.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
7:11–12

"Ask the LORD your God for a sign. But Ahaz said, I will not ask; I will not put the LORD to the test." Ahaz’s refusal is not humility but avoidance. He has already decided to call Assyria instead of God.

7:14

"The virgin (ʿalmâ) will conceive and give birth to a son, and will call him Immanuel." The most debated verse in Isaiah. ʿAlmâ = young woman (Hebrew) or virgin (LXX: parthenos). The LXX translation is what Matthew quotes. Near fulfilment: Isaiah’s own son or a court woman. Ultimate fulfilment: the virgin birth of Christ.

7:15–16

"Before the boy knows enough to reject the wrong and choose the right, the land of the two kings you dread will be laid waste." The timeline: within a few years, Syria and Ephraim will be destroyed. The child is a walking clock.

7:17–20

"The LORD will bring on you and on your people a time unlike any since Ephraim broke away — he will bring the king of Assyria." The razor Ahaz hired will shave him. The ally becomes the threat. Political calculation backfires.
