Isaiah 15

Oracle Against Moab

Verses 1–5 — “In a Night Moab Is Laid Waste”
12345

Hebrew Word Study

מַשָּא massāʾ — oracle / burden: "A prophecy (massāʾ) against Moab: Ar in Moab is ruined, destroyed in a night! Kir in Moab is ruined, destroyed in a night!" (v.1). Moab devastated overnight. The cities fall in rapid succession: Dibon, Nebo, Medeba, Heshbon, Elealeh (vv.2–4). Every head shaved, every beard cut off — mourning signs (v.2). They wear sackcloth on rooftops and in public squares (v.3). The armed men of Moab cry out; their hearts are faint (v.4). "My heart cries out over Moab" (v.5) — remarkably, the PROPHET grieves for the enemy.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
15:1

"Destroyed in a night!" Twice repeated. The suddenness is the shock. Moab had no warning.

15:3–5

"On every head is baldness and every beard cut off. In the streets they wear sackcloth. My heart cries out over Moab." Isaiah weeps for the enemy nation. Prophetic empathy transcends national loyalty.

Verses 6–9 — “The Waters of Dimon Are Full of Blood”
6789

Hebrew Word Study

דָּם dām — blood: "The waters of Dimon are full of blood (dām), but I will bring still more upon Dimon" (v.9). The grasslands wither, the vegetation is gone, the abundance is carried away over the Ravine of the Willows (vv.6–7). Their outcry echoes along the border (v.8). The wordplay: Dimon/dām (blood). Even the water is contaminated. Moab’s oracle continues into ch.16.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
15:6–7

"The waters of Nimrim are dried up. The grass is withered, the vegetation gone." Ecological disaster accompanies political collapse. The land mourns with the people.

15:9

"The waters of Dimon are full of blood." Dimon/dam wordplay. The rivers run red. Isaiah promises: more is coming.
