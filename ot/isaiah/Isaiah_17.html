Isaiah 17

Oracle Against Damascus

Verses 1–8 — “Damascus Will No Longer Be a City”
12345678

Hebrew Word Study

מַפָּלָה mappālâ — ruin-heap: "See, Damascus will no longer be a city but will become a heap of ruins (mappālâ)" (v.1). Syria and northern Israel’s alliance will be shattered. The glory of Jacob will fade; his body will waste away (v.4). The harvest will be like gleaning: a few olives left on the topmost branches, four or five on the outer branches (vv.5–6). "In that day people will look to their Maker and turn their eyes to the Holy One of Israel" (v.7). Suffering produces looking upward — the same dynamic as Nebuchadnezzar in Daniel 4.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
17:1

"Damascus will become a heap of ruins." Historically fulfilled when Assyria conquered Damascus in 732 BC.

17:4–6

"The glory of Jacob will fade. It will be as when a reaper gathers the grain — a few olives left." The harvest metaphor: almost everything is taken; a tiny remnant remains on the outermost branches.

17:7–8

"In that day people will look to their Maker. They will not look to the altars." Suffering redirects the gaze. The Asherah poles and incense altars are abandoned when the Maker becomes visible again.

Verses 9–14 — “The Roar of Many Nations”
91011121314

Hebrew Word Study

שָׁאוֹן shāʾōn — roar / tumult: "Woe to the many nations that rage — they rage like the raging (shāʾōn) sea! But God rebukes them and they flee far away" (v.13). The nations roar like mighty waters but God’s rebuke scatters them like chaff before the wind (v.13). "In the evening, sudden terror! Before the morning, they are gone!" (v.14). The pattern: nations surge like seas; God speaks; they vanish overnight. The theology of all the nation-oracles condensed into one image.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
17:12–14

"The nations rage like the raging sea. But God rebukes them. In the evening, sudden terror! Before the morning, they are gone." The nations are ocean-waves; God’s word is the breakwater. One evening of terror; by morning, nothing.
