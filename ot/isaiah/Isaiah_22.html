Isaiah 22

Oracle Against the Valley of Vision: Jerusalem

Verses 1–14 — “You Did Not Look to the One Who Made It”
1234567891011121314

Hebrew Word Study

חִזָיוֹן ḥizzāyōn — vision: "A prophecy against the Valley of Vision (ḥizzāyōn)" (v.1). The shocking twist: this oracle is against JERUSALEM. The city that should see visions is called the "valley" of vision — brought low. The people flee to the rooftops, not killed in battle but captured without a fight (vv.2–3). Isaiah weeps: "Do not try to console me over the destruction of my people" (v.4). On the day of panic they shore up the walls, store water, tear down houses for fortification (vv.8–11). "But you did not look to the One who made it, or have regard for the One who planned it long ago" (v.11). Military preparation without divine consultation. Then: "The Lord called you to weep and wail. Instead, there is joy and revelry — ‘Let us eat and drink, for tomorrow we die!’" (vv.12–13). Paul quotes v.13 in 1 Corinthians 15:32. "‘Till your dying day this sin will not be atoned for,’ says the Lord" (v.14).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
22:8–11

"You stored water, counted buildings, tore down houses to strengthen the wall. But you did not look to the One who made it." Military engineering without theology. Every human preparation; zero divine consultation.

22:12–13

"The Lord called you to weep. Instead: joy, revelry, ‘Let us eat and drink, for tomorrow we die!’" God called for repentance; they threw a party. Paul quotes this as the logic of those who deny resurrection (1 Cor 15:32).

22:14

"This sin will not be atoned for till your dying day." The severest verdict in Isaiah: no atonement available. The only unforgivable sin in Isaiah’s oracles.

Verses 15–25 — “The Key of David on His Shoulder”
1516171819202122232425

Hebrew Word Study

מַפְתֵחַ maftēaḥ — key: "I will place on his shoulder the key (maftēaḥ) of the house of David; what he opens no one can shut, and what he shuts no one can open" (v.22). Shebna the steward is demoted (vv.15–19); Eliakim replaces him (vv.20–23). Eliakim receives the key of David — authority over the royal household. Revelation 3:7 applies this to Christ: "These are the words of him who holds the key of David. What he opens no one can shut; what he shuts no one can open." The key-bearer controls access to the king. Christ controls access to God.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
22:15–19

"Go to this steward, to Shebna. What are you doing here, and who gave you permission to cut out a grave here?" Shebna carved himself an elaborate tomb — preparing for death in luxury while the city faces destruction. Self-serving leadership indicted.

22:22

"I will place on his shoulder the key of the house of David." Revelation 3:7 applies this directly to Christ. The key-of-David passage: authority to open and shut, admit and exclude.

22:23–25

"I will drive him like a peg into a firm place. But the peg driven into the firm place will give way." Even Eliakim’s peg will eventually fail. No human administrator is permanent. Only the one Revelation identifies with the key endures.
