Isaiah 35

The Highway of Holiness: The Desert Will Rejoice

Verses 1–6 — “The Blind Will See, the Deaf Will Hear, the Lame Will Leap”
123456

Hebrew Word Study

פָּרַח pāraḥ — to blossom / to burst into bloom: "The desert and the parched land will be glad; the wilderness will rejoice and blossom (pāraḥ). Like the crocus, it will burst into bloom" (v.1). THE mirror-chapter to ch.34: devastation answered by restoration. "They will see the glory of the LORD, the splendour of our God" (v.2). "Strengthen the feeble hands, steady the knees that give way" (v.3) — Hebrews 12:12 quotes this. "Then will the eyes of the blind be opened and the ears of the deaf unstopped. Then will the lame leap like a deer, and the mute tongue shout for joy" (vv.5–6). Jesus quotes this list to John the Baptist’s disciples as proof of his messiahship: "The blind receive sight, the lame walk, the deaf hear" (Matt 11:5; Luke 7:22).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
35:1–2

"The desert will rejoice and blossom. They will see the glory of the LORD." After ch.34’s devastation: blooming desert. The same land cursed in ch.34 is redeemed in ch.35.

35:3–4

"Strengthen the feeble hands, steady the knees that give way. Say to those with fearful hearts: Be strong, do not fear; your God will come." Hebrews 12:12 quotes vv.3–4. The pastoral instruction: encourage the weak before the miracle arrives.

35:5–6

"Then the blind will see, the deaf will hear, the lame will leap, the mute will shout." The four reversals. Jesus points to these as credentials (Matt 11:5). His miracles are not random; they fulfil Isaiah 35.

Verses 7–10 — “A Highway Will Be There, Called the Way of Holiness”
78910

Hebrew Word Study

מְסִלָּה məsillâ — highway / raised road: "A highway (məsillâ) will be there; it will be called the Way of Holiness. It will be for those who walk on that Way. The unclean will not journey on it; wicked fools will not go about on it" (v.8). The burning sand becomes a pool; the thirsty ground becomes springs (v.7). No lion, no ravenous beast (v.9). "And those the LORD has rescued will return. They will enter Zion with singing; everlasting joy will crown their heads. Gladness and joy will overtake them, and sorrow and sighing will flee away" (v.10). The last verse of ch.35 is the first taste of what ch.40 will elaborate. Sorrow flees; joy overtakes. The highway leads home.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
35:8

"A highway called the Way of Holiness." Not just a road but a holy road. Access is moral, not geographical. The highway theology: 11:16 (second exodus), 19:23 (Egypt-Assyria), 35:8 (holiness), 40:3 (voice in the wilderness).

35:10

"Those the LORD has rescued will return. They will enter Zion with singing; everlasting joy will crown their heads. Sorrow and sighing will flee away." Isaiah 51:11 repeats this verse nearly verbatim. The refrain of the redeemed. Joy pursues them; sorrow retreats.
