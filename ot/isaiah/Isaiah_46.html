Isaiah 46

Babylon’s Gods Carried vs God Who Carries

Verses 1–7 — “I Have Made You and I Will Carry You”
1234567

Hebrew Word Study

סָבַל sābal — to carry / to bear: "Even to your old age and grey hairs I am he, I am he who will sustain you. I have made you and I will carry (sābal) you; I will sustain you and I will rescue you" (v.4). The contrast: Bel and Nebo (Babylon’s chief gods) are loaded onto weary animals — the gods must be CARRIED (vv.1–2). But Israel’s God CARRIES his people: from birth to old age, from creation to grey hairs (vv.3–4). Pagan gods are burdens; YHWH bears burdens. The idol-maker pays gold, hires a craftsman, then carries the idol and sets it in its place — "it cannot move; it cannot save" (v.7).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
46:1–2

"Bel bows down, Nebo stoops low; their idols are borne by beasts of burden." Babylon’s New Year procession: gods paraded on carts. But the carts are fleeing — the gods cannot save even themselves.

46:4

"Even to your old age I am he. I have made you and I will carry you." The lifetime promise: womb to grave. God’s carrying never expires.

46:7

"They carry it on their shoulders; they set it in its place, and there it stands. It cannot save." The idol-test: can it move? Can it answer? Can it save? No, no, no.

Verses 8–13 — “I Am God, and There Is No Other”
8910111213

Hebrew Word Study

עֵצָה ʿēṣâ — counsel / plan / purpose: "I make known the end from the beginning, from ancient times, what is still to come. I say, ‘My purpose (ʿēṣâ) will stand, and I will do all that I please’" (v.10). "Remember this, you rebels. I am God, and there is no other; I am God, and there is none like me" (v.9). God summons "a bird of prey from the east, a man to fulfil my purpose from a far-off land" (v.11) — Cyrus again. "What I have said, that I will bring about; what I have planned, that I will do" (v.11). "My righteousness is not far away; my salvation will not be delayed" (v.13).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
46:9–10

"I am God and there is none like me. I make known the end from the beginning." The test of deity: can you declare the future? Only YHWH passes this test. The idol-trial theme culminates here.

46:11–13

"A bird of prey from the east. What I have planned, that I will do. My salvation will not be delayed." Cyrus as raptor: swift, unstoppable, divinely directed. God’s timetable is not behind schedule.
