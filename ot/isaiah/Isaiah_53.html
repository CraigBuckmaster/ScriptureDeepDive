Isaiah 53

The Suffering Servant: Pierced for Our Transgressions

Verses 1–6 — “By His Wounds We Are Healed”
123456

Hebrew Word Study

מְחֹלָל məḥōlāl — pierced / wounded: "He was pierced (məḥōlāl) for our transgressions, he was crushed for our iniquities; the punishment that brought us peace was on him, and by his wounds we are healed" (v.5). THE most christological chapter in the OT. "Who has believed our message?" (v.1) — John 12:38 and Romans 10:16 quote this. He grew up like a tender shoot, a root out of dry ground (v.2). No beauty, no majesty, nothing to attract us (v.2). Despised and rejected by mankind, a man of suffering (v.3). "Surely he took up our pain and bore our suffering" (v.4) — Matthew 8:17 applies this to Jesus healing the sick. "We all, like sheep, have gone astray, each of us has turned to our own way; and the LORD has laid on him the iniquity of us all" (v.6). Substitutionary atonement: HIS wounds, OUR healing. HIS punishment, OUR peace.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
53:1–2

"Who has believed our message? He grew up like a tender shoot, a root out of dry ground." John 12:38; Rom 10:16 quote v.1. The servant is unexpected: no royal splendour, no military power. A root from dry ground — life from death.

53:3

"He was despised and rejected by mankind, a man of suffering, and familiar with pain." The most concise summary of Jesus life in the OT. Despised, rejected, suffering, pain. Four words that define the Passion.

53:5

"Pierced for our transgressions, crushed for our iniquities; the punishment that brought us peace was on him, and by his wounds we are healed." The five-fold substitution: pierced FOR us, crushed FOR us, punished FOR our peace, wounded FOR our healing. The preposition “for” carries the entire theology of the cross.

53:6

"We all, like sheep, have gone astray. The LORD has laid on him the iniquity of us all." Universal sin ("all have gone astray") matched by universal imputation ("the iniquity of us all"). 1 Peter 2:24–25 quotes this directly.

Verses 7–12 — “He Was Led Like a Lamb to the Slaughter”
789101112

Hebrew Word Study

אָשָׁם ʾāshām — guilt offering / trespass offering: "The LORD makes his life an offering for sin (ʾāshām)" (v.10). "He was oppressed and afflicted, yet he did not open his mouth; he was led like a lamb to the slaughter" (v.7) — the Ethiopian eunuch was reading this exact verse when Philip met him (Acts 8:32–35). "By oppression and judgment he was taken away. For the transgression of my people he was punished" (v.8). "He was assigned a grave with the wicked, and with the rich in his death" (v.9) — Jesus crucified between criminals, buried in Joseph of Arimathea rich man tomb. "After he has suffered, he will see the light of life and be satisfied" (v.11). The servant DIES and then SEES — resurrection implied. "He bore the sin of many, and made intercession for the transgressors" (v.12). The servant is sacrifice, priest, and intercessor in one.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
53:7

"He was led like a lamb to the slaughter, and as a sheep before its shearers is silent, so he did not open his mouth." Acts 8:32–35: the Ethiopian eunuch reads this and Philip explains Jesus. The lamb-to-slaughter image: Passover lamb (Ex 12), suffering servant (Isa 53), Lamb of God (John 1:29), slain Lamb (Rev 5:6).

53:9

"He was assigned a grave with the wicked, and with the rich in his death, though he had done no violence, nor was any deceit in his mouth." Wicked in death (crucified between thieves), rich in burial (Joseph of Arimathea). The historical precision is remarkable.

53:10–11

"The LORD makes his life a guilt offering. After he has suffered, he will see the light of life." Death AS offering; life AFTER death. The ʾāshām (guilt offering of Lev 5) applied to a person. Resurrection implied: suffering, then seeing, then satisfaction.

53:12

"He poured out his life unto death and was numbered with the transgressors. For he bore the sin of many and made intercession for the transgressors." Luke 22:37: Jesus quotes this at the Last Supper. Numbered with transgressors (crucified between criminals). Bearing sin (2 Cor 5:21). Making intercession (Heb 7:25).
