Isaiah 51

Awake, Awake, Arm of the LORD!

Verses 1–11 — “The Ransomed of the LORD Will Return”
1234567891011

Hebrew Word Study

צוּר ṣūr — rock: "Look to the rock (ṣūr) from which you were cut and to the quarry from which you were hewn. Look to Abraham your father, and to Sarah, who gave you birth" (vv.1–2). Three "listen to me" imperatives (vv.1, 4, 7). God will comfort Zion’s ruins; the desert will become like Eden (v.3). "My justice will become a light to the nations" (v.4). "The heavens will vanish like smoke, the earth will wear out like a garment, but my salvation will last forever" (v.6). "The ransomed of the LORD will return. They will enter Zion with singing; everlasting joy will crown their heads. Gladness and joy will overtake them, and sorrow and sighing will flee away" (v.11) — the refrain from 35:10, repeated verbatim.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
51:1–2

"Look to the rock from which you were cut — Abraham and Sarah." The quarry-metaphor: Israel is hewn from Abraham-rock. One couple became a nation. God can multiply again.

51:6

"The heavens will vanish like smoke, the earth will wear out like a garment, but my salvation will last forever." Three transient things (heavens, earth, inhabitants) versus one permanent thing: God’s salvation.

51:11

"The ransomed will return. Everlasting joy. Sorrow and sighing will flee away." The 35:10 refrain returns. The highway reaches its destination.

Verses 12–23 — “Awake, Awake, Put On Strength, Arm of the LORD!”
121314151617181920212223

Hebrew Word Study

זְרוֹעַ zərōʿa — arm: "Awake, awake, put on strength, arm (zərōʿa) of the LORD! Awake, as in days gone by, as in generations of old. Was it not you who cut Rahab to pieces, who pierced that monster through?" (v.9). The arm that slew the chaos-dragon and dried the sea is summoned to act again. God as comforter: "I, even I, am he who comforts you. Who are you that you fear mere mortals?" (v.12). Jerusalem has drunk the cup of God’s wrath to its dregs; now God takes it from her hand and puts it in the hands of her tormentors (vv.17, 22–23). "You will never drink it again" (v.22).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
51:9–10

"Awake, arm of the LORD! Was it not you who cut Rahab to pieces, who dried up the sea?" Rahab = the primordial chaos-monster (cf. 27:1; Job 26:12). The arm that slew the dragon and parted the sea is called to do it again.

51:12

"I, even I, am he who comforts you. Who are you that you fear mere mortals?" The doubled I: emphatic. Fear of mortals is incompatible with knowledge of God.

51:22–23

"I have taken out of your hand the cup that made you stagger. I will put it into the hands of your tormentors." The cup transfers: from Jerusalem’s lips to her enemies’. Jesus in Gethsemane asks for the cup to pass (Matt 26:39) — the same cup.
