Isaiah 2

The Mountain of the LORD

Verses 1–5 — “They Will Beat Their Swords into Ploughshares”
12345

Hebrew Word Study

הַר har — mountain: "In the last days the mountain (har) of the LORD’s temple will be established as the highest of the mountains; it will be exalted above the hills, and all nations will stream to it" (v.2). The most famous eschatological vision in the OT. Nations stream to Zion; God judges between them. "They will beat their swords into ploughshares and their spears into pruning hooks. Nation will not take up sword against nation, nor will they train for war anymore" (v.4). Micah 4:1–3 contains the same oracle. "Come, descendants of Jacob, let us walk in the light of the LORD" (v.5). The vision: universal peace through divine instruction from Zion.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
2:2–3

"All nations will stream to it. Many peoples will come and say, ‘Come, let us go up to the mountain of the LORD.’ He will teach us his ways. The law will go out from Zion." Nations voluntarily ascending. God’s Torah as the source of international order.

2:4

"They will beat their swords into ploughshares. Nation will not take up sword against nation." The peace vision: military hardware recycled as agricultural tools. War not just ended but forgotten — "nor will they train for war anymore." Joel 3:10 reverses this: "beat your ploughshares into swords" — the opposite command for judgment day.

2:5

"Come, let us walk in the light of the LORD." The response to the vision: Israel should already be living what the nations will one day seek.

Verses 6–22 — “The Eyes of the Arrogant Will Be Humbled”
678910111213141516171819202122

Hebrew Word Study

שָׁפֵל shāfēl — brought low / humbled: "The eyes of the arrogant will be humbled (shāfēl) and human pride brought low; the LORD alone will be exalted in that day" (v.11). The Day of the LORD: against everything proud and lofty (v.12). The cedars of Lebanon, the oaks of Bashan, every lofty tower, every fortified wall, every trading ship (vv.13–16) — all brought low. "People will flee to caves in the rocks from the fearful presence of the LORD when he rises to shake the earth" (v.19). "Stop trusting in mere humans, who have but a breath in their nostrils" (v.22). The contrast: v.4’s peace-vision vs vv.6–22’s judgment. Both are Isaiah’s future. The path to peace passes through humbling.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
2:11–12

"The LORD alone will be exalted in that day. The LORD Almighty has a day in store for all the proud and lofty." The Day of the LORD targets one thing: human arrogance. Everything that exalts itself will be flattened.

2:17–19

"The arrogance of man will be brought low. People will flee to caves from the fearful presence of the LORD." The cave-flight: Revelation 6:15–17 echoes this exact scene — kings and generals hiding in caves from the wrath of the Lamb.

2:22

"Stop trusting in mere humans, who have but a breath in their nostrils." The chapter’s closing command. Humans are breath (neshamah) — the same word as Genesis 2:7. The breath God gave can be withdrawn.
