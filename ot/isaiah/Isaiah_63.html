Isaiah 63

Who Is This Coming from Edom?

Verses 1–6 — “I Have Trodden the Winepress Alone”
123456

Hebrew Word Study

גַּת gat — winepress: "Who is this coming from Edom, from Bozrah, with his garments stained crimson? I have trodden the winepress (gat) alone; from the nations no one was with me" (vv.1, 3). The divine warrior returns from battle, clothes soaked in the blood of nations. "I trampled them in my anger and trod them down in my wrath; their blood spattered my garments" (v.3). Revelation 19:13–15 develops this directly: Christ returns with a robe dipped in blood, treading the winepress of God’s fury. "In my wrath I trampled them and their blood ran in the ground" (v.6). The ferocity is deliberate: the God who comforts (ch.40) also crushes (ch.63). Both are the same love.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
63:1–3

"Who is this from Edom, garments stained crimson?" The question-and-answer format: who? The warrior answers: I, who speak in righteousness, mighty to save. The blood is not his own but the nations.

63:3

"I have trodden the winepress alone; from the nations no one was with me." Alone: no human ally, no angelic army. The solitary warrior. Rev 19:13–15 applies this to Christ’s return.

63:4

"The day of vengeance was in my heart, and the year for my redemption had come." The vengeance that Jesus omitted at Nazareth (61:2b) now arrives. The comma is closed.

Verses 7–19 — “You, LORD, Are Our Father”
78910111213141516171819

Hebrew Word Study

אָבִינוּ ʾābînū — our father: "You, LORD, are our Father (ʾābînū). Our Redeemer from of old is your name" (v.16). The tone shifts entirely: from divine warrior to communal lament. "I will tell of the kindnesses of the LORD, the deeds for which he is to be praised" (v.7). God became their Saviour; in all their distress he too was distressed (vv.8–9). But they rebelled and grieved his Holy Spirit (v.10) — Paul echoes this in Ephesians 4:30: "Do not grieve the Holy Spirit." Where is your zeal and your might? Your tenderness and compassion are withheld from us (v.15). "Though Abraham does not know us and Israel does not acknowledge us, you, LORD, are our Father" (v.16). The appeal bypasses human ancestry to claim divine parentage directly.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
63:9

"In all their distress he too was distressed, and the angel of his presence saved them." God’s empathy: when they hurt, he hurts. Not distant sovereignty but shared suffering.

63:10

"They rebelled and grieved his Holy Spirit." Eph 4:30: "Do not grieve the Holy Spirit of God." The Spirit is personal enough to grieve. Isaiah names the Spirit as person, not force.

63:16

"Though Abraham does not know us, you, LORD, are our Father." The radical move: bypassing the patriarchs to address God directly as Father. Human lineage fails; divine parentage endures.
