Isaiah 4

The Branch of the LORD

Verses 1–2 — “In That Day the Branch of the LORD Will Be Beautiful”
12

Hebrew Word Study

צֶמַח ṣemaḥ — branch / sprout: "In that day the Branch (ṣemaḥ) of the LORD will be beautiful and glorious, and the fruit of the land will be the pride and glory of the survivors in Israel" (v.2). After ch.3’s devastation, a sudden pivot. The Branch: a messianic title (cf. Jer 23:5; 33:15; Zech 3:8; 6:12). The survivors and the land’s fruit are described in terms of beauty and glory — the opposite of ch.3’s stench and sackcloth. Seven women grab one man (v.1): so many men have died that marriage norms collapse. But the Branch grows from the stump.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
4:1

"Seven women will take hold of one man: ‘Let us be called by your name. Take away our disgrace!’" The demographic aftermath of war: so many men dead that social conventions invert.

4:2

"In that day the Branch of the LORD will be beautiful and glorious." The ṣemaḥ: the messianic sprout. After the tree is cut down (ch.3), the branch grows. The pattern: judgment produces remnant; remnant produces Messiah.

Verses 3–6 — “A Canopy Over All the Glory”
3456

Hebrew Word Study

חֻפָּה ḥuppâ — canopy / bridal covering: "Then the LORD will create over all of Mount Zion a cloud of smoke by day and a glow of flaming fire by night; over everything the glory will be a canopy (ḥuppâ)" (v.5). The exodus imagery returns: pillar of cloud and fire (Exod 13:21). God’s glory as a wedding canopy over Zion. "It will be a shelter and shade from the heat of the day, and a refuge and hiding place from the storm and rain" (v.6). The remnant will be called holy (v.3). The filth will be washed away by a spirit of judgment and a spirit of fire (v.4). Six verses of pure hope after three chapters of judgment.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
4:3–4

"Those who remain in Zion will be called holy. The Lord will wash away the filth of the women of Zion by a spirit of judgment and a spirit of fire." Holiness by purification: the fire of ch.1–3 is redemptive, not merely destructive.

4:5–6

"The LORD will create over all of Mount Zion a cloud of smoke by day and fire by night; over everything the glory will be a canopy." The second exodus: cloud and fire return. The glory becomes a wedding canopy (ḥuppah) — God marries Zion again.
