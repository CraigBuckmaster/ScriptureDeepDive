Isaiah 28

The Precious Cornerstone

Verses 1–13 — “Woe to the Drunkards of Ephraim”
12345678910111213

Hebrew Word Study

הוֹי hōy — woe!: "Woe (hōy) to that wreath, the pride of Ephraim’s drunkards, to the fading flower, his glorious beauty" (v.1). The first of six woe oracles (chs.28–33). Ephraim’s leaders are drunk; even priests and prophets stagger from beer (v.7). Tables covered with vomit (v.8). They mock Isaiah’s teaching as childish: "Do this, do that, a rule for this, a rule for that, a little here, a little there" (v.10) — the Hebrew (ṣav lāṣav, qav lāqav) mimics baby-talk. God responds: since you treat my word as baby-talk, I will speak to you through foreign lips — the Assyrians (v.11). Paul quotes v.11 in 1 Corinthians 14:21 for the gift of tongues.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
28:1–4

"Woe to the pride of Ephraim’s drunkards." The fading flower: Samaria’s crown-like position on its hill, wilting under judgment.

28:7–8

"Priests and prophets stagger from beer. All the tables are covered with vomit." Religious leaders drunk on duty. The imagery is deliberately revolting.

28:9–13

"Ṣav lāṣav, qav lāqav." They mock Isaiah as speaking baby-talk. God says: fine — you’ll hear foreign speech you can’t understand at all. Paul quotes (1 Cor 14:21).

Verses 14–29 — “I Lay in Zion a Stone, a Tested Stone, a Precious Cornerstone”
14151617181920212223242526272829

Hebrew Word Study

פִנָּה pinnâ — cornerstone: "See, I lay in Zion a stone, a tested stone, a precious cornerstone (pinnâ) for a sure foundation; the one who relies on it will never be stricken with panic" (v.16). Jerusalem’s rulers boast of a "covenant with death" (v.15) — an alliance they think protects them. God says their refuge of lies will be swept away (v.17). Instead, God lays his own foundation: the cornerstone. Peter applies this to Christ (1 Pet 2:6). Paul does too (Rom 9:33; 10:11; Eph 2:20). "I will make justice the measuring line and righteousness the plumb line" (v.17). The farmer parable closes the chapter: God’s discipline is skilled, varied, purposeful — not random violence (vv.23–29).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
28:15–16

"We have made a covenant with death. We have made a lie our refuge." Then: "I lay in Zion a tested stone, a precious cornerstone." False security versus God’s foundation. Death-covenant versus cornerstone. Every NT author who quotes this passage applies the stone to Christ.

28:17

"I will make justice the measuring line and righteousness the plumb line." God builds with moral precision. The tools of construction are ethical, not architectural.

28:23–29

"Does the farmer plough continually? Does he keep on breaking up the soil? No — he plants, threshes gently, varies his method." God as skilled farmer: each crop is handled differently. Discipline is calibrated, not indiscriminate. "All this comes from the LORD Almighty, whose plan is wonderful, whose wisdom is magnificent."
