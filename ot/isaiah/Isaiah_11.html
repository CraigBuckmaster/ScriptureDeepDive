Isaiah 11

A Shoot from the Stump of Jesse

Verses 1–10 — “The Spirit of the LORD Will Rest on Him”
12345678910

Hebrew Word Study

חֹטֶר ḥōṭer — shoot / branch: "A shoot (ḥōṭer) will come up from the stump of Jesse; from his roots a Branch will bear fruit" (v.1). After the forest is felled (10:33–34), a tiny shoot emerges from the stump. Not from David’s throne (too grand) but from Jesse’s stump (stripped back to origins). The Spirit rests on him sevenfold: wisdom, understanding, counsel, might, knowledge, fear of the LORD (v.2). He judges not by what he sees but with righteousness for the poor (v.4). "He will strike the earth with the rod of his mouth" (v.4). Then the peace-kingdom: "The wolf will live with the lamb, the leopard will lie down with the goat, the calf and the lion together; and a little child will lead them" (v.6). "They will neither harm nor destroy on all my holy mountain, for the earth will be full of the knowledge of the LORD as the waters cover the sea" (v.9). "The Root of Jesse will stand as a banner for the peoples; the nations will rally to him" (v.10) — Paul quotes this in Romans 15:12.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
11:1–2

"A shoot from the stump of Jesse." Not Solomon’s palace but Jesse’s stump. The dynasty is cut down; the Messiah grows from the root. The seven Spirits: wisdom, understanding, counsel, might, knowledge, fear of the LORD (Rev 1:4; 4:5).

11:4

"With righteousness he will judge the needy, with justice he will give decisions for the poor. He will strike the earth with the rod of his mouth." The Messiah’s weapon: his word. Rev 19:15: “coming out of his mouth is a sharp sword.”

11:6–9

"The wolf will live with the lamb. A little child will lead them. The earth will be full of the knowledge of the LORD as the waters cover the sea." The peace-kingdom: predator-prey reconciliation. Nature redeemed. The reversal of the curse. The most beloved vision of eschatological peace in the OT.

11:10

"The Root of Jesse will stand as a banner for the peoples; the nations will rally to him." Paul quotes this in Romans 15:12 for Gentile inclusion. The Messiah is not Israel’s private possession but the nations’ banner.

Verses 11–16 — “A Second Exodus”
111213141516

Hebrew Word Study

שֵׁנִית shēnîṭ — a second time: "In that day the Lord will reach out his hand a second time (shēnîṭ) to reclaim the surviving remnant of his people" (v.11). A SECOND exodus — from Assyria, Egypt, Cush, Elam, Babylonia, Hamath, and the islands of the Mediterranean (v.11). The jealousy between Ephraim and Judah will end (v.13). They will swoop down on Philistia, plunder the east (v.14). The LORD will dry up the gulf of the Egyptian sea, as he did at the Red Sea (v.15). A highway for the remnant from Assyria — "as there was for Israel when they came up from Egypt" (v.16). The new exodus surpasses the first.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
11:11–12

"A second time to reclaim the remnant. He will raise a banner for the nations and gather the exiles of Israel." Worldwide regathering: Assyria, Egypt, the islands. The second exodus is global.

11:15–16

"The LORD will dry up the gulf of the Egyptian sea. There will be a highway for the remnant from Assyria." Red Sea redux. The God who parted the waters once will do it again. Exodus typology applied to future salvation.
