Isaiah 10

Woe to Assyria, the Rod of My Anger

Verses 1–19 — “Assyria: The Rod of My Anger”
12345678910111213141516171819

Hebrew Word Study

מַטֶּה maṭṭeh — rod / staff: "Woe to the Assyrian, the rod (maṭṭeh) of my anger, in whose hand is the club of my wrath!" (v.5). God SENDS Assyria against Israel — but Assyria doesn’t know it’s being used (v.7). Assyria boasts: “By the strength of my hand I have done this, and by my wisdom” (v.13). God responds: “Does the axe raise itself above the person who swings it?” (v.15). The instrument cannot claim credit for the carpenter’s work. Therefore God will punish Assyria’s arrogance — fire will burn the thorns (vv.16–19). The theology: God uses pagan empires as instruments of judgment, then judges the instruments for their pride.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
10:5–7

"Woe to the Assyrian, the rod of my anger. I send him against a godless nation. But this is not what he intends." God’s hidden sovereignty: Assyria executes God’s plan without knowing it. The instrument is guilty because its heart is arrogant.

10:13–15

"By the strength of MY hand and MY wisdom. Does the axe raise itself above the one who swings it?" The most devastating rhetorical question in Isaiah. The tool cannot brag about the craftsman’s work.

10:16–19

"The Light of Israel will become a fire. In a single day it will burn and consume his thorns." The same God who used Assyria will destroy Assyria. The Light that illuminates (9:2) also incinerates.

Verses 20–34 — “A Remnant Will Return”
202122232425262728293031323334

Hebrew Word Study

שְׁאָר shəʾār — remnant: "A remnant (shəʾār) will return, a remnant of Jacob will return to the Mighty God" (v.21). Isaiah’s son Shear-Jashub (“a remnant shall return”) is the walking embodiment of this theology. Though Israel is as numerous as sand, only a remnant will return (v.22) — Paul quotes this in Romans 9:27. “Do not be afraid of the Assyrians. In a very short time my anger against you will end” (vv.24–25). The chapter closes with Assyria’s advance described as trees being felled: Aiath, Migron, Michmash, Geba, Ramah, Gibeah, Gallim, Laishah, Anathoth, Nob — each town is cut down as Assyria marches south toward Jerusalem (vv.28–32). Then: “The LORD will lop off the boughs with great power. The lofty trees will be felled” (vv.33–34). Assyria itself is the tree that falls.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
10:20–22

"A remnant will return to the Mighty God. Though your people Israel be like the sand by the sea, only a remnant will return." Paul quotes this in Romans 9:27–28. The remnant theology: salvation is not for all Israel but for the faithful core.

10:24–25

"Do not be afraid of the Assyrians. In a very short time my anger will end and my wrath will be directed to their destruction." The assurance: God’s anger at Judah is temporary; his anger at Assyria is terminal.

10:33–34

"The LORD will lop off the boughs. The lofty trees will be felled. Lebanon will fall before the Mighty One." The Assyrian forest is cut down. This sets up ch.11 perfectly: from the felled forest, a shoot grows.
