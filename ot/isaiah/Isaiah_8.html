Isaiah 8

Maher-Shalal-Hash-Baz: Swift Is the Plunder

Verses 1–10 — “God Is with Us”
12345678910

Hebrew Word Study

מַהֵר שָׁלָל חָשׁ בַּז mahēr shālāl ḥāsh baz — swift is the plunder, speedy is the prey: "Name him Maher-Shalal-Hash-Baz. For before the boy knows how to say “My father” or “My mother,” the wealth of Damascus and the plunder of Samaria will be carried off by the king of Assyria" (vv.3–4). Isaiah’s second sign-child (after Shear-Jashub). The longest personal name in the Bible — a walking prophecy. The people have rejected the gently flowing waters of Shiloah (God’s quiet provision, v.6), so the LORD brings the mighty floodwaters of the Euphrates — Assyria (v.7). The flood will overflow and sweep into Judah, reaching up to the neck (v.8). But the chapter’s closing defiance: “Raise the war cry, you nations, and be shattered! God is with us (ʿimmānū ʾēl)!” (vv.9–10).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
8:1–4

The longest name in the Bible: Maher-Shalal-Hash-Baz. Before the boy can say “daddy,” Assyria will plunder Syria and Samaria. The child IS the message.

8:6–8

"Because this people has rejected the gently flowing waters of Shiloah, the Lord is about to bring against them the mighty floodwaters of the Euphrates." Quiet stream rejected; raging river received. You didn’t want gentle provision? You’ll get violent invasion.

8:10

"Devise your strategy, but it will be thwarted. Propose your plan, but it will not stand, for God is with us — Immanuel!" The Immanuel promise of ch.7 becomes a battle cry in ch.8.

Verses 11–22 — “To the Law and to the Testimony”
111213141516171819202122

Hebrew Word Study

תּוֹרָה tōrâ — instruction / law / Torah: "To the law (tōrâ) and to the testimony! If they do not speak according to this word, they have no light of dawn" (v.20). God warns Isaiah not to follow the people’s panic (v.11). The LORD himself will be a sanctuary — but for both houses of Israel he will be a stone of stumbling and a rock of offence (v.14). Paul quotes this in Romans 9:33 and 1 Peter 2:8 for Christ as the stumbling-stone. Isaiah binds up the testimony, seals the instruction among his disciples, and waits (v.16). "I will wait for the LORD, who is hiding his face from the house of Jacob. I will put my trust in him" (v.17). When they say “consult the spirits,” Isaiah replies: to the law and the testimony (v.20). Those who reject God’s word will wander in distress and darkness (vv.21–22).

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
8:13–14

"The LORD Almighty — regard him as holy. He will be a holy place; but for both houses of Israel he will be a stone that causes people to stumble." God as both sanctuary AND stumbling-stone. The same God is refuge for the faithful and ruin for the faithless. Paul applies this to Christ (Rom 9:33).

8:16–17

"Bind up this testimony of warning and seal up God’s instruction among my disciples. I will wait for the LORD." Isaiah creates a community of disciples who preserve the word during the dark period. Waiting for a God who hides his face.

8:19–20

"When someone tells you to consult the spirits of the dead, should not a people inquire of their God? To the law and to the testimony!" The alternative to necromancy: Scripture. The Reformation’s watchword traces back to this verse.
