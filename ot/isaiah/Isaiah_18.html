Isaiah 18

Oracle Against Cush

Verses 1–3 — “The Land of Whirring Wings”
123

Hebrew Word Study

צִלְצַל ṣilṣal — whirring / buzzing: "Woe to the land of whirring wings (ṣilṣal) along the rivers of Cush, which sends envoys by sea" (vv.1–2). Cush (Ethiopia/Nubia) — a tall and smooth-skinned people, feared far and wide (v.2). God sends a message: go back to your people (v.2). Then God speaks to the whole world: "All you people of the world, when a banner is raised on the mountains, you will see it; when a trumpet sounds, you will hear it" (v.3). The banner and trumpet: God’s signal to all nations.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
18:1–2

"Woe to the land of whirring wings beyond the rivers of Cush, which sends envoys by sea." Cush’s diplomatic envoys in papyrus boats. The reach of ancient diplomacy.

18:3

"When a banner is raised, you will see it; when a trumpet sounds, you will hear it." God addresses the entire world. The Cush oracle becomes a global announcement.

Verses 4–7 — “Gifts Will Be Brought to Mount Zion”
4567

Hebrew Word Study

שַי shay — gift / tribute: "At that time gifts (shay) will be brought to the LORD Almighty from a people tall and smooth-skinned, from a people feared far and wide — to Mount Zion, the place of the Name of the LORD Almighty" (v.7). God watches quietly, like shimmering heat and a cloud of dew in the harvest (v.4). Before the harvest, God prunes (v.5). The pruned branches are left for mountain birds and wild animals (v.6). But then: Cush brings gifts to Zion. The enemy-nation becomes a worshipping nation. The pattern of Isaiah’s oracles: judgment first, then surprising inclusion.

MacArthur Study Notes

MacArthur Study Bible — Faithful Paraphrase
18:4

"The LORD said to me, ‘I will remain quiet and will look on from my dwelling place, like shimmering heat in the sunshine.’" God’s quietness is not absence. He watches like heat-shimmer — invisible but intensely present.

18:7

"Gifts will be brought to the LORD from Cush — to Mount Zion." The oracle’s surprise ending: the feared nation brings tribute to God. Acts 8:26–40 (the Ethiopian eunuch) can be read as partial fulfilment.
