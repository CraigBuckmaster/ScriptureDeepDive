1 Chronicles 26

Gatekeepers and Treasurers

Verses 1–19 — Divisions of Gatekeepers
12345678910111213141516171819

Context

The gatekeepers are organised by family and assigned to specific gates: north, south, east, west, and the storehouse. Obed-Edom (whose house was blessed when the ark stayed there, 13:14) serves among the gatekeepers with 62 descendants — the blessing of the ark continues generationally. The gatekeepers cast lots for their posts, “each family alike.” Their role is practical (security) and theological (controlling access to holy space).

Cross-Reference

  • 1 Chr 13:14Obed-Edom’s household was blessed during the three months the ark stayed with him. Now his descendants serve as gatekeepers — permanently near the ark.

Calvin’s Commentary

John Calvin, Commentaries — Faithful Paraphrase
26:5

Obed-Edom’s blessing teaches that hospitality to God’s presence produces generational fruitfulness. Those who make room for God in their homes create a legacy of service.

NET Bible Notes

NET Bible Full Notes Edition — Biblical Studies Press
26:15

The “storehouse” (ʾasūppîm) housed both sacred vessels and provisions. Guarding the storehouse was a position of significant trust and responsibility.

Verses 20–32 — Treasury Officials and External Administrators
20212223242526272829303132

Context

Levites manage both the temple treasury (sacred donations, dedicated gifts) and external administration (officers and judges throughout Israel). The Chronicler maps the Levitical presence across the entire kingdom: they serve not only in the temple but as administrators in every region. The chapter closes with a note about officials west of the Jordan and east — Levitical governance extends to the full territorial reach of “all Israel.”

Cross-Reference

  • 2 Chr 19:8–11Jehoshaphat appoints Levites as judges throughout Judah. The administrative role described here persists into the monarchy.

Calvin’s Commentary

John Calvin, Commentaries — Faithful Paraphrase
26:29

Levites as external administrators teach that knowledge of God’s law should shape public governance. Justice in society flows from the same source as worship in the temple.

NET Bible Notes

NET Bible Full Notes Edition — Biblical Studies Press
26:29

The phrase “for outside duties” (lammĕlāʾkāh hahîṣōnāh) distinguishes temple-internal from territory-wide Levitical service. The Levites’ jurisdiction extended beyond the sanctuary walls.
