1 Chronicles 24

Priestly Divisions — the 24 Courses

Verses 1–19 — The 24 Priestly Divisions
12345678910111213141516171819

Context

Aaron’s descendants are divided into 24 courses for rotational temple service. Sixteen from Eleazar, eight from Ithamar — proportional to family size. The eighth division is Abijah (v.10) — the division of Zechariah, father of John the Baptist (Luke 1:5). Lots ensure divine selection. The 24-course system persists through the Second Temple and into the NT.

Cross-Reference

  • Luke 1:5Zechariah belonged to the priestly division of Abijah — the eighth course, established here, still functioning 500 years later.
  • Neh 12:1–26Nehemiah lists priestly divisions in the post-exilic period, confirming continuity.

Verses 20–31 — Remaining Levitical Families Assigned by Lot
202122232425262728293031

Context

The remaining non-priestly Levitical families are assigned by lot, mirroring the priestly process. “They cast lots just as their relatives the descendants of Aaron did.” The eldest brother is treated identically to the youngest — the lot levels hierarchies. The system is designed to be egalitarian within each functional category.

Cross-Reference

  • 1 Chr 23:7–23The Levitical families listed here expand chapter 23’s overview.

Calvin’s Commentary

John Calvin, Commentaries — Faithful Paraphrase

Equal treatment teaches that God’s service is not distributed by human merit or seniority.
