1 Chronicles 16

David’s Psalm of Thanksgiving

Verses 1–7 — The Ark Placed; Offerings and Blessings
1234567

Context

The ark is set inside the tent David pitched for it. Burnt offerings and fellowship offerings are made. David blesses the people in the name of the LORD and distributes food to “each Israelite man and woman.” Then David assigns Asaph and his associates to minister regularly before the ark “according to each day’s requirements.” Worship is not a one-time event but an ongoing institution.

Cross-Reference

  • 2 Sam 6:17–19The parallel in Samuel is brief; the Chronicler expands the liturgical details.

Calvin’s Commentary

John Calvin, Commentaries — Faithful Paraphrase
16:4

Daily worship teaches that God deserves not occasional attention but constant devotion. The assignment of regular ministers ensures that praise does not depend on the king’s mood but on institutional commitment.

16:4

The three verbs — lĕhazkir (to invoke/remember), lĕhōdōt (to give thanks), lĕhallēl (to praise) — define the three modes of Levitical worship ministry.

Verses 8–43 — The Psalm; Regular Worship at Gibeon and Jerusalem
8910111213141516171819202122232425262728293031323334353637383940414243

Hebrew Word Study

darash darash — to seek: The psalm includes the command: “Look to the LORD and his strength; seek his face always” (v.11). The verb darash (“seek”) is the Chronicler’s central theological term, and here it appears in David’s own psalm. The king commands what the Chronicler preaches: seek God.

Context

David’s psalm is a composite: vv.8–22 = Psalm 105:1–15; vv.23–33 = Psalm 96:1–13; vv.34–36 = Psalm 106:1, 47–48. The Chronicler attributes this liturgical anthology to David and places it at the founding moment of Jerusalemite worship. The psalm covers God’s covenant faithfulness, his universal kingship, and a plea for deliverance. After the psalm, the Chronicler notes the dual worship arrangement: Asaph’s group at the ark in Jerusalem; Zadok’s group at the tabernacle in Gibeon. Two sites, one worship.

Cross-Reference

  • Ps 105:1–15The first section of David’s psalm, celebrating God’s covenant and mighty deeds.
  • Ps 96:1–13The second section, calling all nations to worship the LORD.
  • Ps 106:1, 47–48The closing doxology: “Save us, LORD our God, and gather us from the nations.”

Calvin’s Commentary

John Calvin, Commentaries — Faithful Paraphrase
16:23

The call for “all the earth” to sing to the LORD teaches that God’s worship was never intended to be confined to Israel. The missionary impulse is embedded in David’s first psalm.

NET Bible Notes

NET Bible Full Notes Edition — Biblical Studies Press
16:35

The plea “gather us and deliver us from the nations” echoes Ps 106:47 but takes on new urgency in the Chronicler’s post-exilic context. The gathered community prays for further gathering.
