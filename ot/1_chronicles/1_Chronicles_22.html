1 Chronicles 22

David Prepares for the Temple

Verses 1–16 — Materials Stockpiled; David’s Charge to Solomon
12345678910111213141516

Hebrew Word Study

shālōm shalom — peace: Solomon’s name is derived from shālōm (v.9): “A son who will be born to you will be a man of rest. I will give him rest from all his enemies. His name will be Solomon, and I will grant Israel peace and quiet during his reign.” The temple requires peace — David the warrior cannot build it; Solomon the man of peace will.

Context

David amasses enormous resources: iron for nails, bronze beyond weighing, cedar logs without number, 100,000 talents of gold and 1,000,000 talents of silver. Then he charges Solomon: “Now, my son, the LORD be with you... build the house of the LORD your God, as he told you.” David explains why he cannot build it himself: “You have shed much blood and have fought many wars. You are not to build a house for my Name because you have shed much blood.” The warrior prepares; the peacemaker builds.

Cross-Reference

  • 1 Kgs 5:3–5Solomon’s explanation to Hiram: “My father David could not build a temple... because of the wars... But now the LORD my God has given me rest.”
  • 1 Chr 28:3David repeats the reason: “You are not to build a house for my Name, because you are a warrior and have shed blood.”

Calvin’s Commentary

John Calvin, Commentaries — Faithful Paraphrase
22:8

David’s disqualification from temple-building teaches that some forms of service are reserved for specific callings. The warrior cannot build the sanctuary; the builder cannot fight the wars. Both are necessary; neither is sufficient alone.

NET Bible Notes

NET Bible Full Notes Edition — Biblical Studies Press
22:9

shĕlōmōh (Solomon) is explicitly derived from shālōm (peace) in a folk etymology. The name encodes the son’s vocation: he is the peace-king who will build the peace-temple.

Verses 17–19 — David Commands the Leaders to Help Solomon
171819

Context

David commands all the leaders of Israel to help Solomon: “Is not the LORD your God with you? And has he not granted you rest on every side?” The leaders are enlisted as partners in the project. David doesn’t leave Solomon alone with the burden; he builds a coalition of support. “Now devote your heart and soul to seeking the LORD your God.” The verb darash (“seek”) appears one final time in David’s mouth: seeking God is the prerequisite for building God’s house.

Cross-Reference

  • 1 Chr 28:1–10David’s later public charge to the leaders expands on this private commissioning.

Calvin’s Commentary

John Calvin, Commentaries — Faithful Paraphrase
22:19

“Devote your heart and soul” before “build the sanctuary” teaches that the inner temple must be built before the outer one. God does not dwell in buildings made by uncommitted hands.

NET Bible Notes

NET Bible Full Notes Edition — Biblical Studies Press
22:19

The imperative sequence — darash (seek) then banah (build) — establishes a theological priority: devotion before construction. The syntax encodes the Chronicler’s theology.
