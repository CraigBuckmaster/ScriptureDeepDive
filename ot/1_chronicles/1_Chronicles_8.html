1 Chronicles 8

Benjamin’s Genealogy — Saul’s Line

Verses 1–28 — Benjamin’s Clans and Settlements
12345678910111213141516171819202122232425262728

Context

Benjamin receives a second, fuller genealogy (expanding the brief entry in 7:6–12). The detail reflects Benjamin’s importance to the post-exilic community: Jerusalem was in Benjamin’s territory, and many returnees were Benjaminites. The genealogy lists clans, heads of families, and settlements — including Aijalon and Gath, indicating territorial claims that extended into the Shephelah.

Cross-Reference

  • Josh 18:11–28Benjamin’s tribal allotment, which included Jerusalem and key towns along the Judah-Benjamin border.

Calvin’s Commentary

John Calvin, Commentaries — Faithful Paraphrase
8:28

That these families “lived in Jerusalem” connects the ancient genealogy to the post-exilic present. The returned community can trace their residence in the holy city back through centuries.

NET Bible Notes

NET Bible Full Notes Edition — Biblical Studies Press
8:28

The phrase “according to their genealogies” (lĕtōlĕdōtām) indicates that the Chronicler is working from written genealogical records, not oral tradition.

Verses 29–40 — The Gibeon Genealogy Leading to Saul
293031323334353637383940

Context

The genealogy narrows to the family of Gibeon, tracing from Jeiel (the “father of Gibeon”) through Ner to Kish to Saul. This is the bridge between the genealogies (ch 1–9) and the narrative (ch 10): the last name in the list is the first character in the story. Saul’s family tree is repeated almost identically in 9:35–44 — the Chronicler frames the narrative with the genealogy, ensuring the reader understands Saul’s identity before witnessing his death.

Cross-Reference

  • 1 Sam 9:1–2Saul’s introduction in Samuel provides the same ancestry: Kish son of Abiel, a Benjaminite. The Chronicler’s version adds generational depth.
  • 1 Chr 9:35–44The identical genealogy is repeated at the end of chapter 9, creating an inclusio that frames the transition from genealogy to narrative.

Calvin’s Commentary

John Calvin, Commentaries — Faithful Paraphrase
8:33

Saul’s genealogy before his death narrative teaches that God’s purposes work through families, not just individuals. Saul fails, but Jonathan’s line continues. God’s mercy extends beyond the judgment on one person.

NET Bible Notes

NET Bible Full Notes Edition — Biblical Studies Press
8:33

Esh-Baal (“man of Baal”) corresponds to Ish-Bosheth (“man of shame”) in 2 Sam 2:8. The substitution of bōshet for baʿal reflects post-exilic scribal sensitivity to Baal names.
